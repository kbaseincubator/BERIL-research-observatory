# ADP1 condition-dependent essentiality should be modeled as a continuous phenotype landscape with quinate degradation as a discrete exception.

## Introduction

This claim is the deletion-phenotype interpretation of ADP1 carbon fitness. It says the landscape is mostly continuous, with quinate degradation as the discrete exception that should be handled explicitly [stmt:adp1-deletion-continuum-claim; adp1_deletion_phenotypes].

## Synthesis

The claim is supported by tiering and independence. Eight carbon sources separate into demanding, moderate, and robust growth-defect tiers, while carbon-source assays provide multiple independent phenotypic dimensions rather than one shared axis [stmt:adp1-deletion-carbon-tier-finding; adp1_deletion_phenotypes] [stmt:adp1-deletion-condition-independence-finding; adp1_deletion_phenotypes].

Quinate gives the claim its boundary condition. The quinate degradation module is the main discrete exception to the otherwise continuous carbon-fitness landscape, so synthesis pages should not force quinate into the same continuum used for the rest of the panel [stmt:adp1-deletion-quinate-module-finding; adp1_deletion_phenotypes].

The caveats come from model quality: gapfilled growth predictions and FBA class alone are not enough to settle measured growth behavior. The claim should therefore guide interpretation, but not replace follow-up phenotyping or model refinement [stmt:adp1-explorer-gapfilling-caveat; acinetobacter_adp1_explorer] [stmt:adp1-triple-fba-growth-caveat; adp1_triple_essentiality].
