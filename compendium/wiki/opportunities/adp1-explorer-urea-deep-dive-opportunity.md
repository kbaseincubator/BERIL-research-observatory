## Introduction

This opportunity proposes a focused analysis of urea-specific fitness genes and their pangenome conservation. It follows from explorer evidence that urea behaves as an outlier condition in ADP1 mutant fitness [stmt:adp1-explorer-urea-deep-dive-opportunity; acinetobacter_adp1_explorer] [stmt:adp1-explorer-condition-fitness-finding; acinetobacter_adp1_explorer].

## Synthesis

The urea follow-up fits the deletion project's broader interpretation that ADP1 condition-dependent essentiality is a continuous landscape with named exceptions. Urea is not the same exception as quinate, but it is another condition-specific signal that deserves module-level analysis [stmt:adp1-deletion-continuum-claim; adp1_deletion_phenotypes] [stmt:adp1-explorer-condition-fitness-finding; acinetobacter_adp1_explorer].

The analysis should use the explorer's bridge role and the triple-essentiality measurement rule. Pangenome conservation can connect the module to BERDL-style integration, while continuous fitness and orthogonal evidence should guide interpretation instead of binary thresholds alone [stmt:adp1-explorer-database-bridge-claim; acinetobacter_adp1_explorer] [stmt:adp1-triple-continuous-fitness-claim; adp1_triple_essentiality].
