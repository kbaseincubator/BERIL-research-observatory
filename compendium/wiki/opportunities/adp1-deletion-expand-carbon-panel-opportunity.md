# Expanding ADP1 deletion phenotyping beyond eight carbon sources could test whether the observed independent dimensions increase with condition coverage.

## Introduction

This opportunity asks whether the deletion-phenotype result is stable when ADP1 is tested across more than eight carbon sources. It follows from the observation that the current conditions already show independent phenotypic dimensions [stmt:adp1-deletion-expand-carbon-panel-opportunity; adp1_deletion_phenotypes] [stmt:adp1-deletion-condition-independence-finding; adp1_deletion_phenotypes].

## Synthesis

The opportunity matters because the deletion project models condition-dependent essentiality as a mostly continuous landscape with quinate degradation as a discrete exception. A larger panel would test whether more conditions add dimensions to that landscape or mostly refine the same structure [stmt:adp1-deletion-continuum-claim; adp1_deletion_phenotypes] [stmt:adp1-deletion-quinate-module-finding; adp1_deletion_phenotypes].

The follow-up should preserve quantitative interpretation. The triple-essentiality claim says continuous fitness and orthogonal evidence should be prioritized over binary thresholds alone, while the explorer bridge can help connect the expanded phenotyping to broader ADP1 data products [stmt:adp1-triple-continuous-fitness-claim; adp1_triple_essentiality] [stmt:adp1-explorer-database-bridge-claim; acinetobacter_adp1_explorer].
