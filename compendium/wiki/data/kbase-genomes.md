# Kbase Genomes

## Overview

KBase Genomes is a curated collection of bacterial genome sequences and annotations hosted on the KBase platform, providing a shared reference substrate for comparative genomics at the scale of the full bacterial domain. In this wiki it appears as the common data backbone linking two otherwise distinct projects: one that builds a genome-wide atlas of horizontal gene-transfer (HGT) innovation across 18,989 GTDB r214 species representatives, and one that designs ecotype-stratified phage cocktails for Crohn's disease patients. The page exists because both projects drew on the same underlying genome resources and their analytical choices — from phylogenetic tree construction to biosynthetic gene-cluster annotation — trace back to this shared substrate.

The HGT innovation atlas is built on 18,989 quality-filtered species representatives from the Genome Taxonomy Database (GTDB r214), which provides a phylogenetically coherent, dereplicated view of bacterial diversity. Across this tree, the atlas accumulates 13.7 million producer/participation scores and 17 million Sankoff parsimony gain events (Sankoff parsimony is a tree-aware method that attributes gene-family gains to specific branches, allowing distinction between recent and ancient acquisitions) [\[1\]](#references). The ratio of recent-to-ancient gain events turns out to be a function-class signature in its own right: CRISPR-Cas systems show a 24.5× recent-skew consistent with active horizontal exchange, while strict housekeeping genes cluster near a 1× ratio consistent with vertical inheritance [\[2\]](#references).

The atlas organizes findings through a "Producer × Participation" framework — a direction-agnostic classification of each clade as Innovator-Isolated, Innovator-Exchange, Sink-Broker-Exchange, or Stable — which is necessary at deep phylogenetic ranks where full duplication-transfer-loss (DTL) reconciliation is computationally intractable [\[3\]](#references). A key novel finding is that architectural promiscuity (the number of distinct Pfam domain architectures a gene family can adopt) correlates with HGT propensity: mixed-category KOs (KEGG Orthology groups) carry a median of 46 Pfam architectures per KO versus just 1 for photosystem II (PSII) components, mirroring the "modular systems exchange more" pattern described by Denise 2019 [\[4\]](#references).

Atlas claims are made robust by convergence across three independent measurement substrates: tree-aware Sankoff parsimony effect sizes, sample-to-biome ecology metadata (from MGnify and ENVO), and per-strain phenotype tables from BacDive. A finding supported by any single layer alone would be circumstantial; all three converging on the same biology make the atlas claims credible [\[5\]](#references). This convergence is confirmed by biome-enrichment tests: Mycobacteriaceae genomes are 7.88× enriched in host-pathogen environments (p < 10⁻⁴⁵), Cyanobacteriia 2.77× in photic aquatic zones (p < 10⁻⁵²), and Bacteroidota 1.40× in gut/rumen (p < 10⁻³⁵) [\[6\]](#references).

Two pre-registered case studies illustrate the framework at work. Mycobacteriaceae are confirmed as Innovator-Isolated on the mycolic-acid pathway at family and order ranks (producer Cohen's d = +0.31, consumer d = −0.19), indicating high paralog expansion with low cross-clade exchange [\[7\]](#references). A finer-grained analysis shows that this signal is concentrated in host-pathogen-niche mycobacteria rather than soil mycobacteria — a useful refinement of the family-level finding [\[8\]](#references). Cyanobacteria are confirmed as Innovator-Exchange on PSII at the class rank, which is biologically appropriate because PSII evolved before Cyanobacteria diversified (producer d = +1.50, consumer d = +0.70); at genus rank the signal disappears as expected [\[9\]](#references).

From the IBD phage-targeting perspective, KBase genome resources underpin genomic characterization of Crohn's disease pathobionts. Tier-A pathobiont genomes are strongly enriched for iron-siderophore biosynthetic gene clusters (BGCs) — iron acquisition emerges as a pathobiont-defining genomic capability rather than merely a cohort-level pathway signal [\[10\]](#references). The specificity is striking: among the six actionable Tier-A pathobionts, only *E. coli* (the adherent-invasive *E. coli*, AIEC, subset) carries the iron-siderophore and genotoxin BGC signature, localizing this biology to one species [\[11\]](#references). The phage side of cocktail design also rests on genome sequences: a greedy minimum-set-cover algorithm over experimentally tested phage–strain susceptibility matrices yields a five-phage cocktail covering 94.7% of 188 *E. coli* strains in the PhageFoundry collection [\[12\]](#references).

Across both projects, a consistent message emerges: population-level genome databases provide robust background distributions against which clade-specific signals can be evaluated, but they simultaneously expose the limits of pooled approaches. The Alm 2006 correlation of r ≈ 0.74 between histidine-kinase count and lineage-specific expansion, for instance, is not reproduced at full GTDB scale; only modest correlations (r = 0.10–0.29) are recovered across 18,989 genomes [\[13\]](#references). The methodology's qualitative generalizations hold, but quantitative point estimates from 207 genomes do not survive scale-up. Similarly, phage cocktails designed at cohort level will systematically mismatch individual IBD patients unless each patient's microbiome ecotype is determined first [\[14\]](#references).

Caveats and open directions are worth noting. The PSII Innovator verdict is strongly rank-dependent: the clade is Stable at genus rank, Innovator-Exchange at class rank, and Innovator-Isolated at phylum rank (a substrate artifact from insufficient reference consumer data at phylum), so the class rank must be chosen on biological grounds rather than inferred from any single resolution [\[15\]](#references). Composition-based donor inference — identifying which clade donated a gene during HGT — remains a future direction, blocked because per-CDS sequence data is not in the BERDL queryable schema [\[16\]](#references). The mechanistic question of whether high-exchange KOs are mobile-element-mediated awaits phage/plasmid/integron context flagging per gain event [\[17\]](#references).

On the phage-cocktail side, the three preregistered KO sets (PSII, mycolic-acid, polysaccharide utilization loci) all show near-zero rates of MGE-machinery association against a 1.37% atlas baseline, confirming that these gene families are not themselves phage, transposase, integrase, or plasmid genes [\[18\]](#references). The two highest-priority Crohn's pathobiont targets, *H. hathewayi* and *M. gnavus*, have the weakest phage availability, creating a coverage gap for the most actionable species [\[19\]](#references). This gap means that no E1 Crohn's patient can be treated with a pure phage cocktail; only a hybrid combining phages with non-phage alternatives for the gap and temperate-only targets is feasible [\[20\]](#references). Querying external databases such as INPHARED and IMG/VR for gut-anaerobe phages is the highest-priority follow-up to close these gaps [\[21\]](#references).

## Projects Using This Collection

### gene_function_ecological_agora

This project uses the KBase/GTDB genome collection to build a bacterial-domain HGT and functional innovation atlas. It processes 18,989 quality-filtered species representatives through a Sankoff parsimony pipeline to attribute 17 million gene-gain events to recipient ranks across the tree. The project's core output — the Producer × Participation framework and the acquisition-depth (recent/ancient ratio) signal — depends entirely on having a phylogenetically coherent, dereplicated genome set at full bacterial diversity. Among the project's four reusable methodology contributions are the per-rank null model framework, the M22 Sankoff recipient-rank attribution, annotation-density residualization, and tree-based parsimony donor inference [\[3\]](#references).

The IBD ecotype framework developed in the companion project connects here because microbiome ecotypes are shaped by the same [mobile genetic elements](../topics/mobile-genetic-elements.md) and [pangenome architecture](../topics/pangenome-architecture.md) dynamics that the innovation atlas quantifies at full bacterial scale.

### ibd_phage_targeting

This project uses genome data to characterize Crohn's disease pathobionts and design ecotype-stratified phage cocktails. The ecotype framework — four reproducible IBD microbiome states (E0 through E3) trained on 8,489 MetaPhlAn3 samples using LDA and GMM clustering with cross-method ARI as the selection criterion — stratifies patients before any cocktail is designed [\[22\]](#references). Projecting the 23 UC Davis Crohn's patients onto this framework reveals that they span three ecotypes (E0, E1, E3) with none in the Prevotella-dominant E2, and that active disease concentrates in E1 and E3 [\[23\]](#references).

A key design challenge is that clinical covariates (disease severity scores, medication class) can separate healthy from IBD samples but cannot distinguish the transitional E1 from severe E3 ecotypes, making metagenomics a continuing requirement for per-patient ecotype assignment [\[24\]](#references). A confound-free within-substudy meta-analysis across four IBD cohorts recovers the canonical Crohn's signature — pathobionts up, protective commensals down — validating the target list before ecotype stratification is applied [\[25\]](#references). Pooled public cohort data introduce a structural identifiability problem: healthy and Crohn's samples come from disjoint source studies in curatedMetagenomicData, making a pooled case-vs-control mixed model unidentifiable and forcing a within-substudy CD-vs-nonIBD design [\[26\]](#references). The E3-ecotype Tier-A target list rests on single-study evidence and should be treated as provisional until a cohort with sufficient E3 patients and both diagnosis groups is available [\[27\]](#references).

Longitudinal data from a single patient whose ecotype drifts between E1 and E3 across visits demonstrates that the optimal cocktail composition changes moderately between visits (cocktail Jaccard = 0.60), motivating state-dependent cocktail redesign rather than a fixed prescription [\[28\]](#references). The Microviridae member *Gokushovirus WZ-2015a* is robustly depleted in Crohn's disease across multiple ecotypes, independently rediscovering a pre-existing reference signal [\[29\]](#references). A reusable output of this project is the taxonomy synonymy layer — an NCBI taxid plus GTDB-version-aware rename table — which is needed by any multi-cohort microbiome project to avoid log₂FC artifacts from format divergence between cohorts [\[30\]](#references).
