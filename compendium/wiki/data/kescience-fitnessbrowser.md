# Fitness Browser (kescience_fitnessbrowser)

## Overview

The **Fitness Browser** (`kescience_fitnessbrowser`) is a shared BERDL data
collection of genome-wide bacterial gene-fitness measurements generated by
**RB-TnSeq** — randomly barcoded transposon sequencing, in which a pooled
library of mutants, each carrying a transposon insertion tagged by a unique DNA
barcode, is grown competitively across many defined conditions. Counting the
barcodes before and after growth yields a quantitative *fitness* value for every
non-essential gene in every condition: how much knocking out that gene helps or
hurts the organism. Genes whose disruption is lethal never appear in the pooled
data and are scored separately as essential. The collection spans roughly four
to five dozen lab-tractable bacteria — model organisms and environmental
isolates such as *Pseudomonas*, *Shewanella*, *Escherichia coli* Keio,
*Desulfovibrio vulgaris* Hildenborough, and many others — each assayed across
carbon and nitrogen sources, metal and osmotic stresses, antibiotics, and other
perturbations.

This wiki page exists because the Fitness Browser is the single most reused
experimental resource across the compendium: it is the empirical backbone that
turns gene *presence* (what a genome encodes) into gene *function* (what a gene
actually does for the cell). Twenty-nine separate projects in this collection
draw on the same fitness matrices, and the page exists to make that shared
dependency explicit — to show which projects lean on the same data, what the
data can and cannot support, and how fitness measurements connect to the
pangenome, metabolic-model, and literature collections elsewhere in BERDL. A
central, recurring use is to ask whether [gene fitness](../topics/gene-fitness.md)
predicts conservation: across projects, genes that matter for fitness in *any*
tested condition tend to be the genes retained in the core genome, anchoring the
idea that the core genome reflects general functional importance.

The collection's defining strength is also its defining limit. Because every
gene carries a measured phenotype rather than an inferred one, the Fitness
Browser is uniquely able to validate or overturn database annotations — for
example, RB-TnSeq data directly contradict a UniProt fructose-transporter
annotation and show that the ManYZ transporter is in fact mannose/glucosamine
specific. But the organism panel is small and biased: roughly three-quarters of
the organisms are Pseudomonadota, *Actinobacteria* are absent and *Firmicutes*
critically underrepresented, and all strains are lab-adapted. Several projects
flag that this composition limits how far conclusions generalize, and at least
one hypothesis (the fitness cost of co-mobilized AMR genes) simply could not be
tested because the few dozen Fitness Browser organisms do not overlap the
pangenome species being analyzed.

## Projects Using This Collection

The 29 projects fall into a handful of analytical families that reuse the same
fitness matrices for different ends.

**Pangenome architecture and conservation.** A large cluster asks how the
fitness of a gene relates to its place in the [pangenome](../topics/pangenome-architecture.md)
— the full gene repertoire of a species, partitioned into a near-universal
*core* and a variable *accessory* genome whose *openness* measures how readily
new genes are gained. The `costly_dispensable_genes` project uses fitness to
identify genes that impose measurable cost yet are not conserved, interpreting
them as the genomic debris of horizontal gene transfer — insertion sequences,
prophage remnants, and defense systems that are poorly annotated,
taxonomically restricted, and shorter than conserved genes.
`metabolic_capability_dependency` and `pathway_capability_dependency` combine
fitness with **GapMind** (a tool that scores whether a genome encodes a complete
metabolic pathway) to find *latent capabilities* — pathways a genome encodes but
that show no fitness importance under tested conditions — and show that the
fraction of such latent pathways rises with pangenome openness, a quantitative
form of the Black Queen hypothesis. `field_vs_lab_fitness` stratifies one
organism's 757 experiments into field-relevant versus lab conditions and finds,
perhaps surprisingly, that fitness *magnitude* predicts conservation better than
whether a condition is ecologically realistic.

**The antibiotic resistome.** Four projects treat the Fitness Browser as a way
to measure the real cost of [antimicrobial resistance](../topics/amr-resistome.md).
`amr_fitness_cost` finds that AMR-gene knockouts have systematically *higher*
fitness than the baseline under non-antibiotic conditions — all 25 organisms
show the same positive shift — and that core (intrinsic) and accessory
(acquired) AMR genes have essentially identical fitness distributions, refuting
the textbook prediction that recently acquired resistance is costlier.
`amr_cofitness_networks` builds the first pan-bacterial map of AMR co-fitness
neighborhoods across 28 organisms, while `amr_pangenome_atlas` and
`prophage_amr_comobilization` scale the question up to hundreds of thousands of
AMR gene clusters across the GTDB pangenome, using fitness data where it
overlaps.

**Functional dark matter and literature gaps.** `functional_dark_matter` and
`truly_dark_genes` use measurable phenotypes to make the [unannotated genome](../topics/functional-dark-matter.md)
*actionable*: of ~57,011 unannotated genes across 48 organisms, ~17,344 have
strong fitness effects or essentiality, defining the bacterial dark matter worth
characterizing first. `paperblast_explorer` complements this from the literature
side, linking fitness phenotypes to ~130,000 VIMSS cross-references to surface
genes that are functionally important but understudied. `snipe_defense_system`
shows the payoff: a formerly dark domain (DUF4041) was characterized and
renamed by InterPro to the SNIPE-associated antiphage domain.

**Metabolism, respiration, and ecology.** `aromatic_catabolism_network`,
`respiratory_chain_wiring`, `pseudomonas_carbon_ecology`, and
`acinetobacter_adp1_explorer` use condition-specific fitness to reconstruct how
[metabolic pathways](../topics/metabolic-pathways.md) and respiratory chains are
wired — for instance showing that *Acinetobacter baylyi* ADP1 uses qualitatively
different respiratory configurations for each carbon source. `fitness_modules`
applies independent component analysis to the fitness matrices to extract latent
co-regulated modules, and `metal_fitness_atlas`, `metal_cross_resistance`, and
`counter_ion_effects` use the metal-stress conditions to reframe
[metal tolerance](../topics/metal-resistance.md) as a core-genome robustness
problem rather than an accessory-resistance one. Several projects
(`fw300_metabolic_consistency`, `webofmicrobes_explorer`,
`genotype_to_phenotype_enigma`) bridge fitness to exometabolomics and field
isolate data, often hitting the same wall: linking organisms and conditions
across datasets by string-matched names is error-prone, and ID-based
canonicalization is needed to expand the matches.

## Connections

The Fitness Browser is most valuable when joined to other BERDL collections, and
the projects here map out those joins explicitly.

The deepest connection is to [pangenome architecture](../topics/pangenome-architecture.md):
fitness supplies the phenotype, the pangenome supplies the gene-content context,
and almost every conservation argument in this collection lives at their
intersection. Projects repeatedly propose scaling fitness-derived rules from the
few dozen Fitness Browser organisms to the full ~293K-genome BERDL pangenome —
for example predicting AMR cost or respiratory-compensation patterns from
gene-cluster conservation across tens of thousands of species, where statistical
power is far higher.

The collection also bridges to [metabolic pathways](../topics/metabolic-pathways.md)
through FBA (flux balance analysis) and GapMind. In the ADP1 explorer, FBA flux
predictions and TnSeq essentiality calls agree for 73.8% of genes measured by
both, with the discordant quarter flagged as candidates for model refinement —
a concrete example of fitness data correcting metabolic models. Pathways that
GapMind scores incomplete yet fitness marks important point directly at
annotation gaps or salvage routes the tool misses.

Several connections run through [mobile genetic elements](../topics/mobile-genetic-elements.md)
and the [AMR resistome](../topics/amr-resistome.md): costly-dispensable genes,
prophage-AMR co-mobilization, and the SNIPE antiphage system all sit at the
junction of fitness cost and horizontally acquired DNA. The
[functional dark matter](../topics/functional-dark-matter.md) and literature
connections (via PaperBLAST's VIMSS links) close the loop from "measured but
unannotated" back to "annotated but unmeasured." Finer-grained topical threads
run to [metal resistance](../topics/metal-resistance.md),
[microbial ecotypes](../topics/microbial-ecotypes.md),
[environment biogeography](../topics/environment-biogeography.md),
[microbiome engineering](../topics/microbiome-engineering.md),
[subsurface genomics](../topics/subsurface-genomics.md), and the
[ADP1 model system](../topics/adp1-model-system.md), each of which uses fitness
data as one evidence stream among several.

Honest about the seams: the cross-collection links are real but partial. The
Web of Microbes integration recovers genuine connections to the Fitness Browser,
ModelSEED, and the pangenome but is offset by missing consumption data and a
small single-laboratory dataset. Linking the Fitness Browser to GapMind species
clades by NCBI taxonomy IDs returned zero matches because of a malformed taxid
column, forcing organism-level aggregates instead. And the BacDive-to-pangenome
join recovers only 38.4% of GTDB species by name matching. These are the
plumbing problems that determine how much of the Fitness Browser's measured
biology can actually be propagated across the lakehouse.
