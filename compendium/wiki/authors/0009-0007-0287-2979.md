# Beril Admin

This page summarizes the project and topic footprint of the contributor identified as Beril Admin (ORCID 0009-0007-0287-2979) within the BERIL research observatory. It exists in this wiki because this contributor's work on the *Acinetobacter baylyi* ADP1 data explorer spans multiple cross-cutting topics — gene essentiality, metabolic modeling, pangenome architecture, and functional annotation — that connect to many other pages in this knowledge graph.

## Overview

The central contribution is a multi-omics integration project for *Acinetobacter baylyi* ADP1, a naturally transformable soil bacterium widely used as a genetic model for Acinetobacter research. The project built and analyzed a SQLite database of 15 tables (461,522 rows, 135 MB) whose core `genome_features` table covers 5,852 genes with 51 annotation columns spanning six data modalities: TnSeq essentiality, flux balance analysis (FBA) metabolic flux predictions, mutant growth fitness across 8 carbon sources, proteomics across 7 engineered strains, pangenome classification, and functional annotations via COG, KEGG KO, Pfam, and UniRef [\[1\]](#references). This breadth makes it one of the more comprehensive single-organism multi-omics resources in the BERDL lakehouse.

A key finding about data coverage is that no single gene carries data across all six modalities simultaneously [\[2\]](#references). FBA flux data is the sparsest, covering only 15% of genes, which restricts model-experiment concordance analyses to 866 genes — an important caveat when interpreting cross-modality comparisons.

## Projects

The contributor's sole project in this corpus is **acinetobacter_adp1_explorer**, which investigated how well the ADP1 database integrates with the BERDL data lakehouse. Four of five connection types matched at over 90%, with 100% match rates for genome IDs, chemical compounds, and pangenome cluster IDs, and 91% for metabolic reactions [\[3\]](#references). The one gap is notable: ADP1 is absent from the Fitness Browser, making the condition-specific mutant growth fitness data in this project the only source of such data for this organism in BERDL [\[4\]](#references).

A non-trivial integration challenge was the pangenome cluster identifier mismatch: the ADP1 database uses mmseqs2-style cluster IDs while BERDL uses centroid gene IDs, with 0% direct string match. The bridge was discovered through BERDL's `gene_genecluster_junction` table, which maps BERDL cluster IDs to member gene IDs that in turn match the ADP1 `pan_genome_features` table. This approach achieved 100% mapping of all 4,891 BERDL clusters to 4,081 unique ADP1 clusters across 43,754 genes [\[5\]](#references).

On the metabolic modeling side, FBA — a computational method that predicts which reactions carry flux given a genome-scale stoichiometric model and growth constraints — agreed with TnSeq essentiality calls (experimental transposon insertion fitness scores) for 73.8% of the 866 genes with both measurements [\[6\]](#references). The 26% discordant genes are candidates for model refinement or may reflect regulatory effects not captured by the steady-state FBA framework. This concordance analysis is further nuanced by the finding that gene essentiality is media-dependent: 499 genes are essential on minimal media versus only 346 on LB rich medium, reflecting the additional biosynthetic burden that arises when precursors must be synthesized de novo [\[7\]](#references).

A major caveat for metabolic model predictions is the dependence on gapfilling — a computational procedure that adds reactions missing from a draft genome-scale model to make it capable of predicting growth. Of 121,519 growth phenotype predictions across 14 genomes, 87% require at least one gapfilled reaction, meaning that prediction accuracy is tightly coupled to gapfilling quality [\[8\]](#references). The 243 cataloged missing functions represent genuine gaps in genomic evidence. Core metabolism, however, is remarkably conserved: 1,248 of 1,330 unique metabolic reactions (94%) are shared across all 14 Acinetobacter genomes, with only 20 being genome-unique [\[9\]](#references).

The proteomics component measured protein abundance across 7 engineered ADP1 strains (wild-type and six aromatic amino acid pathway derivatives) for 2,383 genes. Cross-strain correlation is high, indicating that the engineered modifications have targeted rather than global effects on the proteome [\[10\]](#references).

## Topics

This contributor's work touches five major topics in the wiki:

**[Adp1 Model System](../topics/adp1-model-system.md)** is the primary experimental system. The database and analyses described here are directly about ADP1 biology, making this the most tightly coupled topic.

**[Gene Fitness](../topics/gene-fitness.md)** is central because mutant growth fitness across 8 carbon sources reveals condition-specific gene requirements. Urea catabolism, for example, involves a nearly independent set of genes relative to quinate (r = 0.11), while butanediol on acetate and lactate show the strongest cross-condition correlations (r = 0.58 and 0.53), consistent with shared central carbon metabolism [\[11\]](#references). Essential genes themselves are dramatically more annotation-rich than dispensable ones — 33% versus 5% carry COG assignments, and 92% versus 53% carry KEGG KO assignments — and are more likely to reside in the core pangenome [\[12\]](#references) [\[13\]](#references).

**[Functional Dark Matter](../topics/functional-dark-matter.md)** becomes relevant through the observation that roughly 8% of essential genes lack KEGG KO assignments. These annotation-dark essential genes are particularly interesting as candidates for novel essential functions, since their necessity is experimentally confirmed even though their biochemical role remains uncharacterized [\[14\]](#references).

**[Metabolic Pathways](../topics/metabolic-pathways.md)** is engaged through the FBA models and the reaction conservation analysis. The high conservation of core metabolism across 14 genomes, combined with the gapfilling caveat, sets the stage for understanding where metabolic models are reliable and where they require experimental validation.

**[Pangenome Architecture](../topics/pangenome-architecture.md)** connects because the project integrates ADP1 gene data with BERDL's pangenome of 3,207 core and 1,684 accessory gene clusters for *A. baylyi*, and because the correlation between essentiality and core-genome membership provides a genome-evolutionary interpretation of fitness data.

The data collections that underpin this work — [Kbase Ke Pangenome](../data/kbase-ke-pangenome.md), [Kbase Msd Biochemistry](../data/kbase-msd-biochemistry.md), [Kbase Uniref](../data/kbase-uniref.md), [Kescience Fitnessbrowser](../data/kescience-fitnessbrowser.md), and [Phagefoundry](../data/phagefoundry.md) — are all adjacent pages because the ADP1 explorer explicitly tested connectivity to each of these BERDL resources, with the Fitness Browser being the one that ADP1 is absent from.
