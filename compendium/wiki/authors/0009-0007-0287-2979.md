# Beril Admin

## Overview

Beril Admin contributes work centered on *Acinetobacter baylyi* ADP1, building and
characterizing a comprehensive multi-omics resource and connecting it to the wider
BERDL data ecosystem. The contributions assembled here come from a single project, the
ADP1 Data Explorer, which integrates six molecular and phenotype data modalities for
ADP1 and a set of related *Acinetobacter* genomes, then validates how that material
bridges into BERDL collections through shared genomes, reactions, compounds, and
pangenome clusters.

The throughline across this work is data integration with a critical eye toward model
quality and condition-specific biology. The ADP1 resource is shown to connect strongly
to BERDL, yet the same analyses surface where the underlying metabolic model leans
heavily on gapfilled reactions and where experimental fitness data reveals structure
that a model alone would miss. These findings feed three topic areas: integrating ADP1
data with BERDL, carbon-source fitness, and metabolic model quality.

## Projects

**ADP1 Data Explorer.** This project assembles a user-provided resource for
*Acinetobacter baylyi* ADP1 spanning six data modalities — TnSeq essentiality, FBA
metabolic flux, mutant growth fitness on eight carbon sources, proteomics across
engineered strains, pangenome classification, and functional annotations — for ADP1 and
thirteen related genomes. Beyond inventorying that material, the project tests how well
it integrates with BERDL. Querying BERDL via Spark confirms strong connectivity across
genomes, reactions, compounds, and pangenome clusters, establishing the resource as a
usable bridge for downstream ADP1 synthesis.

A central technical result is the pangenome cluster-ID bridge: because the ADP1 resource
and BERDL use entirely different cluster-naming conventions, a gene-junction mapping was
needed, and it achieves complete gene-level coverage between BERDL pangenome clusters and
ADP1-style cluster IDs. This mapping is what makes BERDL pangenome annotations joinable to
ADP1 genes.

The project also examines where the data and the model diverge. ADP1 mutant growth
fitness shows condition-specific structure, with urea and quinate behaving as outlier
conditions relative to the other carbon sources — a signal that motivates a dedicated
look at urea-specific genes and their pangenome conservation. On the model side, the
analysis flags a quality caveat: ADP1 model-based growth predictions depend heavily on
gapfilled reactions, so prediction accuracy is tightly coupled to gapfilling quality.
Genes where FBA and TnSeq disagree on essentiality are correspondingly identified as
priorities for metabolic-model refinement.

## Topics

This author's contributions sit across three connected topic areas:

- [Adp1 Data Integration](../topics/adp1-data-integration.md) — the BERDL connectivity
  finding, the database-bridge claim, the pangenome cluster-ID bridge, and the
  multi-omics inventory.
- [Adp1 Carbon Fitness](../topics/adp1-carbon-fitness.md) — the condition-specific
  fitness finding and the urea deep-dive opportunity.
- [Adp1 Model Quality](../topics/adp1-model-quality.md) — the gapfilling caveat and the
  FBA–TnSeq discordance opportunity.

The work draws on and links to several BERDL collections, including the
[KBase KE Pangenome](../data/kbase-ke-pangenome.md),
[KBase MSD Biochemistry](../data/kbase-msd-biochemistry.md),
[KBase UniRef](../data/kbase-uniref.md),
