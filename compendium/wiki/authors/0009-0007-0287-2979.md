# Beril Admin

This page is the contributor profile for Beril Admin (ORCID 0009-0007-0287-2979) within this knowledge-graph wiki. It exists to give a single place to see what this author worked on and which scientific themes their work touches. Their footprint in the compendium is focused: a single project, the *Acinetobacter baylyi* ADP1 explorer, which assembles and cross-validates a multi-omics view of one bacterium against the wider data lakehouse. The sections below summarize that project and the topics it connects to, drawing only on the statements recorded for this author.

## Overview

All of this author's recorded work sits in one project that builds and stress-tests a multi-omics resource for *Acinetobacter baylyi* ADP1, a metabolically versatile soil bacterium used as a chassis organism (see the [Adp1 Model System](../topics/adp1-model-system.md) topic). The central deliverable is a curated database that integrates a user-provided SQLite store of 15 tables (461,522 rows, ~135 MB) whose hub `genome_features` table holds 5,852 genes annotated across 51 columns spanning six data modalities. The recurring engineering theme is integration: tying this organism-specific database into BERDL, the BER Data Lakehouse, and showing that the two representations of the same biology actually line up.

That integration largely succeeded. Four of five connection types between the ADP1 database and BERDL collections matched at over 90% — including a perfect 100% on genome IDs and compound IDs and 91% on reactions — which demonstrates deep, queryable coupling with the lakehouse rather than a siloed dataset. A notable wrinkle is that pangenome cluster identifiers did not match by string at all (0% direct match), yet a gene junction table provided a complete bridge, mapping all 4,891 BERDL gene clusters onto 4,081 unique ADP1 mmseqs2-style clusters. That bridge is what makes the rest of the cross-modality analysis possible.

The work also surfaces honest limitations. No single gene in the database carries data across all six modalities at once, and flux-balance (FBA) flux coverage is sparse (only 15% of genes), which restricts model-versus-experiment concordance analysis to just 866 genes. Those caveats matter for anyone reusing the resource: the multi-omics promise is real but uneven across the genome.

## Projects

### Acinetobacter ADP1 explorer (`acinetobacter_adp1_explorer`)

This is the author's only project here, and it has two intertwined goals — assemble a usable multi-omics database for ADP1, and validate it against independent evidence and against the lakehouse.

**A unique fitness resource.** Because *A. baylyi* ADP1 is absent from the Fitness Browser (the standard repository for bacterial mutant-fitness data, see [Kescience Fitnessbrowser](../data/kescience-fitnessbrowser.md)), the condition-specific mutant growth data collected here is not available anywhere else in BERDL. That makes the project a genuine addition to the [Gene Fitness](../topics/gene-fitness.md) landscape rather than a duplication of existing measurements. Gene essentiality in ADP1 turns out to be media-dependent — 499 genes are essential on minimal media versus 346 on rich LB — which fits the expectation that minimal media imposes extra biosynthetic burden, forcing more pathways to be indispensable. Across eight carbon sources, mutant fitness profiles were only moderately correlated (mean pairwise r = 0.44), and urea catabolism stood out as nearly independent of quinate and the other conditions; this last point is medium-confidence and worth treating as a lead rather than a settled conclusion.

**Metabolic modeling and its concordance with experiment.** The project pairs a genome-scale metabolic model with the experimental fitness data. FBA flux predictions and TnSeq essentiality calls agreed for 73.8% of the 866 genes that had both measurements, and the discordant ~26% were explicitly flagged as candidates for model refinement or for regulatory effects the model does not capture — a constructive way to read disagreement. A serious caveat tempers the growth predictions: 87% of the 121,519 predicted growth phenotypes depend on at least one gapfilled reaction, so prediction accuracy is tightly coupled to the quality of gapfilling. Readers leaning on the [Metabolic Pathways](../topics/metabolic-pathways.md) outputs (drawing on biochemistry from [Kbase Msd Biochemistry](../data/kbase-msd-biochemistry.md)) should keep that dependence in mind.

**Pangenome context.** Comparing across 14 *Acinetobacter* genomes (the comparative-genomics backbone in [Kbase Ke Pangenome](../data/kbase-ke-pangenome.md)), core metabolism is highly conserved: 1,248 of 1,330 unique reactions (94%) are shared by all genomes and only 20 are genome-unique. Essential genes are also more likely to live in the core pangenome, consistent with the general pattern that conserved genes tend to be essential — though this particular claim is medium-confidence here. The cluster-ID bridge described above ties this pangenome view back to the ADP1 database. The project also touched proteomics: protein abundance measured across 7 engineered strains for 2,383 genes showed high cross-strain correlation, suggesting the strain engineering produced targeted rather than genome-wide proteome effects (a medium-confidence reading).

**Annotation and the unknown.** Essential genes are far more annotation-rich than dispensable ones — 33% versus 5% carry COG assignments and 92% versus 53% carry KEGG KO assignments. The remaining ~8% of essential genes that lack a KEGG KO are exactly the kind of well-supported-but-unexplained genes that make good discovery targets, a direct link to [Functional Dark Matter](../topics/functional-dark-matter.md). Reference protein families for that follow-up come from resources like [Kbase Uniref](../data/kbase-uniref.md); the broader strain-engineering context connects to [Phagefoundry](../data/phagefoundry.md).

## Topics

The project, and therefore this author's footprint, spans five themes:

- **[Adp1 Model System](../topics/adp1-model-system.md)** — the organizing subject: the integrated ADP1 multi-omics database, its BERDL connectivity, the media-dependent essentiality, and the targeted proteomics effects all sit here.
- **[Gene Fitness](../topics/gene-fitness.md)** — the unique mutant-fitness resource, condition-specific carbon-source behavior, media-dependent essentiality, and the essential-vs-dispensable annotation contrast.
- **[Metabolic Pathways](../topics/metabolic-pathways.md)** — the genome-scale model, FBA/TnSeq concordance, the gapfilling caveat on growth predictions, and conserved core metabolism.
- **[Pangenome Architecture](../topics/pangenome-architecture.md)** — the 14-genome reaction conservation, the essential-genes-in-core pattern, and the cluster-ID bridge between ADP1 and BERDL clusters.
- **[Functional Dark Matter](../topics/functional-dark-matter.md)** — the unannotated essential genes flagged as candidates for discovering novel essential functions.
