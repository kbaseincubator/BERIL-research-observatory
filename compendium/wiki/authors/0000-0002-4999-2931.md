# Adam P. Arkin

Adam P. Arkin is a contributor in this compendium, identified by ORCID `0000-0002-4999-2931`. This page exists to summarize the footprint of his work across the wiki: which research projects he contributed to, which scientific topics those projects touch, and how their findings, caveats, and follow-up opportunities connect. His ten projects span an unusually wide range -- from designing live bacterial therapeutics for the cystic-fibrosis lung to censusing the genes that bacteria carry but biologists cannot yet name -- but they share a common engine. Each takes a hypothesis about how genomes encode ecology, tests it at scale against the BERDL data lakehouse (the [Kbase Ke Pangenome](../data/kbase-ke-pangenome.md) of ~27,700 species, the [Kescience Fitnessbrowser](../data/kescience-fitnessbrowser.md) of genome-wide fitness assays, and field metagenomes from [Nmdc Arkin](../data/nmdc-arkin.md)), and then asks whether the apparent signal survives the confounders that routinely fool comparative genomics: phylogeny, genome size, annotation error, and cohort sampling bias.

## Overview

Arkin's footprint divides into roughly three problem families, all approached with the same computational-then-validation logic.

The first is **microbiome engineering** -- using genomic and metabolic reasoning to design defined microbial interventions ([Microbiome Engineering](../topics/microbiome-engineering.md)). Two projects here are explicitly therapeutic: a five-organism oral-commensal formulation predicted to suppress *Pseudomonas aeruginosa* in the cystic-fibrosis lung by saturating its amino-acid niche, and a phage-cocktail design framework for inflammatory bowel disease that has to be tailored to a patient's microbiome ecotype rather than prescribed at the cohort level. A third, a plant-microbiome project, mines the pangenome for the gene markers that distinguish beneficial growth promoters from pathogens as a foundation for synthetic-community biocontrol design.

The second family is **field ecology read off gene content** -- inferring how environments structure microbial communities and metabolism ([Environment Biogeography](../topics/environment-biogeography.md), [Subsurface Genomics](../topics/subsurface-genomics.md), [Microbial Ecotypes](../topics/microbial-ecotypes.md)). This includes mapping a subsurface contamination plume purely from 16S community structure at the ENIGMA Oak Ridge field site, predicting bacterial growth and metabolite output from gene presence/absence across the ENIGMA strain collection, and two pan-bacterial ecology surveys -- of the carbon-storage polymer PHB and of prophages -- that ask which genomic traits track which environments across the whole BERDL tree.

The third family is the **functional dark matter problem**: the large fraction of bacterial genes that have a sequence but no assigned function ([Functional Dark Matter](../topics/functional-dark-matter.md), [Gene Fitness](../topics/gene-fitness.md)). Three projects attack this -- one builds an actionable, experimentally prioritized census of dark genes across Fitness Browser organisms; a companion project narrows that census to the *truly* dark genes that survive re-annotation; and a third builds a bacterial-domain atlas of horizontal-gene-transfer and innovation events to ask which gene families are exchanged versus inherited ([Mobile Genetic Elements](../topics/mobile-genetic-elements.md), [Pangenome Architecture](../topics/pangenome-architecture.md)).

A throughline across all three families is **disciplined skepticism about the signal itself**. Nearly every project carries a headline result and an attached caveat that revises it downward: a metabolic model whose cross-validated predictive power is roughly half its training fit [\[1\]](#references), a dark-gene count that shrinks when a newer annotator is applied [\[2\]](#references), a compartment effect that mostly vanishes once taxonomic sampling is accounted for [\[3\]](#references), an effect size that collapsed from a formula error [\[4\]](#references). The recurring confounders are genome size, phylogeny, annotation vintage, and which strains happened to be sampled -- and Arkin's projects make auditing those confounders part of the result rather than a footnote.

## Projects

### cf_formulation_design

This project designs a live bacterial therapeutic for the cystic-fibrosis (CF) lung by reasoning about metabolic competition rather than direct antagonism. The logic is niche exclusion: if a defined community of harmless oral commensals can collectively consume every nutrient that *P. aeruginosa* depends on, the pathogen is starved out. The central design result is a **five-organism FDA-safe formulation** (*Neisseria mucosa*, *Streptococcus salivarius*, *Micrococcus luteus*, *Rothia dentocariosa*, *Gemella sanguinis*) that covers 100% of PA14's amino-acid niche with 78% mean inhibition [\[5\]](#references), with full niche coverage first achieved at a three-organism core [\[6\]](#references). The strategy is robust to PA diversity because the amino-acid catabolic pathways being targeted are 97.4% conserved across 1,796 lung PA genomes, and the formulation species' own metabolic capabilities are species-level traits conserved above 95% across hundreds of genomes via GapMind pangenome analysis [\[7\]](#references) -- GapMind being a tool that scores how completely an organism encodes a given biosynthetic or catabolic pathway. Two anchors (*Rothia dentocariosa*, *Neisseria mucosa*) are naturally lung-adapted rather than repurposed gut commensals [\[8\]](#references), and *N. mucosa* shows the highest patient engraftability score, marking it the most promising colonization anchor [\[9\]](#references).

The mechanistic picture is consistent: no single commensal outgrows PA14 on any carbon substrate, so exclusion must be a community-level property [\[10\]](#references); metabolic carbon-source overlap with PA14 predicts inhibition (r = 0.384) but explains only ~27% of the variance [\[11\]](#references); and there is no single selective prebiotic, because PA14 outgrows the average commensal on every amino acid and simple sugar tested [\[12\]](#references). The most promising lever is timing -- commensals begin growing before PA14 on 43.1% of substrate comparisons, suggesting pre-colonization matters more than raw growth rate [\[13\]](#references) -- alongside candidate sugar-alcohol/pentose prebiotics (xylitol, myoinositol, xylose) that the commensals can use but PA14 cannot, because lung-adapted PA undergoes metabolic streamlining and loses sugar-catabolism pathways while keeping amino-acid catabolism invariant [\[14\]](#references).

The caveats here are unusually load-bearing and Arkin surfaces them honestly. The multivariate metabolic model of inhibition **overfits**: its cross-validated R-squared is ~0.145 versus a training R-squared of 0.274, so true out-of-sample power is closer to 15% [\[1\]](#references). All inhibition assays were run in **planktonic** competition, whereas PA14 grows mainly in CF-lung biofilms with very different spatial and diffusion dynamics [\[15\]](#references). And the assays used **PA14**, an ExoU+ strain representing under 5% of CF PA -- T3SS effector typing of 6,760 genomes shows CF isolates are overwhelmingly ExoS+ (94%) [\[16\]](#references) -- so the measured inhibition has not been validated against the dominant clinical lineage [\[17\]](#references). There is also a design tension: *M. luteus* is the keystone species for full niche coverage yet has zero detected patient engraftability and no lung genomes [\[18\]](#references), so the most metabolically important member is the least likely to persist. The highest-priority validations follow directly: measuring the complete 10-pair interaction matrix of the five core species [\[19\]](#references) and experimentally testing the predicted selective sugar-alcohol prebiotics [\[20\]](#references).

### ibd_phage_targeting

This project designs phage therapy for inflammatory bowel disease (IBD), and its core conclusion is that **one cocktail cannot fit all patients** ([Microbiome Engineering](../topics/microbiome-engineering.md), [Microbial Ecotypes](../topics/microbial-ecotypes.md)). Two independent clustering methods on 8,489 metagenomic samples converge on four reproducible IBD microbiome ecotypes (E0-E3) [\[21\]](#references), and clinical covariates alone separate healthy from IBD but cannot distinguish the transitional E1 from the severe E3 ecotype -- so metagenomic ecotyping is required before a cocktail can be chosen [\[22\]](#references). A confound-free meta-analysis across four cohorts recovers the canonical Crohn's signature (pathobionts up, protective commensals down) [\[23\]](#references), and among the actionable Tier-A pathobionts, iron acquisition emerges as a defining genomic capability: their genomes are enriched for iron-siderophore biosynthetic clusters [\[24\]](#references), and only the *E. coli* (AIEC) subset carries the combined iron-siderophore and genotoxin signature, localizing iron-driven Crohn's biology to *E. coli* [\[25\]](#references).

On the design side, a greedy minimum-set-cover over experimentally tested phage-strain susceptibility from [Phagefoundry](../data/phagefoundry.md) yields a five-phage cocktail covering 94.7% of 188 *E. coli* strains [\[26\]](#references). But cocktail-to-patient matching is fragile: a single patient's ecotype can drift between E1 and E3 between visits (Jaccard 0.60), motivating state-dependent re-design [\[27\]](#references), and no E1 Crohn's patient can be treated with a pure phage cocktail -- a hybrid combining phages with non-phage alternatives is required [\[28\]](#references). Phage cocktails designed at the cohort level will systematically mismatch individual patients [\[29\]](#references).

The caveats define the project's honesty about evidence strength. The pooled curatedMetagenomicData has healthy and Crohn's samples drawn from **disjoint source studies**, making a naive case-vs-control mixed model structurally unidentifiable and forcing a within-substudy CD-vs-nonIBD design [\[30\]](#references). The E3-ecotype Tier-A target list rests on single-study evidence and is explicitly **provisional** [\[31\]](#references). And there is a coverage gap exactly where it hurts: the two highest-priority Crohn's pathobiont targets, *H. hathewayi* and *M. gnavus*, have the weakest phage availability [\[32\]](#references). The natural follow-up is querying external phage databases (INPHARED, IMG/VR) to close that gap [\[33\]](#references), and building a reusable taxonomy-synonymy layer that any multi-cohort microbiome project needs [\[34\]](#references).

### plant_microbiome_ecotypes

This project mines the pangenome for the gene markers that distinguish plant-beneficial bacteria from plant pathogens, as a foundation for synthetic-community biocontrol ([Microbiome Engineering](../topics/microbiome-engineering.md), [Microbial Ecotypes](../topics/microbial-ecotypes.md)). A clean architectural result anchors it: beneficial plant-growth-promoting gene clusters are predominantly **core-encoded** (64.6% core fraction) while pathogenic clusters are **accessory** (45.2% core) [\[35\]](#references), a difference far exceeding the genome-wide baseline. Marker robustness is tiered honestly: of the candidate markers, only three are species-level robust under combined cluster-robust GLM and within-genus permutation tests (nitrogen fixation, ACC deaminase, type III secretion system), five are cassette-level, and six are not robust [\[36\]](#references). An independent MGnify cross-validation confirms the T3SS marker, finding it ~2x more prevalent in rhizosphere than bulk soil [\[37\]](#references).

This is also the project most disciplined about its own statistical artifacts -- a useful case study in why naive enrichment scores mislead. The headline compartment effect (PERMANOVA R-squared = 0.527) turned out to be **largely a taxonomic-sampling artifact**: excluding a handful of genome-rich rhizobial and *Pseudomonas* clades drops the residual R-squared to 0.072 [\[3\]](#references), and 94.2% of tested ortholog groups were significantly plant-associated, indicating the raw enrichment mostly reflects genome-wide taxonomic divergence rather than plant-specific biology [\[38\]](#references). Only the **50 ortholog groups surviving phylum-level phylogenetic control** are candidate plant-interaction genes [\[39\]](#references), dominated by electron-transport and iron-sulfur-cluster functions [\[40\]](#references). A "dual-nature" rate of 78.7% persists even after a refined 17-marker panel [\[41\]](#references), but it is genome-size-dependent, and the categorical dual-nature label cannot discriminate beneficial from pathogenic species [\[42\]](#references). A spectacular caveat documents the danger directly: a Phase-1 complementarity effect of Cohen d = -7.54 was a **formula error** [\[4\]](#references); the corrected effect is about -0.4, though the functional-redundancy direction held [\[43\]](#references). The proposed validation is RNAseq under beneficial versus pathogenic conditions on the Tier-1 markers [\[44\]](#references).

### enigma_sso_asv_ecology

This project reconstructs the subsurface ecology of the ENIGMA Oak Ridge field site (the "SSO" wells) from 16S amplicon data alone ([Subsurface Genomics](../topics/subsurface-genomics.md), [Environment Biogeography](../topics/environment-biogeography.md)). Its striking claim is that a **contamination-plume flow path is visible purely in Bray-Curtis community similarity** -- demonstrating that 16S community structure can map subsurface hydrology at meter scale [\[45\]](#references). All findings converge on a single model: a contamination plume entering from the northeast and flowing southwest through the saturated zone [\[46\]](#references). Wells U3, M6, and L7 form a diagonal community-similarity corridor aligned with the expected flow path [\[47\]](#references); genus-level functional inference arranges biogeochemical processes onto the 3x3 well grid in a sequence that recapitulates the thermodynamic redox ladder [\[48\]](#references); and the central well M5 hosts the highest denitrification potential (7.7% *Rhodanobacter*) [\[49\]](#references). Depth dominates the structure -- hydrogeological depth zone explains 27.5% of sediment community variance while well identity is not significant [\[50\]](#references) -- and groundwater communities are remarkably stable over 9 days, with sampling date explaining only 0.8% of variance versus 49.9% for well identity [\[51\]](#references).

The honesty here concerns what gene content can and cannot stand in for. All environmental inferences rest on **community composition alone**, because the SSO geochemistry has not been loaded into BERDL -- so the plume model awaits geochemical confirmation [\[52\]](#references). Genus-level functional annotation covers only **21% of total reads**, making process-abundance estimates lower bounds [\[53\]](#references). And only 5 of 9 wells have groundwater ASV data, missing the critical M5 denitrification hotspot [\[54\]](#references). The two follow-ups are concrete: loading the 221 registered SSO geochemistry samples into [Enigma Coral](../data/enigma-coral.md) to directly correlate composition with measured parameters [\[55\]](#references), and shotgun metagenomics at the same spatial resolution to capture the unclassified read majority [\[56\]](#references).

### genotype_to_phenotype_enigma

This project asks how far bacterial phenotype can be predicted from gene content across the ENIGMA strain collection, and its answer is sharply conditional ([Gene Fitness](../topics/gene-fitness.md), [Metabolic Pathways](../topics/metabolic-pathways.md)). **Binary growth** (does a strain grow on a substrate or not) is predictable from KO gene presence/absence on amino acids (AUC 0.775) and nucleosides (0.780) [\[57\]](#references), but **continuous growth parameters** (maximum rate, lag, yield) are not predictable at all under cross-genus holdout [\[58\]](#references), a fundamental limit because gene presence encodes metabolic capability, not kinetic rate. The most important methodological finding is about training-set size: mechanistic, condition-specific genotype-to-phenotype prediction needs on the order of 10^4 training pairs [\[59\]](#references). With only n=7 strains the model learns genome-scale artifacts, but at 46K pairs the same architecture surfaces the mechanistically correct transporters and enzymes (rbsC for ribose, proP for proline/betaine, pcaB for protocatechuate). The corpus that makes this possible is 27,632 growth curves across 123 strains, aligned to [Kescience Fitnessbrowser](../data/kescience-fitnessbrowser.md) RB-TnSeq data -- random-barcode transposon sequencing, which measures each gene's fitness contribution under a condition -- through 486 strain-by-condition anchor pairs [\[60\]](#references).

A separate ecological result grounds the field site: a global pH-driven niche partition across 464K worldwide 16S samples explains local Oak Ridge co-occurrence [\[61\]](#references), and all ENIGMA *Pseudomonas* isolates belong to the environmental *Pseudomonas_E* clade rather than the clinically dominant lineage [\[62\]](#references). Growth-predictive KOs and metabolite-production KOs are essentially uncorrelated feature sets answering different questions [\[63\]](#references), and top SHAP KOs show only 1.19x enrichment for Fitness Browser effects, reflecting that gene presence and gene essentiality are different biological questions [\[64\]](#references).

The caveats are about method-appropriateness and database hygiene. Cross-dataset condition alignment is **string-based** (only 42 molecular matches; ChEBI-ID canonicalization would expand this to 60-80 matches) [\[65\]](#references). And linking field isolates by short strain names is hazardous -- "MT20" matched *Streptococcus pneumoniae* and injected 1,751 spurious clinical genomes before GTDB-Tk taxonomy corrected it [\[66\]](#references). Proposed next steps are field-relevance-weighted active learning (a concrete 50-experiment round) [\[67\]](#references) and linking strain wells to per-well geochemistry in CORAL [\[68\]](#references).

### functional_dark_matter

This project builds an actionable census of bacterial **functional dark matter** -- genes that carry a sequence but no assigned function -- across the 48 [Kescience Fitnessbrowser](../data/kescience-fitnessbrowser.md) organisms ([Functional Dark Matter](../topics/functional-dark-matter.md), [Gene Fitness](../topics/gene-fitness.md)). Of all genes, 57,011 (24.9%) lack functional annotation and 17,344 of these have experimentally measurable phenotypes (strong fitness effects or essentiality), defining the "actionable" dark matter [\[69\]](#references). The work makes that set tractable: a darkness spectrum sorts all 57,011 genes into five tiers (from T1 Void with zero evidence to T5 Dawn, nearly characterized), and a weighted set-cover of 42 organisms from 28 genera covers 95% of actionable priority [\[70\]](#references). Guilt-by-association inference is then layered on: 69.3% of dark genes link to the pangenome and 6,142 fall in ICA fitness modules; 65 ortholog groups show cross-organism fitness concordance [\[71\]](#references); and synteny plus co-fitness analysis yields 998 double-validated dark gene-operon partner pairs, the highest-confidence predictions [\[72\]](#references). Multi-dimensional scoring over 6 evidence axes produces 100 top candidates (82% with high-confidence hypotheses) [\[73\]](#references), and the 9,557 **essential** dark genes -- 55% of the actionable set, invisible to fitness-centric ranking -- are ranked separately into a top-50 CRISPRi-ready list [\[74\]](#references). A field link is established too: lab fitness predicts field distribution for 61.7% of testable dark gene clusters, and all four pre-registered abiotic predictions were confirmed against [Nmdc Arkin](../data/nmdc-arkin.md) metagenomes [\[75\]](#references).

The central caveats are about annotation vintage and taxonomic bias. Bakta v1.12.0 reclassifies **83.7%** of pangenome-linked dark genes as not hypothetical, so the 57,011 count **overestimates** the truly uncharacterized genes [\[2\]](#references). And the 48 Fitness Browser organisms are 77% Pseudomonadota with Actinobacteria absent, biasing prioritization toward Gammaproteobacteria [\[76\]](#references). Querying the full 27,690-species GTDB pangenome shows over half of dark gene ortholog groups are pan-bacterial, demonstrating that functional dark matter is not a minor annotation gap but a fundamental limitation in understanding broad bacterial biology [\[77\]](#references). AlphaFold2 structure prediction offers the only remaining computational inference strategy for the T1 Void genes that lack all other evidence [\[78\]](#references).

### truly_dark_genes

This companion project narrows the census above to the genes that remain dark after re-annotation -- the **truly dark** set ([Functional Dark Matter](../topics/functional-dark-matter.md), [Gene Fitness](../topics/gene-fitness.md)). Of 39,532 pangenome-linked Fitness Browser dark genes, bakta v1.12.0 reannotates 83.7%, leaving 6,427 (16.3%) where both the original pipeline and bakta agree the protein is hypothetical [\[79\]](#references). These genes are structurally distinct from mere annotation lag: shorter (median 121 vs 194 aa), less core (43.1% vs 72.7%), lower GC, and far less likely to have orthologs (29.3% vs 63.7%) -- consistent with genuine biological novelty [\[80\]](#references). They bear recent-HGT signatures (4.2x fewer cross-organism orthologs, higher GC deviation, 12% within two genes of a mobile element) [\[81\]](#references) and are concentrated in specific organisms, with *Methanococcus* strains S2 and JJ alone accounting for 55%, reflecting the underrepresentation of archaea in annotation databases [\[82\]](#references). A 12-dimensional clue matrix shows only 3.8% have zero annotation clues; 79.4% have UniRef50 links and 84.7% have database cross-references via [Kbase Uniref](../data/kbase-uniref.md), yet only ~4% have Pfam domains or KEGG KOs -- the databases contain the sequences but cannot assign function [\[83\]](#references). Contrary to the original hypothesis, dark genes with strong fitness phenotypes are depleted in stress conditions and enriched in nutrient, mixed-community, and iron conditions [\[84\]](#references), suggesting metabolic or community-interaction roles.

The caveats define a coverage frontier. An estimated 17,479 dark genes (31%) **lack pangenome links** and could not be assessed by bakta, leaving a 31% coverage gap [\[85\]](#references). Short genes are inherently harder to annotate, and some hypothetical calls may be bakta **false negatives** -- genes with real functions that did not match bakta's database [\[86\]](#references). Only 3 of 65 dark-gene ortholog groups with cross-organism concordance data are truly dark, making them nearly invisible to cross-organism fitness analysis [\[87\]](#references). Within ICA organisms, 41% of the neighbors of truly dark genes are themselves hypothetical, indicating contiguous genomic dark islands [\[88\]](#references). Because archaea contribute 55% of the truly dark genes, a focused *Methanococcus* deep-dive leveraging high co-fitness signal is the proposed next step [\[89\]](#references).

### gene_function_ecological_agora

This project builds a bacterial-domain atlas of horizontal gene transfer and gene-family innovation across 18,989 GTDB species representatives ([Mobile Genetic Elements](../topics/mobile-genetic-elements.md), [Pangenome Architecture](../topics/pangenome-architecture.md)). It computes 13.7M producer/participation scores and 17M Sankoff gain events with recipient-rank acquisition attribution -- Sankoff parsimony being a tree-based method to infer the minimum number of evolutionary events required to explain observed gene distributions -- and introduces a direction-agnostic Producer x Participation framework (Innovator-Isolated / Innovator-Exchange / Sink-Broker-Exchange / Stable) that works at deep ranks where full gene-tree reconciliation is intractable [\[90\]](#references). The atlas confirms expected biology: Cyanobacteria are Innovator-Exchange on photosystem II at the class rank [\[91\]](#references), and Mycobacteriaceae are Innovator-Isolated on the mycolic-acid pathway (high paralog expansion, low cross-clade exchange), refined to host-pathogen mycobacteria [\[92\]](#references). Pre-registered clades are strongly enriched in their expected biomes (Mycobacteriaceae host-pathogen 7.88x, Cyanobacteriia photic aquatic 2.77x) [\[93\]](#references). The ratio of recent-to-ancient gain events is itself a function-class signature -- CRISPR-Cas is highly recent-skewed (24.5x, HGT-active) while housekeeping genes are ancient-skewed [\[94\]](#references) -- and architectural promiscuity (Pfam architectures per KO) correlates with exchange [\[95\]](#references). Robustness comes from convergence across three independent substrates: parsimony effect size, sample-biome ecology metadata, and per-strain phenotype tables [\[96\]](#references).

The caveats are about reproducibility and rank-dependence. The classic **Alm 2006** correlation (r~0.74) between histidine-kinase count and lineage-specific expansion is **not reproduced** at full GTDB scale, recovering only modest correlations (r=0.10-0.29) [\[97\]](#references). Pangenome openness and between-species gain attribution are **uncorrelated** (Spearman r = -0.011 across 894 genera), so pangenome openness is not a valid cross-substrate validation of the acquisition signal [\[98\]](#references). And the PSII Innovator verdict is **strongly rank-dependent** (Stable at genus, Innovator-Exchange at class, Innovator-Isolated at phylum), so the rank must be chosen on biological grounds [\[99\]](#references). Composition-based donor inference at deep ranks remains blocked because per-CDS sequence is not in BERDL queryable schemas [\[100\]](#references); flagging MGE context per gain event is the identified path to a mechanistic explanation of exchange [\[101\]](#references).

### phb_granule_ecology

This project produces the first precise pan-bacterial prevalence estimate for **polyhydroxybutyrate (PHB)**, a carbon-storage polymer that bacteria synthesize as an energy reserve under nutrient-variable conditions, and tests whether its distribution tracks environmental variability ([Metabolic Pathways](../topics/metabolic-pathways.md), [Environment Biogeography](../topics/environment-biogeography.md)). Across 27,690 GTDB species, 21.9% carry phaC (PHA synthase) and 21.7% have a complete PHB pathway [\[102\]](#references). The biology is real and environmental: PHB prevalence follows a >10-fold gradient from temporally variable environments (plant 44%, soil 43.6%) to stable host-associated ones (clinical 7.4%, animal 3.3%) [\[103\]](#references), a chi-squared test of presence against environmental-variability category is overwhelmingly significant [\[104\]](#references), and the enrichment is **robust to genome size**, holding within all four size quartiles [\[105\]](#references). NMDC metagenomic cross-validation shows PHB-high genera are significantly more abundant than PHB-low genera [\[106\]](#references). The pathway is phylogenetically concentrated (Pseudomonadota alone account for 74.9% of phaC carriers) [\[107\]](#references) and shows widespread HGT -- 311 potential acquisition and 278 loss events [\[108\]](#references), with discordant phaC carried as accessory genome at nearly double the baseline rate [\[109\]](#references).

The caveats keep the ecological claim measured. The apparent link between PHB and **broader niche breadth largely disappears** after controlling for genome size (partial rho = -0.047) [\[110\]](#references), making it a confounded artefact. NMDC abiotic correlations with PHB inference were significant but modest (|rho| < 0.12) [\[111\]](#references), likely because abiotic measurements are point-in-time snapshots. And the PHB regulator phaR (K18080) was **absent from eggNOG annotations of all 27K species**, pointing to an annotation gap rather than true biological absence [\[112\]](#references). Family-level Fisher tests across 248 families revealed heterogeneous selection within phyla, with 41 PHB-enriched families and 62 depleted families [\[113\]](#references). Two follow-ups would sharpen the picture: mapping eggNOG Pfam domain names to accession IDs to enable proper Class I-IV PHA-synthase classification [\[114\]](#references), and querying the Fitness Browser for phaC mutant fitness under carbon-variable conditions [\[115\]](#references).

### prophage_ecology

This project surveys **prophages** -- bacteriophage genomes integrated into bacterial chromosomes, where they may replicate quietly alongside their host or be induced to a lytic cycle by environmental stress -- across the entire BERDL pangenome and asks whether environment or phylogeny better explains their distribution ([Mobile Genetic Elements](../topics/mobile-genetic-elements.md), [Environment Biogeography](../topics/environment-biogeography.md)). All 27,702 species carry prophage-associated gene clusters, with packaging, lysis, and lysogenic-regulation modules near-universal while head, tail, and anti-defense modules are structurally variable [\[116\]](#references). The headline result is that **environment explains substantially more variance in module composition than host phylogeny** (PERMANOVA F = 30.04 for environment vs 6.17 for phylogeny) [\[117\]](#references), and module-level and lineage-level ecology are decoupled -- modules show environment effects beyond phylogeny while TerL-terminase lineages do not, supporting modular exchange rather than whole-phage adaptation [\[118\]](#references). Constrained permutation nulls find eight significant module-by-environment enrichments, with tail, head-morphogenesis, and anti-defense modules enriched in human-associated bacteria [\[119\]](#references) -- suggesting host-phage arms races are most intense in human niches [\[120\]](#references). NMDC taxonomy-based inference across 6,365 samples finds 57 significant module-abiotic correlations concordant with the pangenome signal [\[121\]](#references).

The caveats are about detection method and confounding. Prophages were identified from **eggNOG functional annotations rather than dedicated detection tools**, likely inflating prevalence by including domesticated remnants and bacterial homologs [\[122\]](#references). **Genome size is the single dominant predictor** of prophage burden, yet environment still affects module count within each size quartile [\[123\]](#references). And the NMDC validation is **indirect**, since taxonomy-based burden inference assumes genus-level conservation of prophage content [\[124\]](#references). The near-universal packaging, lysis, and regulatory modules likely include many domesticated prophage remnants under purifying selection, so the structurally variable modules are more informative [\[125\]](#references). Running geNomad or VIBRANT on representative genomes to calibrate the false-positive rate [\[126\]](#references), and cross-referencing environment-enriched modules with known induction triggers (SOS, pH, temperature) [\[127\]](#references), are the proposed next steps.

## Topics

Arkin's ten projects touch all ten topics in this wiki, weighted toward functional dark matter, metabolic pathways, microbiome engineering, and environmental ecology.

[Functional Dark Matter](../topics/functional-dark-matter.md) is the most concentrated theme: the dark-matter census and the truly-dark-genes project are built around it, and it recurs as the connective tissue everywhere -- the absent phaR annotation, the eggNOG/bakta disagreements, the unclassified read majority at Oak Ridge, and the hidden functional capacity across all projects are all cases of function that automated annotation misses or mislabels.

[Gene Fitness](../topics/gene-fitness.md) underpins the Fitness Browser-based projects: RB-TnSeq fitness and essentiality define what counts as "actionable" dark matter, anchor the genotype-to-phenotype corpus, and supply the synteny/co-fitness evidence for dark-gene function prediction.

[Metabolic Pathways](../topics/metabolic-pathways.md) recurs in the metabolic-competition logic of the CF formulation, the GapMind niche-coverage analysis, the substrate-level growth prediction in the ENIGMA work, and the PHB carbon-storage survey.

[Microbiome Engineering](../topics/microbiome-engineering.md) covers the three design projects -- the CF commensal formulation, the IBD ecotype-tailored phage cocktail, and the plant synthetic-community biocontrol markers.

[Microbial Ecotypes](../topics/microbial-ecotypes.md) appears wherever a marker maps to a niche or clade -- the four reproducible IBD ecotypes, the beneficial/pathogenic plant split, the acid-tolerant Oak Ridge cluster, and the environmental *Pseudomonas_E* lineage.

[Environment Biogeography](../topics/environment-biogeography.md) frames the field-ecology projects: the SSO contamination plume, the PHB variability gradient, and the prophage environment-over-phylogeny result.

[Mobile Genetic Elements](../topics/mobile-genetic-elements.md) is central to the HGT/innovation atlas and the prophage survey, and recurs as the HGT signatures on truly dark genes, accessory phaC, and pathogen-associated transposase co-occurrence in the plant-microbiome project.
