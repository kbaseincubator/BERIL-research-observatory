# Adam P. Arkin

Adam P. Arkin is a contributor in this compendium, identified by ORCID `0000-0002-4999-2931`. This page exists to summarize the footprint of his work across the wiki: which research projects he contributed to, which scientific topics those projects touch, and how their findings, caveats, and follow-up opportunities connect. His ten projects span an unusually wide range -- from designing live bacterial therapeutics for the cystic-fibrosis lung to censusing the genes that bacteria carry but biologists cannot yet name -- but they share a common engine. Each takes a hypothesis about how genomes encode ecology, tests it at scale against the BERDL data lakehouse (the [Kbase Ke Pangenome](../data/kbase-ke-pangenome.md) of ~27,700 species, the [Kescience Fitnessbrowser](../data/kescience-fitnessbrowser.md) of genome-wide fitness assays, and field metagenomes from [Nmdc Arkin](../data/nmdc-arkin.md)), and then asks whether the apparent signal survives the confounders that routinely fool comparative genomics: phylogeny, genome size, annotation error, and cohort sampling bias.

## Overview

Arkin's footprint divides into roughly three problem families, all approached with the same computational-then-validation logic.

The first is **microbiome engineering** -- using genomic and metabolic reasoning to design defined microbial interventions ([Microbiome Engineering](../topics/microbiome-engineering.md)). Two projects here are explicitly therapeutic: a five-organism oral-commensal formulation predicted to suppress *Pseudomonas aeruginosa* in the cystic-fibrosis lung by saturating its amino-acid niche, and a phage-cocktail design framework for inflammatory bowel disease that has to be tailored to a patient's microbiome ecotype rather than prescribed at the cohort level. A third, a plant-microbiome project, mines the pangenome for the gene markers that distinguish beneficial growth promoters from pathogens as a foundation for synthetic-community biocontrol design.

The second family is **field ecology read off gene content** -- inferring how environments structure microbial communities and metabolism ([Environment Biogeography](../topics/environment-biogeography.md), [Subsurface Genomics](../topics/subsurface-genomics.md), [Microbial Ecotypes](../topics/microbial-ecotypes.md)). This includes mapping a subsurface contamination plume purely from 16S community structure at the ENIGMA Oak Ridge field site, predicting bacterial growth and metabolite output from gene presence/absence across the ENIGMA strain collection, and two pan-bacterial ecology surveys -- of the carbon-storage polymer PHB and of prophages -- that ask which genomic traits track which environments across the whole BERDL tree.

The third family is the **functional dark matter problem**: the large fraction of bacterial genes that have a sequence but no assigned function ([Functional Dark Matter](../topics/functional-dark-matter.md), [Gene Fitness](../topics/gene-fitness.md)). Three projects attack this -- one builds an actionable, experimentally prioritized census of dark genes across Fitness Browser organisms; a companion project narrows that census to the *truly* dark genes that survive re-annotation; and a third builds a bacterial-domain atlas of horizontal-gene-transfer and innovation events to ask which gene families are exchanged versus inherited ([Mobile Genetic Elements](../topics/mobile-genetic-elements.md), [Pangenome Architecture](../topics/pangenome-architecture.md)).

A throughline across all three families is **disciplined skepticism about the signal itself**. Nearly every project carries a headline result and an attached caveat that revises it downward: a metabolic model whose cross-validated predictive power is roughly half its training fit, a dark-gene count that shrinks when a newer annotator is applied, a compartment effect that mostly vanishes once taxonomic sampling is accounted for, an effect size that collapsed from a formula error. The recurring confounders are genome size, phylogeny, annotation vintage, and which strains happened to be sampled -- and Arkin's projects make auditing those confounders part of the result rather than a footnote.

## Projects

### cf_formulation_design

This project designs a live bacterial therapeutic for the cystic-fibrosis (CF) lung by reasoning about metabolic competition rather than direct antagonism. The logic is niche exclusion: if a defined community of harmless oral commensals can collectively consume every nutrient that *P. aeruginosa* depends on, the pathogen is starved out. The central design result is a **five-organism FDA-safe formulation** (*Neisseria mucosa*, *Streptococcus salivarius*, *Micrococcus luteus*, *Rothia dentocariosa*, *Gemella sanguinis*) that covers 100% of PA14's amino-acid niche with 78% mean inhibition, with full niche coverage first achieved at a three-organism core. The strategy is robust to PA diversity because the amino-acid catabolic pathways being targeted are 97.4% conserved across 1,796 lung PA genomes, and the formulation species' own metabolic capabilities are species-level traits conserved above 95% across hundreds of genomes via GapMind pangenome analysis -- GapMind being a tool that scores how completely an organism encodes a given biosynthetic or catabolic pathway. Two anchors (*Rothia dentocariosa*, *Neisseria mucosa*) are naturally lung-adapted rather than repurposed gut commensals, and *N. mucosa* shows the highest patient engraftability score, marking it the most promising colonization anchor.

The mechanistic picture is consistent: no single commensal outgrows PA14 on any carbon substrate, so exclusion must be a community-level property; metabolic carbon-source overlap with PA14 predicts inhibition (r = 0.384) but explains only ~27% of the variance; and there is no single selective prebiotic, because PA14 outgrows the average commensal on every amino acid and simple sugar tested. The most promising lever is timing -- commensals begin growing before PA14 on 43.1% of substrate comparisons, suggesting pre-colonization matters more than raw growth rate -- alongside candidate sugar-alcohol/pentose prebiotics (xylitol, myoinositol, xylose) that the commensals can use but PA14 cannot, because lung-adapted PA undergoes metabolic streamlining and loses sugar-catabolism pathways while keeping amino-acid catabolism invariant.

The caveats here are unusually load-bearing and Arkin surfaces them honestly. The multivariate metabolic model of inhibition **overfits**: its cross-validated R-squared is ~0.145 versus a training R-squared of 0.274, so true out-of-sample power is closer to 15%. All inhibition assays were run in **planktonic** competition, whereas PA14 grows mainly in CF-lung biofilms with very different spatial and diffusion dynamics. And the assays used **PA14**, an ExoU+ strain representing under 5% of CF PA -- T3SS effector typing of 6,760 genomes shows CF isolates are overwhelmingly ExoS+ (94%) -- so the measured inhibition has not been validated against the dominant clinical lineage. There is also a design tension: *M. luteus* is the keystone species for full niche coverage yet has zero detected patient engraftability and no lung genomes, so the most metabolically important member is the least likely to persist. The highest-priority validations follow directly: measuring the complete 10-pair interaction matrix of the five core species (a single antagonistic pair could invalidate the design) and experimentally testing the predicted selective sugar-alcohol prebiotics.

### ibd_phage_targeting

This project designs phage therapy for inflammatory bowel disease (IBD), and its core conclusion is that **one cocktail cannot fit all patients** ([Microbiome Engineering](../topics/microbiome-engineering.md), [Microbial Ecotypes](../topics/microbial-ecotypes.md)). Two independent clustering methods on 8,489 metagenomic samples converge on four reproducible IBD microbiome ecotypes (E0-E3), and clinical covariates alone separate healthy from IBD but cannot distinguish the transitional E1 from the severe E3 ecotype -- so metagenomic ecotyping is required before a cocktail can be chosen. A confound-free meta-analysis across four cohorts recovers the canonical Crohn's signature (pathobionts up, protective commensals down), and among the actionable Tier-A pathobionts, iron acquisition emerges as a defining genomic capability: their genomes are enriched for iron-siderophore biosynthetic clusters, and only the *E. coli* (AIEC) subset carries the combined iron-siderophore and genotoxin signature, localizing iron-driven Crohn's biology to *E. coli*.

On the design side, a greedy minimum-set-cover over experimentally tested phage-strain susceptibility from [Phagefoundry](../data/phagefoundry.md) yields a five-phage cocktail covering 94.7% of 188 *E. coli* strains. But cocktail-to-patient matching is fragile: a single patient's ecotype can drift between E1 and E3 between visits (Jaccard 0.60), motivating state-dependent re-design, and no E1 Crohn's patient can be treated with a pure phage cocktail -- a hybrid combining phages with non-phage alternatives is required.

The caveats define the project's honesty about evidence strength. The pooled curatedMetagenomicData has healthy and Crohn's samples drawn from **disjoint source studies**, making a naive case-vs-control mixed model structurally unidentifiable and forcing a within-substudy CD-vs-nonIBD design. The E3-ecotype Tier-A target list rests on single-study evidence and is explicitly **provisional**. And there is a coverage gap exactly where it hurts: the two highest-priority Crohn's pathobiont targets, *H. hathewayi* and *M. gnavus*, have the weakest phage availability. The natural follow-ups are querying external phage databases (INPHARED, IMG/VR) to close that gap, and building a reusable taxonomy-synonymy layer that any multi-cohort microbiome project needs.

### plant_microbiome_ecotypes

This project mines the pangenome for the gene markers that distinguish plant-beneficial bacteria from plant pathogens, as a foundation for synthetic-community biocontrol ([Microbiome Engineering](../topics/microbiome-engineering.md), [Microbial Ecotypes](../topics/microbial-ecotypes.md)). A clean architectural result anchors it: beneficial plant-growth-promoting gene clusters are predominantly **core-encoded** (64.6% core fraction) while pathogenic clusters are **accessory** (45.2% core), a difference far exceeding the genome-wide baseline. Marker robustness is tiered honestly: of the candidate markers, only three are species-level robust under combined cluster-robust GLM and within-genus permutation tests (nitrogen fixation, ACC deaminase, type III secretion system), five are cassette-level (genus-acquired but not species-distinguishing), and six are not robust. An independent MGnify cross-validation confirms the T3SS marker, finding it ~2x more prevalent in rhizosphere than bulk soil.

This is also the project most disciplined about its own statistical artifacts -- a useful case study in why naive enrichment scores mislead. The headline compartment effect (PERMANOVA R-squared = 0.527) turned out to be **largely a taxonomic-sampling artifact**: excluding a handful of genome-rich rhizobial and *Pseudomonas* clades drops the residual R-squared to 0.072, and a properly separated db-RDA attributes only ~6% of functional variance to compartment. Likewise, because 94.2% of tested ortholog groups were significantly plant-associated, the raw enrichment mostly reflects genome-wide taxonomic divergence; only the **50 ortholog groups surviving phylum-level phylogenetic control** are candidate plant-interaction genes, and they are dominated by electron-transport and iron-sulfur-cluster functions. A "dual-nature" rate (species carrying both beneficial and pathogenic functions) of 78.7% persists even after a refined 17-marker panel, but it is genome-size-dependent (54% in the smallest size quartile vs 87% in the larger ones), and the categorical dual-nature label cannot discriminate beneficial from pathogenic species -- only the continuous pathogenicity ratio can. A spectacular caveat documents the danger directly: a Phase-1 complementarity effect of Cohen d = -7.54 was a **formula error** (dividing by the standard deviation of permutation means); the corrected effect is about -0.4, though the functional-redundancy direction held. The proposed validation is RNAseq under beneficial versus pathogenic conditions on the Tier-1 markers.

### enigma_sso_asv_ecology

This project reconstructs the subsurface ecology of the ENIGMA Oak Ridge field site (the "SSO" wells) from 16S amplicon data alone ([Subsurface Genomics](../topics/subsurface-genomics.md), [Environment Biogeography](../topics/environment-biogeography.md)). Its striking claim is that a **contamination-plume flow path is visible purely in Bray-Curtis community similarity** -- demonstrating that 16S community structure can map subsurface hydrology at meter scale. All findings converge on a single model: a contamination plume entering from the northeast and flowing southwest through the saturated zone. Wells U3, M6, and L7 form a diagonal community-similarity corridor aligned with the expected flow path from the uphill Area 3 source; genus-level functional inference arranges biogeochemical processes onto the 3x3 well grid in a sequence that recapitulates the thermodynamic redox ladder; and the central well M5 hosts the highest denitrification potential (7.7% *Rhodanobacter*), consistent with a plume mixing zone. Depth dominates the structure -- hydrogeological depth zone explains 27.5% of sediment community variance while well identity is not significant -- and groundwater communities are remarkably stable over 9 days (sampling date explains only 0.8% of variance versus 49.9% for well identity).

The honesty here concerns what gene content can and cannot stand in for. All environmental inferences rest on **community composition alone**, because the SSO geochemistry has not been loaded into BERDL -- so the plume model awaits geochemical confirmation. Genus-level functional annotation covers only **21% of total reads**, making process-abundance estimates lower bounds. And only 5 of 9 wells have groundwater ASV data, missing the critical M5 denitrification hotspot and U3 plume entry, so those hotspot interpretations rest on sediment data alone. The two follow-ups are concrete: loading the 221 registered SSO geochemistry samples into [Enigma Coral](../data/enigma-coral.md) to directly correlate composition with measured parameters, and shotgun metagenomics at the same spatial resolution to capture the unclassified read majority.

### genotype_to_phenotype_enigma

This project asks how far bacterial phenotype can be predicted from gene content across the ENIGMA strain collection, and its answer is sharply conditional ([Gene Fitness](../topics/gene-fitness.md), [Metabolic Pathways](../topics/metabolic-pathways.md)). **Binary growth** (does a strain grow on a substrate or not) is predictable from KO gene presence/absence on amino acids (AUC 0.775) and nucleosides (0.780), moderately on carbon sources, but not on metals, antibiotics, or nitrogen -- and **continuous growth parameters** (maximum rate, lag, yield) are not predictable at all under cross-genus holdout, a fundamental limit because gene presence encodes metabolic capability, not kinetic rate. The most important methodological finding is about training-set size: mechanistic, condition-specific genotype-to-phenotype prediction needs on the order of 10^4 training pairs. With only n=7 strains the model learns genome-scale artifacts, but at 46K pairs the same architecture surfaces the mechanistically correct transporters and enzymes (rbsC for ribose, proP for proline/betaine, pcaB for protocatechuate). The corpus that makes this possible is 27,632 growth curves across 123 strains, aligned to [Kescience Fitnessbrowser](../data/kescience-fitnessbrowser.md) RB-TnSeq data -- random-barcode transposon sequencing, which measures each gene's fitness contribution under a condition -- through 486 strain-by-condition anchor pairs.

A separate ecological result grounds the field site: a global pH-driven niche partition across 464K worldwide 16S samples explains local Oak Ridge co-occurrence, with an acid-tolerant *Rhodanobacter*-*Ralstonia*-*Dyella* cluster occupying environments ~1.35 pH units more acidic. And all ENIGMA *Pseudomonas* isolates belong to the environmental *Pseudomonas_E* clade rather than the clinically dominant lineage, so these field isolates occupy niches underrepresented in NCBI.

The caveats are about method-appropriateness and database hygiene. Growth-predictive KOs (across genera) and metabolite-production KOs (within genus) are essentially uncorrelated feature sets answering different questions, so a weak 1.19x enrichment of top SHAP KOs for Fitness Browser effects reflects that gene presence and gene essentiality are different biological questions, not non-mechanistic features. Cross-dataset condition alignment is **string-based** (only 42 molecular matches; ChEBI-ID canonicalization would expand this). And linking field isolates by short strain names is hazardous -- "MT20" matched *Streptococcus pneumoniae* and injected 1,751 spurious clinical genomes before GTDB-Tk taxonomy corrected it. Proposed next steps are field-relevance-weighted active learning (a concrete 50-experiment round) and linking strain wells to per-well geochemistry in CORAL.

### functional_dark_matter

This project builds an actionable census of bacterial **functional dark matter** -- genes that carry a sequence but no assigned function -- across the 48 [Kescience Fitnessbrowser](../data/kescience-fitnessbrowser.md) organisms ([Functional Dark Matter](../topics/functional-dark-matter.md), [Gene Fitness](../topics/gene-fitness.md)). Of all genes, 57,011 (24.9%) lack functional annotation, and 17,344 of these have experimentally measurable phenotypes (strong fitness effects or essentiality) -- the "actionable" dark matter. The work makes that set tractable. A darkness spectrum sorts all 57,011 genes into five tiers (from T1 Void with zero evidence to T5 Dawn, nearly characterized), and a weighted set-cover of 42 organisms from 28 genera covers 95% of actionable priority. Guilt-by-association inference is then layered on: 69.3% of dark genes link to the pangenome and 6,142 fall in ICA fitness modules; 65 ortholog groups show cross-organism fitness concordance (orthologs of the same unknown gene producing matching fitness effects across species); and synteny plus co-fitness analysis yields 998 double-validated dark gene-operon partner pairs, the highest-confidence predictions. Multi-dimensional scoring over 6 evidence axes produces 100 top candidates (82% with high-confidence hypotheses), and the 9,557 **essential** dark genes -- 55% of the actionable set, invisible to fitness-centric ranking -- are ranked separately into a top-50 CRISPRi-ready list. A field link is established too: lab fitness predicts field distribution for 61.7% of testable dark gene clusters, and all four pre-registered abiotic predictions were confirmed against [Nmdc Arkin](../data/nmdc-arkin.md) metagenomes.

The central caveats are about annotation vintage and taxonomic bias. Bakta v1.12.0 reclassifies **83.7%** of pangenome-linked dark genes as not hypothetical, so the 57,011 count **overestimates** the truly uncharacterized genes -- many are annotated in databases the Fitness Browser vintage never checked. And the 48 Fitness Browser organisms are 77% Pseudomonadota with Actinobacteria absent, biasing prioritization toward Gammaproteobacteria. The proposed dual-route experimental campaign (evidence-weighted RB-TnSeq/CRISPRi plus AlphaFold2 structure prediction for the top candidates) directly addresses turning predictions into function.

### truly_dark_genes

This companion project narrows the census above to the genes that remain dark after re-annotation -- the **truly dark** set ([Functional Dark Matter](../topics/functional-dark-matter.md), [Gene Fitness](../topics/gene-fitness.md)). Of 39,532 pangenome-linked Fitness Browser dark genes, bakta v1.12.0 reannotates 83.7%, leaving 6,427 (16.3%) where both the original pipeline and bakta agree the protein is hypothetical. These genes are structurally distinct from mere annotation lag: shorter (median 121 vs 194 aa), less core (43.1% vs 72.7%), lower GC, and far less likely to have orthologs (29.3% vs 63.7%) -- consistent with genuine biological novelty. They bear recent-HGT signatures (4.2x fewer cross-organism orthologs, higher GC deviation, 12% within two genes of a mobile element) and are concentrated in specific organisms, with *Methanococcus* strains S2 and JJ alone accounting for 55%, reflecting the underrepresentation of archaea in annotation databases. A 12-dimensional clue matrix shows only 3.8% have zero annotation clues; 79.4% have UniRef50 links and 84.7% have database cross-references via [Kbase Uniref](../data/kbase-uniref.md), yet only ~4% have Pfam domains or KEGG KOs -- the databases contain the sequences but cannot assign function. Contrary to the original hypothesis, dark genes with strong fitness phenotypes are depleted in stress conditions and enriched in nutrient, mixed-community, and iron conditions, suggesting metabolic or community-interaction roles.

The caveats define a coverage frontier. An estimated 17,479 dark genes (31%) **lack pangenome links** and could not be assessed by bakta, leaving a 31% coverage gap. Short genes are inherently harder to both annotate and assay, so the length difference may partially confound the other structural differences. And some hypothetical calls may be bakta **false negatives** -- genes with real functions that did not match bakta's database at the version tested. Because archaea contribute 55% of the truly dark genes, a focused *Methanococcus* deep-dive leveraging high co-fitness signal is the proposed next step.

### gene_function_ecological_agora

This project builds a bacterial-domain atlas of horizontal gene transfer and gene-family innovation across 18,989 GTDB species representatives ([Mobile Genetic Elements](../topics/mobile-genetic-elements.md), [Pangenome Architecture](../topics/pangenome-architecture.md)). It computes 13.7M producer/participation scores and 17M Sankoff gain events with recipient-rank acquisition attribution, and introduces a direction-agnostic Producer x Participation framework (Innovator-Isolated / Innovator-Exchange / Sink-Broker-Exchange / Stable) that works at deep ranks where full gene-tree reconciliation is intractable. The atlas confirms expected biology: Cyanobacteria are Innovator-Exchange on photosystem II at the class rank (the appropriate rank, since PSII predates Cyanobacteria diversification), and Mycobacteriaceae are Innovator-Isolated on the mycolic-acid pathway (high paralog expansion, low cross-clade exchange), refined to host-pathogen mycobacteria. Pre-registered clades are strongly enriched in their expected biomes (Mycobacteriaceae host-pathogen 7.88x, Cyanobacteriia photic aquatic 2.77x). The ratio of recent-to-ancient gain events is itself a function-class signature -- CRISPR-Cas is highly recent-skewed (24.5x, HGT-active) while housekeeping genes are ancient-skewed -- and architectural promiscuity (Pfam architectures per KO) correlates with exchange. Robustness comes from convergence across three independent substrates: parsimony effect size, sample-biome ecology metadata, and per-strain phenotype tables.

The caveats are about reproducibility and rank-dependence. The classic **Alm 2006** correlation (r~0.74) between histidine-kinase count and lineage-specific expansion is **not reproduced** at full GTDB scale, recovering only modest correlations (r=0.10-0.29). Pangenome openness and between-species gain attribution are **uncorrelated** (Spearman r = -0.011 across 894 genera), so pangenome openness is not a valid cross-substrate validation of the acquisition signal. And the PSII Innovator verdict is **strongly rank-dependent** (Stable at genus, Innovator-Exchange at class, Innovator-Isolated at phylum), so the rank must be chosen on biological grounds. Composition-based donor inference at deep ranks remains blocked because per-CDS sequence is not in BERDL queryable schemas; flagging MGE context per gain event is the identified path to a mechanistic explanation of exchange.

### phb_granule_ecology

This project produces the first precise pan-bacterial prevalence estimate for **polyhydroxybutyrate (PHB)**, a carbon-storage polymer, and tests whether its distribution tracks environmental variability ([Metabolic Pathways](../topics/metabolic-pathways.md), [Environment Biogeography](../topics/environment-biogeography.md)). Across 27,690 GTDB species, 21.9% carry phaC (PHA synthase) and 21.7% have a complete PHB pathway. The biology is real and environmental: PHB prevalence follows a >10-fold gradient from temporally variable environments (plant 44%, soil 43.6%) to stable host-associated ones (clinical 7.4%, animal 3.3%), a chi-squared test of presence against environmental-variability category is overwhelmingly significant, and the enrichment is **robust to genome size**, holding within all four size quartiles. NMDC metagenomic cross-validation shows PHB-high genera are more abundant than PHB-low genera. The pathway is phylogenetically concentrated (Pseudomonadota alone account for 74.9% of phaC carriers) and shows widespread HGT -- 311 potential acquisition and 278 loss events, with discordant phaC carried as accessory genome at nearly double the baseline rate, consistent with recent horizontal acquisition.

The caveats keep the ecological claim measured. The apparent link between PHB and **broader niche breadth largely disappears** after controlling for genome size (partial rho = -0.047), making it a confounded artefact rather than independent evidence. NMDC abiotic correlations with PHB inference were significant but modest (|rho| < 0.12), likely because abiotic measurements are point-in-time snapshots. And the PHB regulator phaR (K18080) was **absent from eggNOG annotations of all 27K species**, pointing to an annotation gap or KO misassignment rather than true biological absence. Two follow-ups would sharpen the picture: mapping eggNOG Pfam domain names to accession IDs to enable proper Class I-IV PHA-synthase classification, and querying the Fitness Browser for phaC mutant fitness under carbon-variable conditions.

### prophage_ecology

This project surveys **prophages** -- bacteriophage genomes integrated into bacterial chromosomes -- across the entire BERDL pangenome and asks whether environment or phylogeny better explains their distribution ([Mobile Genetic Elements](../topics/mobile-genetic-elements.md), [Environment Biogeography](../topics/environment-biogeography.md)). All 27,702 species carry prophage-associated gene clusters, with packaging, lysis, and lysogenic-regulation modules near-universal while head, tail, and anti-defense modules are structurally variable. The headline result is that **environment explains substantially more variance in module composition than host phylogeny** (PERMANOVA F = 30.04 for environment vs 6.17 for phylogeny), and module-level and lineage-level ecology are decoupled -- modules show environment effects beyond phylogeny while TerL-terminase lineages do not, supporting modular exchange rather than whole-phage adaptation. Constrained permutation nulls find eight significant module-by-environment enrichments, with tail, head-morphogenesis, and anti-defense modules enriched in human-associated bacteria beyond phylogenetic expectation -- suggesting host-phage arms races are most intense in human niches. This is concordant with [Nmdc Arkin](../data/nmdc-arkin.md): taxonomy-based inference across 6,365 NMDC samples finds 57 significant module-abiotic correlations, with head, tail, and anti-defense modules matching the pangenome signal.

The caveats are about detection method and confounding. Prophages were identified from **eggNOG functional annotations rather than dedicated detection tools**, likely inflating prevalence by including domesticated remnants and bacterial homologs at an uncharacterized false-positive rate -- so the structurally variable modules, not the near-universal ones, are the informative signal. **Genome size is the single dominant predictor** of prophage burden, and although environment still affects module count within each size quartile, residual confounding cannot be fully excluded. And the NMDC validation is **indirect**, since taxonomy-based burden inference assumes genus-level conservation of prophage content. Running geNomad or VIBRANT on representative genomes to calibrate the false-positive rate, and cross-referencing environment-enriched modules with known induction triggers (SOS, pH, temperature), are the proposed next steps.

## Topics

Arkin's ten projects touch all ten topics in this wiki, weighted toward functional dark matter, metabolic pathways, microbiome engineering, and environmental ecology.

- [Functional Dark Matter](../topics/functional-dark-matter.md) is the most concentrated theme: the dark-matter census and the truly-dark-genes project are built around it, and it recurs as the connective tissue everywhere -- the absent phaR annotation, the eggNOG/bakta disagreements, the unclassified read majority at Oak Ridge, the hidden multi-heme and PQQ products are all cases of function that automated annotation misses or mislabels.
- [Gene Fitness](../topics/gene-fitness.md) underpins the Fitness Browser-based projects: RB-TnSeq fitness and essentiality define what counts as "actionable" dark matter, anchor the genotype-to-phenotype corpus, and supply the synteny/co-fitness evidence for dark-gene function prediction.
- [Metabolic Pathways](../topics/metabolic-pathways.md) recurs in the metabolic-competition logic of the CF formulation, the GapMind niche-coverage analysis, the substrate-level growth prediction in the ENIGMA work, and the PHB carbon-storage survey.
- [Microbiome Engineering](../topics/microbiome-engineering.md) covers the three design projects -- the CF commensal formulation, the IBD ecotype-tailored phage cocktail, and the plant synthetic-community biocontrol markers.
- [Microbial Ecotypes](../topics/microbial-ecotypes.md) appears wherever a marker maps to a niche or clade -- the four reproducible IBD ecotypes, the beneficial/pathogenic plant split, the acid-tolerant Oak Ridge cluster, and the environmental *Pseudomonas_E* lineage.
- [Environment Biogeography](../topics/environment-biogeography.md) frames the field-ecology projects: the SSO contamination plume, the PHB variability gradient, and the prophage environment-over-phylogeny result.
- [Mobile Genetic Elements](../topics/mobile-genetic-elements.md) is central to the HGT/innovation atlas and the prophage survey, and recurs as the HGT signatures on truly dark genes, accessory phaC, and pathogen-associated transposase co-occurrence.
- [Pangenome Architecture](../topics/pangenome-architecture.md) runs through the core-vs-accessory partitioning (beneficial-core/pathogenic-accessory plant genes, accessory phaC), the pangenome-linkage that enables guilt-by-association, and the pangenome-openness analysis in the atlas.
- [Subsurface Genomics](../topics/subsurface-genomics.md) anchors the ENIGMA Oak Ridge projects -- the depth-dominated zonation, the plume corridor, and the subsurface strain collection.
- [Metal Resistance](../topics/metal-resistance.md) enters only at the margin, via the metal-condition growth phenotypes in the ENIGMA genotype-to-phenotype work, where growth on metals is notably *not* predictable from gene content alone.
