# David Lyon

David Lyon is a researcher whose work in this corpus spans the genomics of deep-subsurface microbial communities and the global distribution of lanthanide-dependent metabolism. This wiki page exists because Lyon is a contributing author on three BERIL projects — [`clay_confined_subsurface`](../projects/clay-confined-subsurface.md), [`bacillota_b_subsurface_accessory`](../projects/bacillota-b-subsurface-accessory.md), and [`lanthanide_methylotrophy_atlas`](../projects/lanthanide-methylotrophy-atlas.md) — that together generate a large fraction of the corpus's statements about [subsurface genomics](../topics/subsurface-genomics.md), [metabolic pathways](../topics/metabolic-pathways.md), [pangenome architecture](../topics/pangenome-architecture.md), and [metal resistance](../topics/metal-resistance.md).

## Overview

Lyon's projects all work from the BERDL pangenome — a collection of approximately 293,000 GTDB-classified bacterial and archaeal genomes — and use pangenome-scale Fisher's exact tests, binomial enrichment tests, and phylogenetic mixed models to test pre-registered hypotheses about subsurface microbial specialization and lanthanide biochemistry.

The clay-confined subsurface project asked which metabolic hallmarks distinguish deep-clay isolates (primarily from Mont Terri Opalinus boreholes in Switzerland) from soil-baseline and shallow-clay organisms. The most striking positive result came from the sulfate-reduction module: cultured deep-clay genomes carry the dissimilatory sulfate-reduction (SR) operon dsrAB-aprAB-sat in 5 of 5 Bacillota_B isolates versus only 4 of 19 soil-baseline Bacillota_B isolates, an odds ratio of infinity that survives within-phylum phylogenetic control [\[1\]](#references). At the cohort level the full "anaerobic toolkit" — combining the Wood–Ljungdahl carbon-fixation pathway, group 1 [NiFe]-hydrogenase for H₂ oxidation, and dissimilatory sulfate reduction — was enriched in deep clay versus baseline and shallow-clay cohorts (mean toolkit score 1.89 vs 0.39 vs 0.03 of 3 modules) [\[2\]](#references). However, within-phylum control revealed that the Wood–Ljungdahl and [NiFe]-hydrogenase components track the Bacillota_B lineage rather than habitat depth, since these markers are carried at high background rates even in soil Bacillota_B [\[3\]](#references). Sulfate reduction alone is the genuine deep-clay-specific signal.

The porewater-bias finding complements the metabolic picture. BERDL's cultured genomes carry the porewater "Bagnoud paradigm" SR-rich signature rather than the rock-attached "Mitzscherling paradigm" IR-rich signature [\[4\]](#references): SR enrichment relative to the Mitzscherling rock-attached null distribution is overwhelming (binomial p = 4 × 10⁻¹²) [\[5\]](#references), and the deep versus shallow cohort profiles are a mirror image — deep dominated by SR-only, shallow dominated by iron-reduction-only [\[6\]](#references). This is a direct, quantitative diagnostic that cultivation systematically captures the porewater fraction and misses rock-attached lineages [\[7\]](#references). The anchor genera include exactly the lineages Bagnoud identified across seven Mont Terri boreholes, including Desulfosporosinus and BRH-c8a [\[8\]](#references), lending cross-platform credibility to the indigeneity of the cohort.

One prior hypothesis — that deep-subsurface specialists should show elevated biosynthetic self-sufficiency, meaning the ability to synthesize all essential amino acids without cross-feeding — was not supported. Measured using GapMind (a database-backed pathway completeness scorer), the deep-clay cohort scored comparably to or slightly below the soil baseline (CheckM-filtered mean 15.5/18 vs 17.1/18 pathways complete, p = 0.009, Cohen's d = −0.84) [\[9\]](#references). This negative result is interpretively important: the extremely self-sufficient lineages described in the literature, such as *Ca.* Desulforudis audaxviator, are characteristically uncultivated and absent from a culture-collection-derived pangenome cohort [\[10\]](#references).

## Projects

### clay_confined_subsurface

This project established the cultivation-bias diagnostic and the sulfate-reduction porewater signal described above. It also identified a methodological error in its own H3 iron-reduction analysis: the KOs originally used as iron-reduction markers (K07811, K17324, K17323) encode TMAO reductase, glycerol ABC transport, and glycerol permease — unrelated to iron reduction [\[11\]](#references). After correction with a triple-signal multi-heme cytochrome detector (combining PFAM PF02085 and PF22678 with sequence-level CXXCH heme-binding motif counting), the original "shallow much greater than deep" iron-reduction pattern disappeared and no cohort comparison remained statistically significant [\[12\]](#references). The sulfate-reduction side of the same finding was unaffected, because its KEGG KO assignments (K11180, K11181, K00394, K00395, K00958) were correct from the start [\[13\]](#references).

Important caveats apply. Because the cohort is culture-derived, all conclusions apply only to cultivable porewater-cultured isolates; CPR/DPANN episymbionts and rock-attached Geobacter/Geothrix lineages are essentially absent [\[14\]](#references). The GapMind self-sufficiency metric also has a ceiling effect: the 18-pathway amino-acid universe saturates near 18 for most cultivable organisms, leaving limited resolving power at the upper end [\[15\]](#references). The strongest open direction is MAG-augmented expansion — ingesting deep-subsurface MAGs from Mont Terri, Olkiluoto, MX-80 bentonite, and Oak Ridge to test whether self-sufficiency and rock-attached signals emerge with uncultivated lineages [\[16\]](#references). The SR/IR cultivation-bias diagnostic itself is portable and could be applied to granite-hosted, basalt-hosted, and salt-cavern subsurface cohorts in BERDL [\[17\]](#references).

### bacillota_b_subsurface_accessory

This companion project examined the accessory genome of Bacillota_B specifically, using a pangenome enrichment approach: Fisher's exact testing on 14,109 Firmicutes-level eggNOG orthologous groups (OGs) with BH-FDR correction. The result was 547 OGs significantly enriched in deep-clay Bacillota_B versus soil-baseline Bacillota_B [\[18\]](#references), far exceeding the pre-registered minimum signal prediction of at least 10. The enriched set populates five functional categories pre-registered before analysis: anaerobic respiration, sporulation revival, mineral attachment, anaerobic regulators, and osmoadaptation, with anaerobic respiration as the largest annotated hit [\[19\]](#references). Among the top individual signals, COG1977 — encoding the molybdopterin cofactor required by anaerobic respiration enzymes such as nitrate reductase, formate dehydrogenase, and DMSO reductase — was present in all ten anchor genomes versus only 11 of 62 baseline genomes [\[20\]](#references).

A key counter-intuitive finding was genomic expansion rather than streamlining. Deep-clay Bacillota_B genomes are roughly 1 Mbp larger than soil-baseline congeners (4.1 vs 3.0 Mbp mean, Cohen's d = +1.39, p = 0.025), and this size difference is not a metagenome-assembled genome (MAG) quality artefact — anchor and baseline cohorts have identical CheckM completeness [\[21\]](#references). This is the opposite direction from the "small is mighty" streamlining observed in Patescibacteria/CPR, and suggests that within cultivable Bacillota_B, deep-clay specialization correlates with gene-content expansion, not reductive evolution [\[22\]](#references). See [pangenome architecture](../topics/pangenome-architecture.md) for broader context on genome size gradients across environments.

The iron-reduction correction described above established a reusable methodological contribution: combining curated PFAMs with sequence-based CXXCH heme-motif counting is a reliable multi-heme cytochrome detector applicable across the pangenome whenever KEGG KO assignment is unreliable for niche electron-transfer proteins [\[23\]](#references). Multi-heme cytochrome PFAMs are sparse within Bacillota_B itself (only about 6 clusters total across four PFAMs), so CXXCH motif counting carries most of the corrected iron-reduction signal [\[24\]](#references).

Methodological caveats matter here. With only 10 anchor versus 62 baseline genomes, marginal enrichment effects resting on anchor counts of 3–5 should be treated descriptively rather than inferentially, and genus-level phylogenetic confounding is only partly mitigated [\[25\]](#references). Keyword-based functional categorization also undercounted the anaerobic signal: manual inspection of top enriched OGs suggests the true anaerobic-respiration-relevant count is closer to 80–100 rather than the 42 returned by the keyword scanner, because many gene families encode anaerobic functions under domain names rather than the pre-registered keywords [\[26\]](#references). Future work on per-genus decomposition — separating genus-specific lineage markers from recurrent cross-genus signals — would improve both precision and statistical power [\[27\]](#references).

### lanthanide_methylotrophy_atlas

The third project asks a global question: how prevalent is lanthanide-dependent methanol oxidation across the bacterial kingdom, and where does it occur environmentally? The core enzyme is XoxF (methanol dehydrogenase, EC 1.1.2.8), which uses lanthanides (rare-earth elements, REEs) as a cofactor instead of the classical calcium-dependent MxaF (EC 1.1.2.7). Across 293,059 GTDB genomes, xoxF outnumbers mxaF by approximately 19:1 (3,690 vs 195 genomes; Clopper-Pearson 95% CI [13.07, 27.69]) [\[28\]](#references). This dominance is robust to phylogenetic correction: a Bayesian binomial GLMM with random intercepts for phylum and family yields a phylogeny-corrected ratio of approximately 143:1 [\[29\]](#references), ruling out the result as an artifact of Pseudomonadota's numerical dominance in the dataset.

The most striking xoxF carriers are not classical methylotrophs. Acidobacteriota carry xoxF at 28.3% per-genome rate (95:1 xoxF:mxaF ratio), and Gemmatimonadota at 25.4%, expanding the candidate set of lanthanide-utilizing organisms well beyond the organisms traditionally studied in C1 metabolism [\[30\]](#references). Host-associated environments, by contrast, are dramatically depleted in xoxF (odds ratio 0.058, p_BH = 0), consistent with the absence of methylotrophy in gut and host niches [\[31\]](#references), while soil and sediment environments show the strongest enrichment (OR = 1.92, p_BH = 6 × 10⁻³⁹) [\[32\]](#references). REE-impacted sites show a 3.5-fold descriptive elevation in xoxF rate, but at n = 37 this signal does not clear the FDR threshold (p_BH = 0.082) and should be treated as descriptive only [\[33\]](#references). Counterintuitively, the 37 REE-acid-mine-drainage MAGs are dominated by acidophilic and metal-tolerant lineages rather than methylotrophs — only 4 of 37 carry any xoxF, and none carry validated lanmodulin [\[34\]](#references). This connects to the [metal resistance](../topics/metal-resistance.md) topic, which covers how organisms cope with high metal concentrations.

Lanmodulin — a small protein that binds lanthanides with extraordinarily high affinity and is thought to be involved in REE acquisition by methylotrophs — shows total phylogenetic restriction: all 62 bakta-validated lanmodulin genomes fall within three alpha-Proteobacterial methylotroph families (Beijerinckiaceae, Acetobacteraceae, Hyphomicrobiaceae) [\[35\]](#references). However, lanmodulin co-occurrence with xoxF is 79%, just below the pre-registered 80% threshold (one-sided binomial p = 0.65), so lanmodulin presence does not reliably predict a co-located lanthanide-MDH operon [\[36\]](#references). An apparent "xoxF-without-PQQ" annotation gap — PQQ being an obligate XoxF cofactor — is largely an artifact: 59% of xoxF genomes lacking eggNOG PQQ annotations carry at least one bakta PQQ product hit that eggNOG missed [\[37\]](#references). This illustrates a broader marker-calibration lesson: eggNOG and bakta disagree sharply by marker type, with eggNOG `Preferred_name='lanM'` producing 505 false positives concentrated in unrelated gut Bacillota [\[38\]](#references), whereas bakta product calls for lanmodulin are reliable [\[39\]](#references).

Open directions include recruiting a larger collection of REE-impacted metagenomes to convert the descriptive enrichment signal into an inferential test [\[40\]](#references), and targeted RB-TnSeq (randomized-barcode transposon sequencing, a pooled fitness assay that measures genome-wide gene essentiality under a stress condition) under lanthanum or cerium chloride stress in the FitnessBrowser organism panel, which would identify the first transposon-validated REE-tolerance gene set in soil organisms already carrying xoxF [\[41\]](#references). The [gene fitness](../topics/gene-fitness.md) topic page covers RB-TnSeq methods and FitnessBrowser resources in more detail.

## Topics

Lyon's work intersects eight wiki topics. [Subsurface genomics](../topics/subsurface-genomics.md) and [metabolic pathways](../topics/metabolic-pathways.md) are the primary themes, capturing the deep-clay SR/toolkit findings and the lanthanide xoxF prevalence results respectively. [Pangenome architecture](../topics/pangenome-architecture.md) connects to the 547-OG enrichment and the genome-expansion finding. [Microbial ecotypes](../topics/microbial-ecotypes.md) is adjacent because the porewater-versus-rock-attached dichotomy is a concrete example of within-species habitat differentiation driving distinguishable genomic profiles. [Functional dark matter](../topics/functional-dark-matter.md) is relevant because a large fraction of the 547 enriched OGs remain unannotated or annotated only at the domain level, and the annotation-gap findings (eggNOG vs bakta disagreement, keyword scanner undercounting) illustrate how hard it is to assign function to novel subsurface gene families. [Metal resistance](../topics/metal-resistance.md) connects through the REE-AMD case study and the lanthanide-handling machinery. [Environment biogeography](../topics/environment-biogeography.md) captures the xoxF soil-versus-host gradient and the cultivation-bias diagnostic applicable across subsurface environments. [Gene fitness](../topics/gene-fitness.md) enters through the proposed RB-TnSeq REE-tolerance experiments.

The underlying genomic data for all three projects comes from the [BERDL pangenome](../data/kbase-ke-pangenome.md), which aggregates NCBI environmental genome metadata, GTDB taxonomy, eggNOG functional annotations, GapMind pathway predictions, and Bakta gene-product calls at pangenome scale.
