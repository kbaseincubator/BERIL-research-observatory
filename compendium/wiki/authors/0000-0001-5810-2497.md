# Paramvir S. Dehal

## Overview

Paramvir S. Dehal's contributions assembled in this wiki center on
*Acinetobacter baylyi* ADP1, moving from a comprehensive multi-omics resource for
the organism through to a synthesis of what makes a gene essential in it. Three
projects sit behind this body of work, and together they trace a single arc: first
building and validating an integrated ADP1 dataset against the wider BER Data
Lakehouse (BERDL), then mining its growth-fitness measurements for condition-specific
biology, and finally reconciling those experimental signals against metabolic-model
predictions.

A recurring theme across the three projects is that ADP1's phenotype landscape is
better described as continuous and condition-specific than as a set of fixed,
binary categories. Mutant fitness varies smoothly across carbon sources rather than
collapsing onto a single shared sensitivity axis, continuous fitness values predict
essentiality far better than binary thresholds, and the metabolic model's
weaknesses cluster in interpretable places — heavy reliance on gapfilled reactions
and systematic gaps around aromatic degradation. These findings feed three connected
topic areas: integrating ADP1 data with BERDL, carbon-source fitness, and metabolic
model quality.

## Projects

**ADP1 Data Explorer.** This project assembles a user-provided resource for
*Acinetobacter baylyi* ADP1 that integrates six molecular and phenotype data
modalities — TnSeq essentiality, FBA metabolic flux, mutant growth fitness on eight
carbon sources, proteomics across engineered strains, pangenome classification, and
functional annotation — for ADP1 and a set of related *Acinetobacter* genomes.
Beyond inventorying that material, the project tests how well it bridges into BERDL.
Querying BERDL via Spark confirmed strong connectivity across genomes, reactions,
compounds, and pangenome clusters, establishing the resource as a usable bridge for
downstream ADP1 synthesis. The most technically involved result is the pangenome
cluster-ID bridge: because the ADP1 resource and BERDL use different cluster-naming
conventions, a gene-junction mapping reconciles them, achieving complete gene-level
coverage between BERDL pangenome clusters and ADP1-style cluster IDs. The same
analyses surface where data and model diverge — urea and quinate behave as outlier
conditions in the fitness data, growth predictions lean heavily on gapfilled
reactions, and the genes where FBA and TnSeq disagree on essentiality stand out as
refinement targets — motivating a dedicated look at urea-specific metabolism.

**ADP1 Deletion Phenotypes.** This project takes the carbon-source fitness thread
further, characterizing how ADP1 deletion phenotypes are distributed across eight
carbon sources. Rather than one shared growth-sensitivity axis, the carbon assays
provide multiple largely independent phenotypic dimensions, with low pairwise
correlations between conditions indicating that each carbon source imposes its own
set of gene requirements. The eight conditions partition into demanding, moderate,
and robust tiers according to the fraction of genes showing growth defects. The
dominant pattern is continuous, and the one clear discrete exception is a small
quinate-degradation module whose genes show extreme quinate-specific defects and
near-zero effects elsewhere. This grounds the broader claim that ADP1
condition-dependent essentiality should be modeled as a continuous phenotype
landscape, with quinate degradation as the discrete exception, and that expanding
phenotyping beyond eight carbon sources could test whether the number of independent
dimensions grows with condition coverage.

**ADP1 Triple Essentiality.** This project synthesizes essentiality across
orthogonal lines of evidence — flux-balance analysis, TnSeq fitness, and proteomics.
Its central methodological finding is that continuous fitness values outperform
binary essentiality fractions for predicting essentiality, and that proteomics is a
strong, comparably performing signal in ROC analysis. The FBA model's binary
classification, by contrast, does not predict which TnSeq-dispensable genes carry
measurable growth defects, and aromatic-degradation genes are sharply enriched among
FBA-discordant genes, pointing to systematic model gaps. These results lead to the
recommendation that ADP1 essentiality synthesis prioritize continuous fitness and
orthogonal evidence over binary thresholds alone, and that FBA media definitions be
refined with trace aromatic compounds and retested against the aromatic-degradation
discordance.

## Topics

This author's contributions sit across three connected topic areas:

- [Adp1 Data Integration](../topics/adp1-data-integration.md) — the multi-omics
  inventory, the BERDL connectivity finding, the database-bridge claim, and the
  pangenome cluster-ID bridge.
- [Adp1 Carbon Fitness](../topics/adp1-carbon-fitness.md) — the condition-specific

  the quinate exception, the continuous-phenotype claim, and the urea deep-dive and
  expanded-carbon-panel opportunities.
- [Adp1 Model Quality](../topics/adp1-model-quality.md) — the gapfilling caveat, the
  FBA growth-prediction caveat, the continuous-fitness predictor finding, the
