# Costly Dispensable Genes

This project asked a fundamental question about microbial genomes: what do genes look like when they impose a metabolic burden but don't stick around long enough to become universal across a species? Analyzing 142,190 genes across 43 bacterial species, the team found that costly-but-dispensable genes are overwhelmingly mobile genetic elements—insertion sequences, prophage remnants, and transposases—not degrading metabolic pathways, suggesting that horizontal gene transfer, not slow decay, drives their accumulation and that they're likely caught mid-purge from genomes that don't need them. These genes show all the hallmarks of recent acquisitions: they're poorly annotated, narrowly distributed across organisms, shorter than typical genes, and systematically depleted of core metabolic functions.

## Key findings

- The high singleton fraction of costly+dispensable genes is partly structural because core genes cannot be singletons, and within the dispensable category costly genes are only slightly more likely to be singletons than neutral genes (OR=1.09). *(confidence: medium)*
- The pangenome core/auxiliary classification is binary, so a quantitative measure of the fraction of genomes carrying each gene would provide more resolution than the current dispensability call. *(confidence: medium)*
- Costly+dispensable genes are candidates for ongoing gene loss, having been acquired via HGT but imposing sufficient cost that they will likely be purged from the genome unless they provide a selective advantage in specific environments. *(confidence: medium)*
- Costly+dispensable genes carry the hallmarks of recently acquired DNA, being poorly annotated, taxonomically restricted, enriched in singletons, and shorter than conserved genes. *(confidence: high)*
- Costly+dispensable genes represent the genomic debris of horizontal gene transfer -- insertion sequences, prophage remnants, transposases, and defense systems that impose metabolic cost and are not conserved across the species pangenome. *(confidence: high)*
- This is the first systematic cross-organism characterization of genes that are simultaneously burdensome and not conserved, spanning 142,190 genes across 43 bacteria and adding a pangenome conservation dimension to fitness data. *(confidence: medium)*
- Costly+dispensable genes are poorly characterized, with only 50.8% carrying SEED annotations versus 74.9% for costly+conserved genes and 20.0% carrying KEGG annotations versus 42.7%. *(confidence: high)*
- Costly+dispensable genes are shorter than costly+conserved genes, with a median length of 615 bp versus 765 bp, consistent with IS elements and gene fragments. *(confidence: high)*
- Costly+dispensable genes are significantly depleted in 14 core metabolism SEED categories including Protein Metabolism, Respiration, Carbohydrates, and Amino Acids, which remain maintained in the costly+conserved quadrant. *(confidence: high)*
- Costly+dispensable genes are taxonomically restricted, with 44.5% being orphan genes (versus 13.1%) and a median ortholog breadth of 15 organisms versus 31 for costly+conserved genes. *(confidence: high)*
- Despite being burdensome and non-conserved, 14.1% of costly+dispensable genes have condition-specific phenotypes, far above neutral+dispensable genes (2.7%), indicating these genes are not inert and may persist under specific conditions. *(confidence: medium)*
- Pseudomonas stutzeri RCH2 is an outlier, contributing 21.5% of its genes as costly+dispensable, far above the next organism (B. thetaiotaomicron at 14.0%), suggesting a strain-specific mobile element invasion. *(confidence: medium)*
- The 5,526 costly+dispensable genes are overwhelmingly mobile genetic elements, being 7.45x more likely to carry mobile element keywords and 11.7x enriched in the SEED Phage/Transposon/Plasmid category versus costly+conserved genes. *(confidence: high)*
- ENIGMA CORAL community composition data could be used to test the Black Queen Hypothesis by checking whether organisms with more costly+dispensable genes co-occur with organisms that provide the lost functions. *(confidence: low)*
- Genomic context analysis could determine whether the Pseudomonas stutzeri RCH2 outlier reflects a recent phage invasion, IS element expansion, or genomic island acquisition. *(confidence: low)*
