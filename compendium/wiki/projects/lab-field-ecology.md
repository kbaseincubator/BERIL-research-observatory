# Lab Field Ecology

This project tested whether lab-measured metal tolerance in the Fitness Browser predicts field microbial community composition at uranium-contaminated Oak Ridge groundwater sites. While aggregate lab fitness scores did not significantly predict field abundance ratios, genus-level correlations with uranium concentration revealed actionable ecological indicators: Caulobacter and Sphingomonas are depleted at high-uranium sites, while Herbaspirillum and Bacteroides become more abundant—providing the first direct link between Fitness Browser laboratory data and ENIGMA CORAL field ecology across a natural geochemical gradient.

## Key findings

- 16S amplicon data resolves only to genus level, so species- or strain-level matching of field taxa to specific Fitness Browser lab organisms is not possible. *(confidence: high)*
- Genus-uranium correlations do not control for confounding geochemistry such as pH, dissolved oxygen, and carbon sources, so uranium's independent role on community structure remains uncertain. *(confidence: medium)*
- The aggregate metal tolerance score is crude, and condition-specific fitness scores such as uranium-only metrics would be more informative for testing whether lab metal tolerance predicts field abundance. *(confidence: medium)*
- Genus-level uranium correlations provide actionable indicators: Caulobacter and Sphingomonas are sensitive indicators of uranium contamination, while Herbaspirillum and Bacteroides may be tolerant colonizers. *(confidence: medium)*
- Lab metal tolerance does not simply predict field abundance because field niches are multidimensional, communities involve interactions, 16S resolves only to genus, and geochemistry snapshots miss temporal dynamics. *(confidence: high)*
- This is the first study to directly link Fitness Browser lab fitness data with ENIGMA CORAL field community composition across a geochemical gradient, and it demonstrates the previously undocumented CORAL database holds analysis-ready ecological data. *(confidence: high)*
- Consistent with Rhodanobacter strains acquiring heavy metal resistance via horizontal gene transfer at Oak Ridge, metal resistance genes are enriched in the accessory genome rather than the core. *(confidence: medium)*
- Despite being ENIGMA model organisms, both Desulfovibrio and Pseudomonas show no correlation between genus abundance and site uranium concentration. *(confidence: high)*
- Desulfovibrio, the primary ENIGMA model organism, is detected at only 34% of Oak Ridge sites and at very low abundance, making it an unreliable subject for correlation analysis. *(confidence: high)*
- Oak Ridge sites split at the median uranium concentration show distinct community compositions, with rare-biosphere taxa and subsurface specialists becoming more prominent at contaminated sites. *(confidence: high)*
- Of 11 FB genera with sufficient prevalence, 5 show significant correlations with uranium after BH-FDR correction, with Herbaspirillum and Bacteroides increasing and Caulobacter, Sphingomonas, and Pedobacter decreasing at high-uranium sites. *(confidence: high)*
- Of 26 unique genera represented in the Fitness Browser, 14 are detected in Oak Ridge groundwater communities via 16S amplicon sequencing, with Sphingomonas, Pseudomonas, and Caulobacter the most prevalent. *(confidence: high)*
- The correlation between lab-derived metal tolerance score and the high-uranium/low-uranium field abundance ratio is suggestive but not significant (Spearman rho=0.503, p=0.095, n=12 genera), with the trend in the predicted direction but limited statistical power. *(confidence: medium)*
- Adding Rhodanobacter, which dominates contaminated Oak Ridge wells but is absent from the Fitness Browser, to the Fitness Browser would be a high-impact target for linking lab fitness to field ecology. *(confidence: medium)*
- Using metagenomic data instead of 16S amplicons could match Fitness Browser organisms at species or strain level, overcoming the genus-resolution limitation of this analysis. *(confidence: medium)*
