# Clay Confined Subsurface

This project genome-sequenced cultured isolates from clay-confined deep-subsurface environments (primarily the Mont Terri Opalinus formation) to test whether subsurface microbes exhibit the biosynthetic self-sufficiency and anaerobic metabolic toolkit hypothesized in the literature. The study found that cultured clay-confined genomes carry the porewater-dominant sulfate-reduction signature described by Bagnoud, not the rock-attached iron-reduction signature—a direct diagnostic that cultivation captures only the porewater fraction. More importantly, after controlling for phylogeny, dissimilatory sulfate reduction emerged as the only truly depth-specific metabolic signal; the broader "anaerobic toolkit" signal was largely driven by sampling bias toward Bacillota lineages that carry these markers across habitats.

## Key findings

- All conclusions apply only to cultivable porewater-cultured deep-clay isolates rather than the full Mont Terri or bentonite community, since CPR/DPANN episymbionts and rock-attached Geobacter/Geothrix lineages are essentially absent. *(confidence: high)*
- The 18-pathway GapMind amino-acid universe saturates near 18 for most cultivable bacteria, limiting the resolving power of the self-sufficiency metric at the upper end. *(confidence: medium)*
- Within-phylum control reveals the toolkit signal is largely phylogenetic: Wood-Ljungdahl and [NiFe]-hydrogenase track the Bacillota_B lineage rather than deep-clay habitat, since these markers are carried at high background rates even in soil Bacillota_B. *(confidence: high)*
- Because all eight Opalinus genomes trace to BRC-3 or BIC-A1 boreholes, the cohort's marker profile provides a direct, quantitative diagnostic that BERDL captures the porewater fraction rather than the rock-attached fraction. *(confidence: high)*
- The negative self-sufficiency result reflects that BERDL's cultured cohort excludes the extreme self-sufficient lineages (e.g. Ca. Desulforudis audaxviator) the literature highlights, which are characteristically uncultivated and recovered as MAGs or single-cell genomes. *(confidence: medium)*
- Anchor_deep genera include exactly the recurrent Mont Terri lineages Bagnoud identified across seven boreholes, including Desulfosporosinus and BRH-c8a (Peptococcaceae c8a), providing cross-platform validation of indigeneity. *(confidence: medium)*
- At the cohort level, deep-confined clay isolates jointly carry the Wood-Ljungdahl + group 1 [NiFe]-hydrogenase + dissimilatory sulfate-reduction toolkit at much higher rates than soil baseline or shallow-clay cohorts (mean toolkit score 1.89 vs 0.39 vs 0.03 of 3 modules). *(confidence: high)*
- BERDL's cultured clay-confined deep-subsurface genomes carry the Bagnoud Mont Terri porewater signature (sulfate-reduction-rich), not the Mitzscherling rock-attached signature (iron-reduction-rich). *(confidence: high)*
- Biosynthetic self-sufficiency does not generalize to BERDL's cultured cohort: deep-clay GapMind amino-acid pathway completeness is comparable to or slightly below the soil baseline (CheckM-filtered mean 15.5/18 vs 17.1/18, p=0.009, Cohen's d=-0.84). *(confidence: high)*
- Deep clay-confined isolates are dominated by sulfate-reduction-only marker profiles while shallow-clay isolates are dominated by iron-reduction-only profiles, a mirror-image contrast (Fisher OR=infinity, p=2x10^-4 for SR_complete). *(confidence: high)*
- Only dissimilatory sulfate reduction (dsrAB-aprAB-sat) survives within-phylum control, being enriched in 5/5 deep Bacillota_B isolates versus 4/19 soil-baseline Bacillota_B isolates (OR=infinity, p=0.003, p_BH=0.04). *(confidence: high)*
- Sulfate-reduction marker enrichment in the clay-confined cohort is overwhelming relative to the Mitzscherling rock-attached null distribution (binomial p = 4.0x10^-12). *(confidence: high)*
- Ingesting deep-subsurface MAGs from Mont Terri, Olkiluoto, MX-80 bentonite, and Oak Ridge and re-running the H1/H2/H3 framework would test whether the self-sufficiency signal emerges once rock-attached and uncultivated lineages are present. *(confidence: medium)*
- The SR/IR marker-rate cultivation-bias diagnostic can be rapidly applied to other subsurface cohorts (granite-hosted Olkiluoto, basalt-hosted Oak Ridge, salt-cavern) to quantify porewater bias across BERDL or future ingests. *(confidence: medium)*
