# Ecotype Analysis

This project investigated whether environmental factors or phylogenetic ancestry better shape bacterial gene content within species, analyzing 172 bacterial species with sufficient environmental and evolutionary data. The analysis revealed that phylogenetic relatedness is a stronger predictor of bacterial gene similarity than environmental similarity in most species (60.5%), suggesting that vertical inheritance through evolutionary history dominates over adaptation to local ecological conditions. These findings indicate that while environmental pressures do influence some bacterial populations, evolutionary constraints and clonal ancestry are the primary drivers of genome-wide variation in most cases.

## Key findings

- Separating environmental and host-associated bacteria did not reveal a stronger environment effect in this analysis. *(confidence: high)*
- Environmental adaptation may be easier to detect in specific functional gene subsets than in whole-genome gene-content similarity. *(confidence: medium)*
- Across species with sufficient data, phylogenetic relatedness generally predicts bacterial gene-content similarity more strongly than environmental similarity. *(confidence: high)*
