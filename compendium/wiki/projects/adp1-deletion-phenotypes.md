# Project: Adp1 Deletion Phenotypes

## Introduction

The ADP1 deletion-phenotypes project supplies the core carbon-source fitness interpretation. It models condition-dependent essentiality as a mostly continuous phenotype landscape and identifies quinate degradation as the main discrete exception [stmt:adp1-deletion-continuum-claim; adp1_deletion_phenotypes] [stmt:adp1-deletion-quinate-module-finding; adp1_deletion_phenotypes].

## Synthesis

The project's evidence has two main pieces. Eight carbon sources separate into demanding, moderate, and robust growth-defect tiers, and the carbon-source assays provide multiple independent phenotypic dimensions rather than one shared growth-sensitivity axis [stmt:adp1-deletion-carbon-tier-finding; adp1_deletion_phenotypes] [stmt:adp1-deletion-condition-independence-finding; adp1_deletion_phenotypes].

The quinate module gives the project its most explicit exception. Quinate degradation should be tracked separately because it is the named discrete exception to the otherwise continuous ADP1 carbon-fitness landscape [stmt:adp1-deletion-quinate-module-finding; adp1_deletion_phenotypes].

The project's follow-up is to expand ADP1 deletion phenotyping beyond eight carbon sources and test whether the observed independent dimensions increase with condition coverage [stmt:adp1-deletion-expand-carbon-panel-opportunity; adp1_deletion_phenotypes].
