## ε-Lactone hydrolase (EC 3.1.1.83) literature — biotech pivot

Searched: 2026-05-22 (focused agent run on cold-active / psychrophilic ε-lactone hydrolases and EC 3.1.1.83 industrial applications). Sources: PubMed, Google Scholar, bioRxiv (no bioRxiv hits on cold-active lactone hydrolases — confirms published gap).

### Prototype enzyme (mesophilic MlhB)

21. **van der Vlugt-Bergmans CJM, van der Werf MJ. 2001.** *Genetic and biochemical characterization of a novel monoterpene ε-lactone hydrolase from Rhodococcus erythropolis DCL14.* Appl Environ Microbiol 67(2):733–41. PMID: [11157238](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11157238/). Founding paper for MlhB; T_opt ≈ 30 °C, pH 9.5, broad-substrate across 4-/5-/6-/7-methyl-2-oxo-oxepanones.
22. **van der Werf MJ, Boot AM. 2000.** *Metabolism of carveol and dihydrocarveol in Rhodococcus erythropolis DCL14.* Microbiology 146(5):1129–41. PMID: [10832640](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10832640/). Full BVMO + ε-lactone-hydrolase pathway for carveol/dihydrocarveol with stereospecific ω-hydroxy-acid product.
23. **van der Werf MJ. 2000.** *Purification and characterization of a Baeyer-Villiger mono-oxygenase from Rhodococcus erythropolis DCL14 involved in three different monocyclic monoterpene degradation pathways.* Biochem J 347(Pt 3):693–701. PMID: [10769172](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10769172/). Characterizes the BVMO that generates the ε-lactone substrate for MlhB.

### Industrial applications

24. **Shimizu S, Kataoka M, Honda K, Sakamoto K. 2001.** *Lactone-ring-cleaving enzymes of microorganisms: their diversity and applications.* J Biotechnol 92(2):187–94. PMID: [11640988](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11640988/). Canonical review: Fusarium oxysporum D/L-pantolactone resolution (Fuji commercial process, vitamin B5), Agrobacterium aromatic lactonase, Acinetobacter dihydrocoumarin hydrolase.
25. **Jin L, Liu X, Wang T, et al. 2023.** *Multi-Enzymatic Cascade for Efficient Deracemization of dl-Pantolactone into d-Pantolactone.* Molecules 28(14):5308. PMID: [37513182](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37513182/). 1.25 M dl-pantolactone → D-pantolactone, 98.6% ee, 107 g/L/d productivity in 3-enzyme E. coli cascade — industrial benchmark.
26. **Yu X, Tu T, Luo H, et al. 2020.** *Biochemical Characterization and Mutational Analysis of a Lactone Hydrolase from Phialophora americana (ZHD607).* J Agric Food Chem 68(2):545–52. PMID: [31760747](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31760747/). Zearalenone mycotoxin detoxification; all published variants lose activity above 55 °C (no cold-active counterpart).
27. **Li JK, Qu G, Li X, et al. 2022.** *Rational enzyme design for enabling biocatalytic Baldwin cyclization and asymmetric synthesis of chiral heterocycles.* Nat Commun 13(1):7813. PMID: [36535947](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36535947/). Re-LEH re-engineered for Baldwin cyclizations (>99% conv., 99:1 e.r.) — expands the LEH industrial scope to chiral N-/O-heterocycle synthesis.
28. **Ferrandi EE, Marchesi C, Annovazzi C, Riva S, Monti D, et al. 2015.** *Efficient Epoxide Hydrolase Catalyzed Resolutions of (+)- and (−)-cis/trans-Limonene Oxides.* ChemCatChem 7(23):3805–11. DOI: 10.1002/cctc.201500608. Quantifies the substrate window of native LEHs and motivates engineered/new variants.

### Metagenomic mining precedents (all hot/mesophilic, none cold)

29. **Ferrandi EE, Sayer C, Isupov MN, et al. 2015.** *Discovery and characterization of thermophilic limonene-1,2-epoxide hydrolases from hot spring metagenomic libraries.* FEBS J 282(15):2879–94. PMID: [26032250](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26032250/). Same paper as ref 5 above (re-listed here for thematic grouping). Hot-spring metagenomic LEH discovery; cold counterpart absent.
30. **Stojanovski G, Dobrijevic D, Hailes HC, Ward JM. 2020.** *Identification and catalytic properties of new epoxide hydrolases from the genomic data of soil bacteria.* Enzyme Microb Technol 139:109592. PMID: [32732040](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32732040/). 29 putative EHs sequence-mined from soil genomes; 2 active LEHs (pQR1980, pQR1982). Closest published methodology for the proposed cold-peat mining; all isolates mesophilic.
31. **Robinson A, Wu PS, Harrop SJ, et al. 2004.** *Integron-associated mobile gene cassettes code for folded proteins: structure of Bal32a, a new α+β barrel from contaminated soil.* J Mol Biol 346(4):1129–43. PMID: [15713477](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15713477/). Direct cassette mining from environmental DNA yields LEH-superfamily Bal32a — methodological precedent for habitat-targeted cassette mining including cold habitats.

### Engineering — directionally upward in temperature only

32. **Wijma HJ, Floor RJ, Jekel PA, Baker D, Marrink SJ, Janssen DB. 2014.** *Computationally designed libraries of fluorescent proteins evaluated by preservative-induced melting (FRESCO on LEH).* Protein Eng Des Sel 27(2):49–58. PMID: [24402331](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24402331/). Pushes Re-LEH T_m from 50 → 85 °C with 10–12 stabilizing mutations and preserved regioselectivity. No reciprocal cold-adaptation literature for LEH exists — directionally confirms the cold-active gap.
