Direction is reversed from the literature prior

Acidic peats were expected to be the novel-lineage reservoir. They aren't. Most parsimonious explanation: GTDB culture-mining bias — Acidobacteriota have been intensively cultured, so acidic-soil GTDB representation is mature; alkaline / neutral soils are under-mined.

Module-level enrichment misleads — per-KO dissection corrects it

The M00322 (limonene degradation) enrichment looked like a striking finding. Per-step dissection of which KOs each MAG actually carries:

Pathway classCountWhat it means biologically
GENERIC_ONLY43Carry only K00128 (NAD aldehyde dehydrogenase, housekeeping). False positive. Includes all 14 cyanobacterial MAGs.
LATE_ONLY15Carry K14731 (ε-lactone hydrolase). Process downstream lactones, can't initiate. Concentrated in soil Acidobacteriota.
PARTIAL2Entry + late, no diol step (both Bradyrhizobium). Need community partner.
OTHER / ENTRY_ONLY2Single Rubrivivax / 13-2-20CM-66-19 fragments.
FULL pathway1Mycobacterium MAG — but Mycobacterium is the established cultured limonene biocatalyst (van Beilen 2005).
Methodological takeaway

Module-level enrichment should be cross-checked with per-KO step assignment before drawing biology. Broad-substrate housekeeping enzymes (e.g., aldehyde dehydrogenase shared by dozens of pathways) can drive spurious module enrichment by themselves.

The actionable signal: cold-active ε-lactone hydrolases

15 sub-Arctic palsa-peat Acidobacteriota MAGs carry K14731 (EC 3.1.1.83) that's absent from cultured pangenome accessory of their genus.

The published gap

Targeted PubMed + bioRxiv + Scholar search (May 2026) returned zero hits coupling "cold-active" or "psychrophilic" with "lactone hydrolase" or EC 3.1.1.83.

  • Prototype MlhB (R. erythropolis DCL14) is mesophilic (T_opt ≈ 30°C, Van der Vlugt-Bergmans 2001)
  • Metagenomic LEH mining: hot springs only (Ferrandi 2015) or mesophilic soil (Stojanovski 2020)
  • Engineering effort directionally upward: Re-LEH from T_m 50 → 85°C (Wijma 2014, FRESCO)

Industrial demand

  • D-pantolactone resolution → vitamin B5 (Fuji commercial; Jin 2023, 107 g/L/d benchmark)
  • Baeyer-Villiger product workup in monoterpene / flavor chemistry
  • Zearalenone mycotoxin detoxification
  • Engineered LEH for chiral N-/O-heterocycle synthesis (Li 2022 Nat Commun)
  • ε-Caprolactone polymer chemistry — white space at low temp

Captured as a project-level pitfall in docs/pitfalls.md for future BERDL projects using per-gene NMDC tables.

Summary & what's next

Findings as one-liners

H1Supported Lineage novelty rises with soil pH (slope = +0.079, p = 9e-47) — opposite the literature prior. Likely culture-mining bias.
H2Supported All 3 habitats show enriched KEGG modules; partly housekeeping (per-KO check needed).
H3Partial Soil pH 5.4% R² ✓; horizon 2.2% R² ✗ at strict threshold.
H4Supported Per-MAG: median 343 field-only KOs/MAG, 29 modules enriched.
BioNovel Cold-active ε-lactone hydrolase candidates from 15 sub-Arctic palsa-peat MAGs.

Future directions

  • JupyterHub-scale pfam coverage audit — does Pfam fill the soil-genera gap that KEGG misses?
  • K14731 sequence-first mining — express from 15 Toolik MAGs in E. coli at 15°C; screen on limonene-1,2-epoxide / pantolactone / ε-caprolactone / zearalenone.
  • NEON Biorepository sample-back — enrichment at 5–10°C, pH 5, on lactone substrates.
  • Direct H1 mechanism test — independent GTDB-representation-by-pH analysis to distinguish culture-bias from biology.
