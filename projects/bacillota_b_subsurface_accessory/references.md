## Synthesis (for Literature Context in REPORT.md)

The Bacillota_B phylum (formerly Firmicutes Class II per Schoch et al. 2020 NCBI taxonomy reorganization, comprising Desulfotomaculales, Desulfitobacteriales, Peptococcaceae, Heliobacteriaceae and other strict-anaerobe spore-forming Firmicutes) is the cultivable counterpart to the uncultivable Patescibacteria/CPR superphylum at deep-subsurface sites (Beaver & Neufeld 2024). At Mont Terri Opalinus Clay, Bagnoud et al. (2016) reported three MAGs recurring across seven independent boreholes, including the **Peptococcaceae c8a / BRH-c8a** lineage that appears in our anchor cohort. Vandieken et al. (2017) described three new *Desulfosporosinus* species from Baltic Sea subsurface sediments with H₂-driven sulfate/thiosulfate/sulfite/DMSO/S⁰ reduction and broad respiratory versatility. Hilpmann et al. (2023) demonstrated U(VI) reduction in *Desulfosporosinus hippei*, relevant for nuclear-waste-repository microbiology. Becraft et al. (2021) showed that *Ca.* Desulforudis audaxviator — a cultivable-relative subsurface lineage that is **literally in our anchor cohort** (genome `GB_GCA_020725505.1` from a Russian Beyelii Yar borehole) — exhibits evolutionary stasis across continents, with a self-sufficient genome encoding full N-fixation, amino-acid biosynthesis, and C-fixation. Beller et al. (2012) showed the Hanford 100H *Pelosinus* HCF1 carries 2 [NiFe] + 4 [FeFe] hydrogenases plus N-oxide reductase complement and methylmalonyl-CoA pathway — a direct comparative-genomics template for subsurface-adapted Firmicutes. Tian et al. (2020) reported that ORFRC Patescibacteria/CPR have small (~1 Mbp), streamlined genomes — the "small is mighty" thesis — but explicitly contrast this with non-CPR phyla which retain larger genomes. The companion `clay_confined_subsurface` project (PR #231) found within-Bacillota_B sulfate-reduction enrichment (5/5 deep vs 4/19 baseline, p_BH=0.04) as the only marker that survived phylum control. The clay project's IR-side analysis used misidentified KOs (K07811, K17324, K17323 = TMAO reductase, glycerol ABC, glycerol permease — not iron reduction) and is corrected here.
