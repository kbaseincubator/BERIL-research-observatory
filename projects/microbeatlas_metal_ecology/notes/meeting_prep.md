## H6: Mine + Superfund exposure effect on priority OTUs

**Groups:**
- **(a) Neither** – no USGS MRDS mine within radius, no EPA NPL Superfund site

### Key numbers
- **0/8** OTUs show statistically significant differences (MW p < 0.05) between group (d) and (a).
- **2/8** OTUs have higher median relative abundance under compound exposure (FC > 1).
- Largest fold-change: **Staphylococcus A** (97_9189) — FC = 7.17, p = n/a.

### Interpretation
OTUs with FC > 1 are enriched in the compound contamination scenario (mine + Superfund), suggesting
tolerance to or association with co-occurring metal and industrial pollution. OTUs with FC < 1 are
suppressed under compound exposure, consistent with sensitivity to cumulative stress. The Mann-Whitney
test uses a random sample of ≤1000 observations per OTU drawn from the full atlas.

## H2: Peatland OTU richness on ultramafic vs. other rock

**Ultramafic / high-Cu definition:** GeoROC rock type contains a keyword in
{PERIDOTITE, DUNITE, HARZBURGITE, LHERZOLITE, WEHRLITE, KOMATIITE, SERPENTINITE}
**or** georoc_Cu_ppm > 100.

| Rock class | n samples | Median OTU richness |
|---|---|---|
| Other rock | 323 | 387 |
| Ultramafic / high-Cu | 765 | 153 |

- 70.3% of peatland samples with GeoROC data sit on ultramafic or anomalously Cu-rich substrate.
- Mann-Whitney p = **2.02e-85** (ultramafic vs. other).

**Figure:** `figures/fig_h2_peatland_rock.png`

### Interpretation
Peatlands on ultramafic/high-Cu rock have significantly **lower** OTU richness than those on other substrates (p = 2.02e-85). This suppressed richness suggests that metal toxicity reduces diversity even under natural (geogenic) exposure conditions.
