# Direction 1: The pH-Toxicity Interaction (Bioavailability) — 2026-04-04

## Hypothesis
Heavy metals are known to be highly soluble and bioavailable at lower pH levels. 
We hypothesize that pH acts as an **amplifier** of toxicity. A mine located in an acidic environment 
should exhibit a much steeper and more lethal suppression of OTU richness than a mine located in an alkaline environment.

## Multiple Linear Regression (Interaction Model)
`OTU_Richness ~ log_Distance_centered * pH_centered + log_Depth`

## Key Findings
* **The Main Distance Effect:** A positive Distance β confirms the H1 baseline (as you move further from the mine, richness goes up).
* **The Interaction Term (Dist * pH):** A significant interaction term indicates that the slope of the toxicity effect changes depending on the soil pH.
    * If the Interaction β is **positive**, it means that as pH increases (becomes more alkaline), the "recovery" slope becomes steeper, or conversely, acidic conditions flatten the recovery because the entire near-mine zone is devastated.
