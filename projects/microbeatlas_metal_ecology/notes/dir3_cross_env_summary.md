# Direction 3: Cross-Environment Generalization — 2026-04-04

## Hypothesis
The mine-proximity richness suppression observed in desert (H1) is driven by metal
toxicity, which should operate across all terrestrial environments. If universal,
the near-vs-mid fold-change should be positive and significant across soil, forest,
and agricultural environments too.  If environment-specific, organic matter/moisture
buffering may be protecting communities from metal stress.
