OTU richness than comparable samples on other major plates, reflecting the extreme

## Interpretation
Nazca Plate desert samples have a median OTU richness of 44 —
8.5× lower than other plates (MW p = 1.70e-40). GeoROC Cu is negatively
correlated with richness across all desert samples (ρ = 0.429, p = 3.39e-09),
and Nazca samples have the highest median Cu (47 ppm).
The Nazca coefficient remains significant after controlling for mine distance and GeoROC Cu (OLS p = 4.81e-29), indicating a plate-level signal **beyond** mine proximity and lithogenic Cu — consistent with pervasive diffuse metal enrichment from subduction-driven hydrothermal and aeolian Cu deposition across the Atacama.
