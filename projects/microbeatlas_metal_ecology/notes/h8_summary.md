# H8: Tectonic Boundary Type vs. CWM Metal-Type Diversity — 2026-04-04

## Approach
CWM metal-type diversity was computed for 419 samples drawn equally across
tectonic boundary types (≤150 per type; seq depth ≥ 1,000 reads).
OTU counts were queried from `otu_counts_long` and merged with `otu_pangenome_link.csv`
(12,438 OTUs with GTDB pangenome links). CWM = Σ(rel_abund × n_metal_types) / Σ(rel_abund_annotated).

AMR annotation coverage: 36648/222832 OTU-sample rows (16.4%).

## Results

| Boundary type | n | Median CWM | IQR |
|---|---|---|---|
| Transform | 142 | 1.7293 | 1.461–2.073 |
| Convergent | 142 | 1.8472 | 1.469–2.600 |
| Divergent | 135 | 1.5445 | 1.182–2.037 |


**Kruskal-Wallis**: H = 16.093, p = 3.20e-04

## Dunn Post-hoc (Bonferroni)

|---|---|

## Interpretation
CWM metal-type diversity differs significantly across tectonic boundary types (KW p = 3.20e-04). Convergent boundaries show the highest median CWM (1.8472), consistent with elevated geogenic metal flux at convergent margins (subduction-related Cu/Cr/Ni enrichment). Post-hoc Dunn tests identify the significant pairs.
