# H1: Desert Mine Proximity vs. OTU Richness — 2026-04-04

## Approach
All desert samples (`Env_Level_1 = 'desert'`) with USGS MRDS mine-distance data and
OTU richness > 0 (seq depth ≥ 1,000) were extracted from the atlas (n = 1355).
Samples were binned by distance to nearest mine deposit: Near (<5 km), Mid (5–25 km),
Far (>25 km). OTU richness = `n_genes_by_counts` from `sample_metadata`.

## Interpretation
OTU richness in desert samples **increases** with distance from mines (Spearman ρ = 0.148, p = 4.68e-08). The Kruskal-Wallis test confirms richness differs significantly across distance bins (H = 147.11, p = 7.43e-34). The OLS model shows β(log_dist) = 133.07 (p = 1.38e-09) after controlling for sequencing depth, confirming a robust distance–richness gradient. Nearby mining activity is associated with reduced OTU richness, consistent with metal contamination suppressing community diversity in arid soils.
