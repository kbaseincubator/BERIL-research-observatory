# Refined Ecological Hypotheses

_Genera with ≥1 candidate OTU (top-10% niche breadth × metal diversity, or nitrifier positive control)._

## *Pseudomonas* (Rep. OTU: `97_39338`, n=56 candidates)

**Condition:** Cu-contaminated soils (e.g., vineyard or smelter-impacted sites).

**Prediction:** The relative abundance of *Pseudomonas* OTU `97_39338` should increase by ≥2-fold compared to metal-free controls after 7–14 days of incubation. This prediction is grounded in the genus's mean niche breadth (Levins' B = 0.820) and metal-type diversity (2.12 types), which jointly suggest broad environmental tolerance and multi-metal resistance capacity.

**Mechanism:** *Pseudomonas* carries `copA / pcoABCD` resistance determinants. Under Cu stress, susceptible competitors experience growth inhibition, creating a selective vacuum that *Pseudomonas* — protected by its resistance operon — is positioned to fill.

**Falsification:** If *Pseudomonas* does not increase, the resistance genes may be on accessory elements absent from the soil ecotype, or the stress concentration may exceed the MIC of its specific alleles.
