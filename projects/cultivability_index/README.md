# Metabolic Self-Sufficiency Index — Predicting Cultivability from Pangenome Pathway Completeness

## Research Question
Can metabolic self-sufficiency, measured as the completeness of essential biosynthesis and energy pathways across a genome's annotated metabolic capability, predict which uncultured species in the BERDL pangenome are most likely to be cultivable in pure culture?

## Status
Exploration — research plan not yet written.

## Overview
The cultured fraction of the bacterial pangenome is small (<1% in most environments) and ecologically biased — clay_confined_subsurface and oak_ridge_cultivation_gap demonstrated that cultured isolates misrepresent native community composition. A practical question for experimentalists is which uncultured genomes are tractable targets for cultivation. This project tests whether per-genome metabolic self-sufficiency (the share of essential amino acid, vitamin/cofactor, and central-carbon biosynthesis pathways with complete GapMind annotations) predicts cultured vs uncultured status across the BERDL pangenome, calibrated against GTDB and MGnify cultured/MAG metadata. The deliverable is a ranked "most cultivable MAGs" candidate list with confidence scores that bench microbiologists can use to prioritize isolation attempts.
