# Speaker notes — substory `S4`

**Substory punchline:** Peptidoglycan synthesis downshifts with targeted Pal-Tol induction — a coordinated wall remodel.
**Throughline:** A single envelope catastrophe — lipid A loss — triggers five partially independent adaptive layers (Fur-released transport, ChvI two-phase signaling, constitutive sphingolipid substitution, peptidoglycan remodeling, and cross-species structural unavailability) that together explain how *and why* *Caulobacter* alone tolerates the deletion.
**Tier:** STRONG
**Mode:** talk-30

---

## position 0 — section_divider — `Peptidoglycan synthesis downshifts with targeted Pal-Tol induction — a coordinated wall remodel.`

Three adaptive layers have now been established. Fur derepression releases the transport machinery that carries Layer 1's fitness signal. ChvI runs the two-phase cascade that schedules the envelope rebuild over time. A constitutive sphingolipid pool, moved by maintained Lpt machinery, provides the outer-membrane lipid substitute. Layer 4 asks whether this adaptive program reaches the cell wall — whether the peptidoglycan layer itself reorganizes when the outer membrane's lipid chemistry changes so dramatically.

The short answer is yes, and the direction of that reorganization matters. The dominant signal is not induction but shutdown: the normal basal machinery of cell division and elongation — penicillin-binding proteins, murein biosynthesis enzymes, endopeptidases — is broadly suppressed in the Δlpxc rescued strain. Against that backdrop of reduced synthesis turnover, a small number of specific activities are induced, most notably SdpA, a soluble lytic murein transglycosylase, and Pal, the peptidoglycan-anchored lipoprotein of the Tol-Pal envelope-integrity complex.

Pal is the mechanistic centerpiece of this layer because it emerges independently from two analyses that never shared a gene set. The NB03 ChvI late-cohort partition, which asked how the envelope-stress regulator organizes its transcriptional program over time, flagged Pal as a top late-cohort hit. The NB05 PG-remodeling test, which asked prospectively whether a pre-curated set of 53 PG-enzyme loci responded to lipid A loss, flagged the same gene with concordant transcript and protein evidence. Cross-notebook convergence built on independent gene sets and independent thresholds is the strongest signal this dataset can produce.

The mechanistic interpretation of Pal induction is grounded in Tan and Chng 2025, which reframed the Tol-Pal complex's primary function as retrograde phospholipid transport for outer-membrane lipid homeostasis — precisely the relevant function when the outer leaflet's lipid composition has been fundamentally altered by Δlpxc. Two caveats thread through all three content slides that follow: the protein-level inductions are single-replicate, and the Pal mechanistic interpretation derives from an external E. coli study, not from direct Caulobacter measurement.

## position 1 — methods_summary — `H4 PG-remodeling test: pre-curated gene set locked before differential expression`

The H4 test is non-circular by design. We assembled 53 PG-remodeling loci from GO annotation before any differential expression analysis ran — penicillin-binding proteins across class A, B, and non-classical families; murein biosynthesis enzymes including MurD and the lipid-II flippase MviN/MurJ; M23-family endopeptidases; amidases and hydrolases; transglycosylases; and anchoring factors including Tol-Pal complex members. The gene set was locked before the DE results were examined. The H4 test therefore answers the prospective question "do these pre-specified PG genes respond to lipid A loss?" rather than the retrospective "did DE hits happen to include PG genes?" That is the non-circularity the audience should register.

The key contrast is strain 4599 — Δfur Δsspb Δlpxc, lipid A absent — versus strain 4584 — Δfur Δsspb, lipid A intact. This pairing isolates the Δlpxc-specific wall response from the Δfur and Δsspb background effects already present before LpxC was removed. Two evidence modalities were used: transcript significance by edgeR FDR < 0.05 in the 4599-vs-4584 contrast, and protein change magnitude |log2| > 1 in the single-replicate OM proteome, where per-protein FDR cannot be calculated. Twenty-five loci cleared the transcript bar; six cleared the protein bar; after deduplication, 28 unique loci. The H4 pre-registered pass criterion was ≥3 unique loci meeting either threshold — a conservative bar set because PG remodeling was a post-hoc hypothesis, motivated by SdpA appearing at +4.8 log2 protein in the NB00 orientation scoping. At 28 observed loci, the bar is exceeded by nearly a factor of ten; the REPORT scorecard records this as PASS strongly.

Two procedural flags. First, the cross-notebook check: NB03's late-ChvI cohort was independently compared against the NB05 PG hits to identify convergent loci; Pal at CCNA_00784 emerged as the only gene satisfying both analyses' independent criteria simultaneously. Second, two loci in the pre-curated set are regex false-positives. CCNA_00565 is a γ-glutamyltranspeptidase involved in glutathione metabolism, not PG synthesis; CCNA_01833 is a glucosylceramidase that matched the "amidase" search string. REPORT.md Limitation L#11 flags both explicitly; neither changes the H4 verdict and no mechanistic claim in Layer 4 rests on either gene.

## position 2 — data_figure — `28 PG-remodeling loci meet H4 threshold: broad downshift with targeted SdpA and Pal induction`

When the heatmap appears, the dominant directional signal is immediately legible: the PG-remodeling landscape of the Δlpxc rescue strain is driven by downregulation, with a small number of targeted inductions standing out against a suppressed background.

Twenty of the 28 loci meeting the H4 threshold are DOWN. The specific identities matter for delivery. FtsI, the penicillin-binding protein responsible for cell-division-stage PG synthesis, is suppressed. PbpZ declines at −1.08 transcript and PbpC at −1.15 protein, both involved in cell elongation. The D,D-transpeptidase domain protein falls −1.27 log2 at protein. MurD, the murein biosynthesis ligase, and MviN/MurJ, the lipid-II flippase, are both down. The M23-family endopeptidases LdpD, LdpE, and LdpF are down, as are the amidase AmiC and the hydrolase RipA. The single largest protein decline in the set is a membrane-bound transglycosylase at −2.47 log2 protein. Collectively, the cell has suppressed the normal growth-phase repertoire of PG synthesis, modification, and remodeling that accompanies division and elongation.

Against that suppressed backdrop, three specific inductions stand out. SdpA at CCNA_01252 shows the largest change: +4.8 log2 protein, a soluble lytic murein transglycosylase. PleA and PbpX also increase. Most mechanistically interesting is Pal at CCNA_00784: +2.08 transcript (FDR-significant in 4599-vs-4584) AND +2.84 protein — the only locus in the full H4 hit set showing concordant induction at both transcript and protein modalities. Pal belongs to the Tol-Pal envelope-integrity complex, not the PG synthesis machinery per se, and its dual-modality agreement combined with independent appearance in NB03 places it in a distinct evidentiary category from every other locus in this heatmap.

One framing note to carry in delivery: earlier draft language described this pattern as "coordinated bidirectional reorganization." The current REPORT framing is more accurate — predominantly downshift of synthesis machinery punctuated by specific inductions. The shutdown is the dominant signal; the inductions are targeted exceptions. The mandatory caveat: protein inductions — SdpA at +4.8 and Pal at +2.84 — are from the single-replicate OM proteome. Direction is reliable; magnitudes require replicated proteomics to confirm, planned for summer 2026. The transcript-level Pal induction at +2.08 stands on independent FDR-corrected statistical ground.

## position 3 — claim_evidence — `Pal-Tol induction is consistent with retrograde phospholipid transport for OM lipid homeostasis`

Pal at CCNA_00784 is the interpretive anchor of Layer 4 because two independent analyses converge on it. In NB03, Pal appeared in the late-consequence ChvI cohort — transcript logFC +2.08 — identified by the ChvI regulon partition test, which was designed around the TCS cascade question with no reference to PG remodeling. In NB05, the same gene appeared independently in the pre-curated PG-remodeling hit set with both transcript and protein data concordant. The two analyses used different gene sets, different thresholds, and were developed at different stages of the project. Convergence on the same locus across those independent design choices is the strongest evidentiary signal in this layer.

The mechanistic interpretation rests on Tan and Chng 2025, Nature Communications 16:2293, PMID 40055349. Using engineered E. coli chimeric strains that dissociated Tol-Pal's division-plane enrichment from its OM function, that paper established that the primary role of the Tol-Pal complex is retrograde phospholipid transport for OM lipid homeostasis — not LPS-Pal structural anchoring, as our earlier draft proposed. In the Δlpxc state, the OM outer leaflet has lost its LPS, disrupting lipid asymmetry. Upregulating Pal is consistent with increased demand for retrograde PL transport to restore that balance. The data establish that Pal is up; the retrograde-transport reading is the Tan-Chng 2025 framing applied to our Caulobacter context.

Two qualifiers are mandatory in delivery. The protein induction — Pal at +2.84 log2 — is from the single-replicate OM proteome; direction is reliable, but magnitude and statistical weight require the replicated proteomics planned for summer 2026. The transcript induction at +2.08 (FDR-significant in 4599-vs-4584) stands on independent statistical footing. The mechanistic interpretation also depends on Tan and Chng's E. coli system, not Caulobacter-direct evidence. REPORT Limitation L#5 records this explicitly: the data show Pal up; the mechanism is from the cited paper.

Caulobacter context adds a viability dimension. Yeh and colleagues 2010, Journal of Bacteriology 192:4847, PMID 20693330, established that Caulobacter Tol-Pal is essential for OM integrity and for OM constriction at cell division — unlike E. coli, where the complex is dispensable. A rescued strain completing cell division without an LPS outer leaflet, in an organism where Tol-Pal is essential for that step, faces a viability-critical reason to maintain the complex beyond adaptive advantage alone.

With all four adaptive layers characterized — Fur-released transport relief, ChvI two-phase cascade, constitutive sphingolipid substitution, and coordinated PG remodeling — S5 asks whether the sphingolipid pathway and ChvI signaling that enable this program are structurally available to the other Gram-negatives that have independently managed to lose lipid A, and whether their structural unavailability explains why only Caulobacter tolerates the Δlpxc deletion.
