# Speaker notes — substory `S1`

**Substory punchline:** Fur-released transport relief, not the respiratory arm, carries the Layer-1 fitness signal.
**Throughline:** A single envelope catastrophe — lipid A loss — triggers five partially independent adaptive layers (Fur-released transport, ChvI two-phase signaling, constitutive sphingolipid substitution, peptidoglycan remodeling, and cross-species structural unavailability) that together explain how *and why* *Caulobacter* alone tolerates the deletion.
**Tier:** STRONG
**Mode:** talk-30

---

## position 0 — section_divider — `Fur-released transport relief, not the respiratory arm, carries the Layer-1 fitness signal.`

S1 opens the five-layer mechanism story with the question Zik 2022 raised but did not fully quantify: when *Caulobacter* loses lipid A through the Δlpxc deletion, does Fur derepression reshape envelope transport — and does that relief carry a measurable fitness benefit? Zik and colleagues established, in Cell Reports (PMID 35649364), that both Δfur and anionic sphingolipids are required for rescue, and that "alterations in Fur-regulated processes, rather than iron status per se, underlie the ability to survive when lipid A synthesis is blocked." What remained unresolved is which arm of the Fur regulon is load-bearing.

The Fur regulon in *Caulobacter* controls dozens of TonB-dependent transporters and iron-uptake systems held under Fe-Fur repression in wild type, as characterized by Leaden 2018 (Front. Microbiol.) and da Silva Neto 2009 (Nucleic Acids Res.). The Zik 2022 rescue background also carries Δsspb, which buffers the cbb3/cyd/fix-NOPQ micro-aerobic respiratory operon at the transcript level through the SspB/ClpXP proteolytic adaptor (Bhat 2013). Two arms release when Fur is deleted in the Δsspb background; the question is whether fitness benefit rides on both, or just one.

This substory covers three analyses in sequence: a methods slide establishing the Spearman concordance design and the BERDL Fitness Browser calibration baseline; the NB00 orientation figure delivering a conceptual reframing before formal testing began; and a data table presenting the hypergeometric H2 verdict. The conclusion slide names the Layer-1 finding explicitly — transport arm carries the signal, respiratory arm does not. The planned negative result for the respiratory arm sharpens the story rather than weakening it: it defines precisely what Layer 1 claims, not merely what it is consistent with. This substory is the opening bracket of the throughline's five-layer architecture; the audience needs the honest Layer-1 verdict in hand before Layers 2–5 build on it.

## position 1 — methods_summary — `Methods: Fur-concordance and fitness ranking to define the Layer-1 gene sets`

The Layer-1 analysis runs in two conceptually distinct steps, and the speaker should arm the audience with two numbers before the results slides arrive: Spearman rho=0.315, which defines the concordance that partitions the gene sets, and 33.25%, which is the genome background rate that anchors the hypergeometric baseline.

Step one is gene-set identification. We compared our 4584-vs-4580 contrast — Δfur Δsspb Δrsab versus Δrsab alone — against the 93 differentially expressed genes from Leaden 2018's published Δfur *Caulobacter* RNA-seq (Front. Microbiol., PMC6120978), extracted from their supplementary Table 2. Spearman concordance across those 93 DEGs yielded rho=0.315, p=2.08e-03, 71% sign concordance — confirming Fur derepression as a major driver of the 4584-vs-4580 signal. This concordance produced the partition: Path A genes are concordant_strong — up in both Leaden and our data; Path B genes are SspB-buffered — up in Leaden with logFC values from −5 to −9 but near zero in our Δfur Δsspb data, because SspB deletion prevents ClpXP-mediated degradation of the respiratory chain regulators. The partition rests on the established SspB/ClpXP adaptor mechanism confirmed in *Caulobacter* by Bhat 2013.

Step two is fitness ranking. BERDL's kescience_fitnessbrowser provides 198 RB-TnSeq fitness experiments for *Caulobacter* NA1000 (Price 2018, Nature). Of these, 22 are envelope-stress conditions — vancomycin, polymyxin, SDS, bile — the relevant phenotypic axis for H2. There are zero iron-limitation experiments in the compendium: the iron-axis arm of H2 was descoped to envelope-axis-only at the plan's preflight stage, and all fitness results that follow reflect envelope stress only. Phenotype-bearing is defined as |fitness t| > 4 in at least two of the 22 envelope-stress experiments.

One calibration lesson the speaker should note briefly: the genome-wide background phenotype-bearing rate is 33.25% — K=1311 of n=3943 *Caulobacter* genes. The pre-registered ≥10% threshold sits below that background. A threshold any random gene set passes is uninformative about specific mechanistic importance. The formal H2 verdict uses hypergeometric enrichment over the 33.25% background, not threshold-passing alone — a recalibration surfaced by adversarial review and recorded in REPORT Novel Contribution §7 as a methodological lesson for future fitness-browser projects. Deliver it as: "our pre-registered bar was below the genome rate, so enrichment against background is the operative verdict." Then move to the orientation figure.

## position 2 — data_figure — `NB00: sphingolipid biosynthesis is constitutive across the rescue strain series`

Before formal hypothesis testing began, the NB00 orientation notebook delivered a reframing that changed the Layer-1 question entirely. This heatmap shows the sphingolipid biosynthesis gene locus in log2(CPM+1) across the four strains in the rescue series: wild type (4580), the Δfur Δsspb background (4584), the intermediate Δlpxc strain (4659), and the fully rescued Δlpxc strain (4672). The picture is flat to slightly decreasing — not induced.

This eliminated the simplest version of the Fur story. The initial framing entering analysis was: Fur normally represses sphingolipid biosynthesis, so Δfur derepresses it, giving the cell extra ceramide phosphoglycerate (CPG) to substitute for lipid A. That mechanism predicts the sphingolipid locus going up in 4584 and up further in 4672. The data show the opposite: the pathway is constitutive. The CPG pool is already present in wild type; rescue is not a matter of making more sphingolipid in response to lipid A loss, but of routing an existing pool into a new function.

This observation redirected the analysis to the productive framing. The question shifted from "why does Δfur induce sphingolipid biosynthesis?" to "given that the CPG pool is already standing, what Fur-released components enable its trafficking into the outer membrane?" Uchendu 2026 (bioRxiv, doi:10.1101/2026.04.12.717747) provides the mechanistic hypothesis: homologs of inner-membrane Lpt transport proteins are required for sphingolipid transport in lipid-A-deficient *Caulobacter*. The Path A TBDTs and iron-uptake systems derepressed by Δfur are, on this model, candidate components facilitating CPG routing via the shared Lpt apparatus.

One caveat the speaker must carry: this orientation figure uses CPM normalization from NB00 and is descriptive, not formally DE-tested with FDR correction. The rigorous statistical confirmation of constitutivity — spt logFC −0.64 FDR 0.002, sphk −0.40 FDR 0.02, zero of six biosynthesis genes significantly up — belongs to NB04 in Layer 3. What this slide establishes is the conceptual reframing; the statistical support comes later. Deliver the distinction cleanly: "NB00 showed us the CPG pool is already standing in wild type — the Layer-1 question is what Fur derepression releases to route it into the outer membrane." Transition: with the simplest Fur story ruled out, we turn to the fitness compendium to ask which Fur-released arm carries phenotypic weight under envelope stress.

## position 3 — data_table — `Path A transport genes show marginal enrichment; Path B respiratory set is background-equivalent`

This table delivers the formal H2 verdict: hypergeometric enrichment of the two Fur-released gene sets against the 33.25% *Caulobacter* genome background. The result is strikingly asymmetric, and the speaker's job here is to read both numbers at their actual evidence level — not to promote Path A beyond what p=0.016 warrants, and not to soften the Path B negative.

Path A — the 32 concordant_strong Fur-released TBDTs and iron-uptake systems — shows 17 of 32 genes phenotype-bearing under the 22 envelope-stress experiments: 53.1%, fold enrichment 1.60×, hypergeometric p=0.016. That is marginal but real enrichment above the 33.25% genome background. The per-gene signals within Path A are what make this finding credible: ChvT (CCNA_03108) carries |t|=43.7 under envelope stress — an exceptionally large per-gene effect for a single TonB-dependent transporter. The TBDT cluster CCNA_02910, 00210, 02048, and 00028 shows |t| ranging from 9 to 28, well above background noise. Large individual signals within a marginal ensemble enrichment are consistent with a real transport-layer contribution concentrated in a subset of Path A, not a random artifact of gene-set size.

Path B — the 26 SspB-buffered genes dominated by the cbb3/cyd/fix-NOPQ micro-aerobic respiratory operon — shows 9 of 26 genes phenotype-bearing: 34.6%, fold enrichment 1.04×, p=0.515. Statistically indistinguishable from the 33.25% genome background. The transcript-level buffering by Δsspb on the respiratory chain is real — 53 of 93 Leaden Δfur DEGs are blunted at logFC ~0 in our Δfur Δsspb data despite −5 to −9 logFC values in Leaden — but the fitness data provide no independent evidence that these specific respiratory genes are more critical under envelope stress than any randomly chosen *Caulobacter* gene.

Two delivery caveats. First, Path A enrichment is marginal: p just below 0.05, fold 1.60×, and the pre-registration threshold was miscalibrated below background. Speaker language should be "consistent with a fitness-bearing transport arm," not "transport criticality demonstrated." Second, Path B failure is a clean negative at the ensemble level; individual respiratory chain genes might still matter singly, but the ensemble test cannot detect single-gene contributions within a background-equivalent set. Deliver both qualifiers directly — this precision lets the speaker defend the Layer-1 claim in Q&A rather than retreat from an overstatement.

## position 4 — claim_evidence — `Fur-released transport relief, not the respiratory arm, carries the Layer-1 fitness signal.`

Pulling Layer 1 together: Fur derepression in the Δlpxc rescue background releases the 32-gene Path A transport machinery — TBDTs and iron-uptake systems — that is marginally but demonstrably enriched for envelope-stress fitness phenotypes. The 26-gene SspB-buffered respiratory arm is not enriched. The Layer-1 fitness signal is in the transport arm.

The evidence base has two direct findings and one partial finding. Direct (A1): Spearman rho=0.315, p=2.08e-03 over 93 Leaden Δfur DEGs with 71% sign concordance confirms Fur derepression as a major driver of the 4584-vs-4580 signal — the foundation that defines both gene sets. Direct (A5): Path B fold enrichment 1.04×, p=0.515 — the clean negative that demotes the respiratory hypothesis; direct evidence that the SspB-buffered respiratory genes are background-equivalent under envelope stress. The "respiratory ATP required for envelope remodeling" framing from earlier drafts of this work is hereby demoted to working hypothesis. Partial (A4): Path A fold enrichment 1.60×, p=0.016 — marginal enrichment against a 33.25% background, with the pre-registration threshold miscalibration acknowledged. Speaker language: "consistent with a fitness-bearing transport arm," not "transport criticality conclusively established."

Two scope qualifiers. First, the iron-limitation arm of H2 is untestable in the current compendium: zero of 198 *Caulobacter* Fitness Browser experiments are under iron limitation. Whether the same Path A transporters that score under envelope stress also score under iron limitation — their canonical Fur-regulated context — requires future RB-TnSeq experiments under bipyridyl chelation or hemin stress. Second, the mechanistic link from "Path A genes carry envelope-stress fitness signal" to "specifically CPG trafficking via the shared Lpt apparatus" is grounded in Uchendu 2026 (bioRxiv) as an inference, not a direct measurement from this project's data. The fitness result establishes which gene set carries the signal; the trafficking mechanism is the Uchendu model that makes structural sense of it and will be tested by the replication proteomics scheduled for summer 2026.

Transition to S2: ChvT (CCNA_03108), the top Path A fitness hit at |t|=43.7, also appears in the late-consequence ChvI cohort identified in NB03. The ChvI envelope-stress two-component system organizes the broader transcriptional response to lipid A loss over time. S2 asks whether ChvI's engagement is real, what its temporal structure looks like, and whether it schedules the envelope rebuild beyond the transport relief Layer 1 establishes.
