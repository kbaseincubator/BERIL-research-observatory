# Speaker notes — substory `S2`

**Substory punchline:** ChvI runs a two-phase cascade: regulators first, envelope-structural genes second.
**Throughline:** A single envelope catastrophe — lipid A loss — triggers five partially independent adaptive layers (Fur-released transport, ChvI two-phase signaling, constitutive sphingolipid substitution, peptidoglycan remodeling, and cross-species structural unavailability) that together explain how *and why* *Caulobacter* alone tolerates the deletion.
**Tier:** STRONG
**Mode:** talk-30

---

## position 0 — section_divider — `ChvI runs a two-phase cascade: regulators first, envelope-structural genes second.`

S1 established that Fur-released transport relief — specifically the Path A concordant-strong set of TonB-dependent transporters — carries the measurable fitness signal of Layer 1, while the SspB-buffered respiratory arm is statistically indistinguishable from the genome-wide background rate of 33.25%. That finding isolated what is fitness-bearing in the Fur-release arm. The hand-off to S2 is precise: if transport relief is the operative Layer-1 mechanism, then the envelope-stress two-component system ChvI is the regulator most directly wired to sense the downstream consequence — sustained outer-membrane disruption from lipid A loss.

ChvG-ChvI in Caulobacter is the sensor kinase–response regulator pair that monitors osmotic imbalances and peptidoglycan damage to maintain envelope homeostasis, as characterized by Quintero-Yanes et al. 2022 (PLoS Genet 18:e1010465, PMID 36480504). Greenwich et al. 2023 (Annu Rev Microbiol 77:131) frames it as a conserved global regulatory circuit across alphaproteobacteria with pervasive impacts on envelope composition and host interactions. The question S2 addresses is whether ChvI mounts a single undifferentiated transcriptional wave in response to lipid A loss, or choreographs distinct early and late programs.

The answer, documented in NB03, is a two-phase cascade. Using the published ChvI regulon union from Stein 2021 and Quintero-Yanes 2022 — 488 ChvI-induced genes in the tested universe — we partitioned the response into three disjoint cohorts: 20 unique-to-early, 10 both-phase, and 49 unique-to-late. All three comfortably exceed the pre-registered ≥10/cohort threshold; phase structure is real. The autoregulation signal at the center — ChvI itself (CCNA_00237) at logFC +1.45 in the early contrast belongs to the 10-gene both-phase cohort — is the most mechanistically compact datum in this section.

Two findings carry important caveats that set expectations before the evidence slides arrive. The functional theme shift from regulator-rich early cohort to envelope-structural late cohort is directionally consistent with a regulator-then-structural scheduling logic but is not statistically discriminated: Fisher exact test p=0.243 on the between-cohort functional theme comparison. The SigU hypothesis — CCNA_02977 as the sigma-factor driver of the late structural program — fails both arms of the pre-registered coherence criterion: 24.5% enrichment against a ≥50% relaxed threshold, and PaperBLAST returned zero substantive snippets for CCNA_02977 in the published Caulobacter literature. SigU is a hypothesis, not a finding. What this section does deliver cleanly — phase structure and ChvI autoregulation — will be carried verbatim into S3, which asks what the late structural program actually installs.

## position 1 — methods_summary — `Methods: ChvI two-phase partition via dual RNA-seq contrasts against published regulon reference`

The core design decision for this section is worth naming explicitly: we used a reference-regulon cross-study approach rather than de novo ChvI regulon discovery in the Δlpxc strain background. We did not re-ChIP or re-sequence ChvI binding targets in our rescued strains; instead, we asked which genes from the two published ChvI-induced regulons show induced expression in our own RNA-seq contrasts. This places our Δlpxc-state observations in direct dialogue with established ChvI biology, allowing a clean answer to which published ChvI-responsive genes are already active at the Δfur/ΔsspB stage versus which require the full lipid-A-loss signal to appear.

The union of Stein et al. 2021 (J Bacteriol 203:e00199-21, PMID 34124942) and Quintero-Yanes et al. 2022 (PLoS Genet 18:e1010465, PMID 36480504) yields 488 ChvI-induced genes in our tested genome background. We use the union rather than the intersection to capture any gene that either study identified as ChvI-responsive, giving a generous reference set that minimizes false negatives. Each gene is scored in both contrasts at FDR < 0.05 (edgeR): early = 4584-vs-4580 (Δfur Δsspb ΔrsaA versus ΔrsaA), the Fur/SspB release phase; late = 4599-vs-4584 (Δlpxc rescued versus Δfur Δsspb background), the lipid-A-loss phase. Genes induced in both contrasts are both-phase; in early only, unique-to-early; in late only, unique-to-late. Genes not significantly induced in either contrast are excluded.

One methodological correction merits explicit statement: the original report draft described an "early cohort of 30 genes" by combining unique-to-early and both-phase into a single bucket. The corrected disjoint partition is 20 + 10 + 49 = 79 ChvI-induced genes engaged in our data. Both-phase and unique-to-early genes answer different biological questions — both-phase genes are cooperatively sustained across phases, while unique-to-early genes are specifically active at the Fur/SspB release stage and fall out before lipid A is lost. ChvI itself (CCNA_00237) is a both-phase gene, not unique-to-early; the classification is not a labeling convention but a biological distinction that directly shapes the autoregulation interpretation.

The SigU driver hypothesis applied two pre-specified coherence criteria: a literature anchor (PaperBLAST scout on CCNA_02977, returning zero substantive snippets for any Caulobacter SigU study) and a functional coherence threshold (≥50% of late-cohort genes in envelope/transport/regulator categories at Fisher p<1e-3). Both criteria were pre-specified in the research plan before results were examined. The result — 24.5% coherence, Fisher p=0.243 — failed both arms. The methods slide makes explicit what the data can and cannot support before the audience sees the results. Transition: with the design in view, the next slide shows what the partition found.

## position 2 — data_table — `ChvI disjoint partition: 20 unique-early + 10 both-phase + 49 late — ChvI autoregulation at the center`

Three features of the disjoint partition reward particular attention. First, all three cohorts clear the pre-registered ≥10/cohort threshold: 20 unique-to-early, 10 both-phase, and 49 unique-to-late. Phase structure is a direct finding (A8). An earlier version of this analysis described an "early cohort of 30 genes" by summing unique-to-early and both-phase; the corrected disjoint partition separates them because the two cohorts answer different biological questions — both-phase genes are cooperatively sustained through both rescue phases, while unique-to-early genes are specifically active at the Δfur/ΔsspB release stage and fall out before lipid A is lost.

Second, and mechanistically the most significant single datum in this section: ChvI itself (CCNA_00237) belongs to the 10-gene both-phase cohort at logFC +1.45 in the 4584-vs-4580 contrast and continues induction through 4599-vs-4584. This is direct evidence of ChvG-ChvI autoregulation sustained across both phases. ChvI autoregulation via shared promoter elements is noted in the published alphaproteobacterial ChvG-ChvI literature (Greenwich 2023); what our data add is that the autoregulation is active and maintained through the entire temporal span of the Δlpxc rescue — from Fur/SspB release all the way through full lipid-A loss. Also in the both-phase cohort: the amelogenin/CpxP-related protein CCNA_03997. In Escherichia coli, CpxP is the periplasmic chaperone partner of the CpxA-CpxR envelope-stress two-component system; its Caulobacter relative appearing in the cooperatively sustained cohort is consistent with broader envelope-stress chaperone engagement alongside ChvI, though the Caulobacter CpxP-related function is not independently characterized.

Third, the unique-to-late cohort carries the envelope-structural payload. LolA-family CCNA_03820 at logFC +2.89 is exactly the gene Uchendu et al. 2026 (bioRxiv 2026.04.12.717747) showed induced under Δspt — sphingolipid loss; its induction here under Δlpxc, lipid A loss, confirms it as a generic OM-stress lipoprotein-carrier response rather than a lipid-A-specific signal. Pal CCNA_00784 at logFC +2.08 transcript is a cross-notebook convergence hit: it appears in both the NB03 late ChvI cohort (this slide) and the NB05 peptidoglycan-remodeling set at logFC +2.84 protein — a convergence that will anchor the mechanistic discussion in S4.

A caveat for Q&A: the 49 late-cohort genes are not all mechanistically characterized in Caulobacter. LolA, Pal, and the TBDT entries in the table are the most structurally interpretable hits; the "Envelope-structural / OM" theme label is a directional description, not a proof of mechanism. The full cohort includes osrP stress protein and a Zot-like membrane perturber — envelope-relevant but functionally uncharacterized. Transition: whether the early-to-late functional theme shift is statistically discriminated is the question addressed on the next slide.

## position 3 — claim_evidence — `ChvI schedules two phases — theme shift consistent with regulator-then-structural; SigU link unresolved`

The theme distribution quantifies the directional pattern previewed in the section divider. In the 20-gene unique-to-early cohort, 6.7% are regulator or TCS components — regulatory machinery preferentially activated at the Δfur/ΔsspB release stage — and 0% are lipid/membrane or PG/cell wall genes. In the 49-gene unique-to-late cohort, 10.2% are envelope/OM genes and 12.2% are TBDTs — the late phase enriches for outer-membrane building and solute transport. This directional pattern matches what the regulator-then-structural scheduling hypothesis predicts: regulatory response installed first, structural envelope components deployed second.

The critical caveat must be delivered plainly: the Fisher exact test of this between-cohort theme shift yields p=0.243, not statistically significant even at a relaxed criterion. The "regulator-then-structural" scheduling narrative is directionally consistent with the data but is not statistically discriminated (A11 = partial per the plan's critical-analysis inventory). The structural analysis in this section therefore has two layers with different evidence quality. The partition structure itself — 20 + 10 + 49 genes, all three cohorts above the ≥10/cohort pre-registered threshold — is a direct finding (A8). ChvI autoregulation at logFC +1.45 in the both-phase set is a direct finding (A9). The functional interpretation of what early versus late genes are doing differently is well-motivated but not yet statistically confirmed. Speaker language: "the data are consistent with a regulator-first scheduling logic," not "the regulator-then-structural theme shift is proven."

The SigU analysis is the section's most explicitly demoted finding. SigU (CCNA_02977) is an attractive candidate — a sigma factor that, if active in the late envelope-stress context, could explain why the late cohort turns structural — but the coherence check returned 24.5% enrichment in the 49-gene late cohort, well short of the ≥50% relaxed threshold, with Fisher p=0.243. More fundamentally, PaperBLAST returned zero substantive snippets for CCNA_02977 in any Caulobacter study — Caulobacter SigU is simply uncharacterized in the published literature, and without an independent SigU gold standard, the cohort attribution cannot be validated. The experiment that would resolve this is targeted SigU-induction RNA-seq in a vanillate-inducible overexpression strain; until that experiment is done, SigU is a named hypothesis, not a finding (A10 = ✗ at the relaxed coherence criterion per plan inventory).

Transition to S3: this section established that ChvI runs a two-phase program — regulators early, envelope-structural genes late — but it did not identify what the late structural program actually installs in the outer membrane. The answer, addressed in S3, is a constitutive anionic-sphingolipid pool already present in wild type: not an inducible emergency response synthesized when lipid A is lost, but an existing reservoir that is repurposed when the lipid A slot vacates.
