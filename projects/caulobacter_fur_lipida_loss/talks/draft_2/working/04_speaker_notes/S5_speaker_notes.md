## position 0 — section_divider — `The machinery is *Caulobacter*-restricted, and every layer carries its pre-registered grade.`

This closing substory addresses the two questions every audience asks after multi-layer mechanistic data: how far does the mechanism extend beyond this organism, and how much should each layer be believed? S5 has two faces joined by a single punchline. The first is scope: the sphingolipid biosynthesis pathway and the ChvG-ChvI envelope-stress regulatory circuit are annotation-confirmed absent from the three species known to tolerate lipid A loss by entirely different routes — Acinetobacter baumannii, Neisseria meningitidis, and Moraxella catarrhalis. Those organisms cannot use the Caulobacter route because they lack the structural prerequisites. The second face is confidence: every mechanism layer carries exactly the evidence grade the pre-registered scorecard assigned, not a flattened or promoted version of it.

Four substories have built the evidence ladder in sequence. S1 established Δfur derepression as the driver: Spearman ρ = 0.315, p = 2.08e-03, 71% sign concordance over 93 Leaden Δfur DEGs, with iron limitation ruled out as the primary explanation. S2 showed that only the Fur-released Path A subset enriches for envelope-stress fitness above the calibrated 33.25% genome background — fold 1.60×, p = 0.016 — while the SspB-buffered Path B falls to background at fold 1.04×, p = 0.515. S3 demonstrated the two-phase ChvI response: 20 unique-to-early genes, 10 both-phase, 49 late-consequence, with the late-cohort driver remaining open at SigU coherence 24.5%, Fisher p = 0.243. S4 showed sphingolipid biosynthesis is constitutive — 0 of 6 pathway genes induced when lipid A is lost — and that 28 PG-remodeling loci engage, dominated by 20 genes DOWN with specific inductions including SdpA at +4.8 log2 protein and Pal at +2.08 transcript and +2.84 protein.

S5 maps the reach and the grade simultaneously. The comparative-genomics arm (A21/A22) is direct evidence, graded by NCBI annotation; its method caveat (A23) is partial — annotation-scoped, not structural-homology-verified. The graded synthesis (A26) is partial, only as strong as its direct-tier component layers. The pre-registered scorecard (A27) is direct — the discipline that prevents post-hoc re-grading and makes every SUPPORTED verdict in this deck credible. We open with the method before the annotation table because understanding why NB06b replaced NB06 is prerequisite to reading the comparator-absence data at the correct confidence level.

## position 1 — methods_summary — `Comparative annotation: NCBI presence/absence across four species`

Before the species-comparison table appears, the evolution from NB06 to NB06b requires a brief explanation, because it shifts the headline claim from "PaperBLAST-screen supported" to "NCBI-annotation confirmed" — a transition triggered by the adversarial review's finding of an approximately 80% false-negative rate in NB06 for Caulobacter's own lipid A biosynthesis genes.

The original NB06 used PaperBLAST text-matching, which searches literature descriptions for gene names and function keywords. REPORT §Finding 6 (Method check row) documents the specific false-negative pattern: LpxA returned 0 PaperBLAST hits but 11 NCBI annotation hits for C. crescentus; LpxC — the gene deleted in this project — returned 0 PaperBLAST hits but 15 NCBI hits; LpxD returned 0 PaperBLAST hits but 15 NCBI hits; LpxK returned 0 PaperBLAST hits but 18 NCBI hits. The cause is Caulobacter's naming convention: genes are annotated primarily under CCNA_* locus tags rather than the canonical Gram-negative gene symbols that PaperBLAST's description-text matching relies on.

NB06b, added post-review, reran the comparative panel using Biopython Entrez against NCBI's curated protein database across each of the four named RefSeq genomes — C. crescentus NA1000, A. baumannii ATCC 17978, N. meningitidis MC58, and M. catarrhalis BBH18 — querying by canonical gene symbols. The critical point for interpreting the headline biological claim: the finding depends on absences in the three comparator species, not on presences in Caulobacter. For those absences, the naming-convention false-negative issue does not apply — A. baumannii, N. meningitidis, and M. catarrhalis genuinely lack spt, cerR, ChvG, and ChvI under any annotation scheme. NCBI's 0-hit pattern in the comparators is robust regardless of how Caulobacter labels its own genes; the approximately 80% PaperBLAST false-negative rate for Caulobacter's lipid A genes actually strengthens confidence in the comparator absence data rather than weakening it.

The scope limitation stands. Annotation-based searches can miss diverged orthologs annotated under functional descriptors rather than canonical gene symbols. REPORT §Future Directions §8 specifies the full-rigor next step: HMM-based homology search using Pfam profiles PF00155 (serine palmitoyltransferase), PF03331 (LpxC), and PF01475 (Fur) against the four named RefSeq proteomes via hmmsearch or pyhmmer, estimated 2-4 hours via the CDM Task Service. That analysis has not been run. Uniqueness is annotation-scoped, not structural-homology-verified. The next slide shows what the NCBI annotation returns.

## position 2 — data_table — `Sphingolipid pathway and ChvG-ChvI absent from all three comparator species`

The table delivers a biologically clean result: four gene families queried across four genomes, with the three comparator species returning zero annotation hits in every row.

From REPORT §Finding 6 and §Interpretation §9, confirmed in NB06b cell 5: spt (serine palmitoyltransferase, the first committed step of sphingolipid biosynthesis) returns 7 NCBI annotation hits in C. crescentus and 0 hits each in A. baumannii, N. meningitidis, and M. catarrhalis. cerR (ceramide reductase) returns 6 in C. crescentus and 0/0/0. ChvG (the envelope-stress sensor histidine kinase) returns 5 in C. crescentus and 0/0/0. ChvI (the cognate response regulator) returns 6 in C. crescentus and 0/0/0. All four families show the same 1-0-0-0 pattern: present specifically in Caulobacter, absent from all three comparators by NCBI annotation.

The sphingolipid result is independently supported by Olea-Ozuna et al. 2021 and 2024, who characterized the Caulobacter ceramide biosynthesis cluster and documented its restriction to organisms that encode this particular pathway. The ChvG-ChvI result is consistent with Greenwich et al. 2023's review documenting alphaproteobacterial conservation of this envelope-stress regulatory circuit — A. baumannii is Gammaproteobacteria, N. meningitidis is Betaproteobacteria, and M. catarrhalis belongs to Moraxellaceae; none is Alphaproteobacteria and none encodes ChvG-ChvI.

The biological consequence is structural unavailability: each comparator is limited to its own genomic toolkit when it tolerates lipid A loss. A. baumannii uses septal class A penicillin-binding protein PBP1A inactivation plus ld-transpeptidases LdtJ and LdtK — the Kang et al. 2021 PG-remodeling route. N. meningitidis requires intact capsular polysaccharide biosynthesis — the Steeghs et al. 2001 capsule-substitution route. M. catarrhalis uses late acyltransferase truncation at lpxX and lpxL — the Gao et al. 2008 route. None of these routes involve Fur-derepressed sphingolipid bypass or a ChvG-ChvI regulatory circuit. The Caulobacter mechanism is not merely absent from those organisms; it is inaccessible because they lack both the substitute lipid and the regulator that deploys it.

The annotation-based 0-hit pattern in the comparators is robust, but it rests on gene symbols rather than structural homology. Two explicit scope flags — the method caveat and the sphk named exception — require statement before the uniqueness claim can be taken at full value. Those flags are the subject of the next slide.

## position 3 — claim_evidence — `Annotation-scoped uniqueness holds for spt, cerR, ChvG, ChvI — sphk is a named exception`

Two scope flags on the uniqueness claim must be stated explicitly before the audience accepts it at full value.

The first is A23 in the plan, graded partial. NB06b reports annotation-based presence using canonical gene symbols from NCBI's curated protein database. A diverged ortholog performing the serine palmitoyltransferase reaction in, say, A. baumannii but annotated under a different functional descriptor or a species-specific locus tag would be entirely missed. This risk is not hypothetical — the same NCBI annotation misses MsbA in C. crescentus (present as CCNA_00307, annotated "phospholipid/LPS ABC transporter") and LptA because Caulobacter uses CCNA_* locus tags rather than canonical Gram-negative symbols. REPORT §Limitations states explicitly that the annotation-based search is "confirmatory but not exhaustive." The full-rigor test is Pfam HMM searches — PF00155 (serine palmitoyltransferase), PF03331 (LpxC), and PF01475 (Fur) — against the four RefSeq proteomes: GCF_000196795.1 for A. baumannii ATCC 17978, GCF_000008805.1 for N. meningitidis MC58, GCF_000092265.1 for M. catarrhalis BBH18, estimated 2-4 hours via the CDM Task Service (REPORT §Future Directions §8). That search has not been run. The uniqueness claim is annotation-scoped, not structural-homology-verified.

The second flag is A17, the sphk named exception, surfaced by adversarial review N1. Sphingosine kinase (sphk) returns 287 NCBI hits outside Caulobacter — it is broadly distributed and not Caulobacter-restricted. sphk catalyzes a downstream step in ceramide metabolism, and its presence elsewhere does not reconstitute the full ceramide phosphoglycerate biosynthesis pathway in organisms lacking the upstream entry step spt and the ceramide-forming steps cerR. The speaker must not claim the entire sphingolipid pathway is Caulobacter-unique without naming sphk as an explicit exception. The honest bounded claim is: the core biosynthetic commitment steps — spt at the pathway entry and cerR for ceramide reduction — are Caulobacter-restricted by NCBI annotation; sphk is a named and broadly distributed exception that must be stated whenever the species-uniqueness framing is used.

The PaperBLAST false-negative observation paradoxically strengthens confidence in the comparator absence data. The naming-convention problem is Caulobacter-specific — LpxC: 0 PaperBLAST hits vs. 15 NCBI hits exemplifies the pattern. The comparators have no CCNA_* aliases to generate false negatives for genes they genuinely lack; their 0-hit results are robust regardless of PaperBLAST's Caulobacter limitations. With these two flags on the record — annotation scope and the sphk exception — the final slide presents the pre-registered scorecard that grades the full five-layer mechanism.

The mechanistic synthesis (A26, partial) integrates these heterogeneous evidence layers and is only as strong as its direct-tier components. The wiring between Δfur derepression and Lpt repurposing for sphingolipid trafficking is consistent with the Uchendu 2026 shared-component model but is not directly demonstrated by this dataset; replicated proteomics scheduled for summer 2026 are the key next experiment before any mechanistic-wiring claim can advance from inferential to supported.

The pre-registered scorecard (A27, direct) is the discipline that makes every SUPPORTED verdict in this deck credible. Each threshold was locked before DE analysis. The CtpA rejection is the most visible product of that discipline: a monotonic CPM rise from 37 to 42 to 63 across the strain series looks suggestive of gradual induction, but the pre-registered 4599-vs-4584 contrast — designed to isolate ΔlpxC-specific induction — does not meet the bar at either transcript (FDR = 0.109) or protein (not detected) level. The cumulative 4599-vs-4580 contrast at FDR = 0.035 is noted in REPORT §Finding 4 as a supplemental observation consistent with CtpA being Fur-regulated, but it conflates Δfur, ΔsspB, and ΔlpxC effects and was not the pre-registered test. Honoring that verdict is precisely what makes the Path A enrichment, the ChvI partition, and the sphingolipid constitutivity results credible: the same bar applies everywhere.

The arc closes here. Leaden concordance opened it by establishing Δfur as the demonstrable driver; the graded scorecard closes it by grounding every downstream layer at its actual evidence level. The Caulobacter sphingolipid substitution mechanism is structurally inaccessible to the three known comparator species. What is supported is stated supported; what is rejected is named rejected; what remains open is defined by the Future Directions the REPORT specifies — replicated proteomics, SigU-induction RNA-seq, and HMM-based homology search to harden the comparative-genomics arm. That gradient of confidence is the intellectual contribution of the pre-registered approach, and it is the honest version of the result.
