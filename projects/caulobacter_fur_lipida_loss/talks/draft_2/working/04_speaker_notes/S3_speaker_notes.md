# Speaker notes — substory `S3`

**Substory punchline:** The Fur-set envelope state triggers a two-phase ChvI response; its late regulator stays open.
**Throughline:** Δfur derepression is the demonstrable driver of the lipid A-loss-permissive envelope state, and everything downstream is the supporting (and still-open) mechanism it sets in motion.
**Tier:** STRONG
**Mode:** talk-30

## position 0 — section_divider — `The Fur-set envelope state triggers a two-phase ChvI response; its late regulator stays open.`

Coming off S2's Path A/B partition — where we showed that only the Fur-released TBDT subset enriches for envelope-stress fitness at fold 1.60× over the 33.25% genome background, while the SspB-buffered respiratory arm falls to fold 1.04× — we now follow the Fur-set envelope state into the regulatory signaling layer. S3 asks not which Fur targets carry the fitness signal, but what the Fur-established envelope state activates at the transcriptional-sensor level. The sensor is ChvG-ChvI, Caulobacter's dedicated two-component envelope-homeostasis system.

The argument of S3 divides into two honest halves. The first is what the driver demonstrably activates: the ChvI regulon partitions into three fully disjoint phases across our strain-series contrasts — 20 genes unique-to-early (Fur-release-coincident), 10 in both phases (cooperative continuation), and 49 late-consequence genes responding specifically to lipid-A loss. All three cohorts clear the pre-registered ≥10-gene phase-structure threshold strongly, and the partition is robust and descriptive. The H1 phase-structure verdict is SUPPORTED. The second half is what we could not resolve: the SigU coherence test, the pre-registered sub-hypothesis for what drives the late 49-gene cohort, returned 24.5% envelope/transport/regulator enrichment with Fisher p=0.243 — below both the statistical significance threshold and the 50% relaxed criterion. The late-cohort driver is genuinely unassigned in this work, and that is the honest state of the evidence.

What makes this partition meaningful — and what distinguishes it from any prior ChvI description — is the three-strain design established in S1. By holding the Fur/SspB permissive state constant in the early contrast (4584-vs-4580, LpxC still present) and then comparing it to the lipid-A-deficient state (4599-vs-4584, LpxC deleted), we can separate what ChvI does because of Fur derepression from what it does specifically because lipid A is lost. That contrast design is what makes the early/late phase partition interpretable as a biological result rather than a condition-confounded composite. The adversarial review also raised ChvR sRNA as an untested post-transcriptional confound on the late cohort; we acknowledge it as an open question with no resolution in this dataset. The three content slides in this section walk through the method setup, the partition counts and exemplars, and the SigU/ChvR open question in order.

## position 1 — methods_summary — `ChvI phase partition: two published regulons cross-referenced against our strain-series contrasts`

The foundation for this analysis is the ChvI reference regulon — 488 ChvI-induced genes assembled as the union of two independent published datasets. Stein 2021 characterized the ChvG-ChvI system via deletion in defined medium; Quintero-Yanes 2022 captured ChvI response under vancomycin and osmotic stress in C. crescentus NA1000. Taking their union gives the broadest possible ChvI-induced working universe, avoiding gene dropout caused by condition-specific induction in either study alone. Any ChvI-regulated gene appearing in at least one of those two published regulons is eligible for phase assignment in our data.

The early-phase contrast is 4584-vs-4580: Δfur ΔsspB ΔrsaA versus the ΔrsaA parent. This captures the combined dual-release state — Fur suppression and SspB buffering are both removed — while LpxC remains fully intact. The cell is not yet lipid-A-deficient in this contrast; the Fur/SspB permissive state is established, but the envelope is structurally complete. The late-phase contrast is 4599-vs-4584: the ΔlpxC rescue state versus the dual-release intermediate. This isolates what ChvI does specifically in response to lipid-A loss, with the Fur/SspB permissive state already present and held constant across the comparison. The phase assignment is fully disjoint: every gene appears in exactly one cohort. The three counts — 20 unique-to-early, 10 both-phase, 49 late-consequence — sum to 79 total engaged ChvI-regulon genes, and no gene is counted in two cohorts.

The pre-registered threshold for the H1 phase-structure verdict was ≥10 genes per cohort (RESEARCH_PLAN §hypotheses H1). This prevents a handful of coincidental regulon overlaps from being interpreted as a biological phase. All three cohorts clear the threshold strongly, and the H1 verdict is SUPPORTED. The SigU coherence test was the pre-registered H1 sub-hypothesis: if SigU is the late-cohort driver, the late 49 genes should enrich for envelope/transport/regulator functional categories relative to the early 20. The relaxed criterion — 50% enrichment instead of the original 75%, p<1e-3 instead of p<0.05 — gave SigU the best possible inferential chance. The adversarial review (N3) flagged ChvR sRNA as a post-transcriptional alternative for late-cohort modulation; we lack ChvR expression data in our strain backgrounds, so this confound is acknowledged but unresolved. One scope caveat spanning the whole section: everything here describes the ChvI response to our specific Fur/SspB/LpxC perturbation series under PYE-rich conditions. The phase structure under other envelope stresses or growth media has not been measured. The next slide shows the three-phase cohort counts and their exemplar genes.

## position 2 — data_table — `ChvI two-phase partition: ChvI autoregulation sits in the both-phase cohort`

The three-phase ChvI partition shown here — 20 unique-to-early, 10 both-phase, and 49 late-consequence genes — is the core H1 result. All counts are verbatim from REPORT §Finding 3 and NB03 cell 7; together they total 79 ChvI-induced genes engaged across at least one contrast in our strain series.

The unique-to-early cohort is notable for its stress-responder composition. The lasso peptide cyclase CCNA_02794 at logFC +8.6 is the highest-magnitude early hit. ApbE, the iron-sulfur cluster repair protein, registers at +3.8. ImuB, the SOS DNA polymerase, registers at +1.5. That imuB entry is mechanistically connecting — Leaden 2018 documented SOS activation as part of the Δfur transcriptional program, and seeing imuB at +1.5 in the early-phase ChvI cohort confirms that the SOS and ChvI regulatory responses share the same precipitating event: constitutive Fur derepression in our strain. These are not independent signals; they are two readouts of the same Fur-release event appearing in different analytical layers, and together they corroborate the S1 driver claim.

The both-phase cohort contains the mechanistically critical observation: ChvI itself, CCNA_00237 at logFC +1.45 in the 4584-vs-4580 early contrast, sits in this set — direct autoregulation evidence. Once the ChvG-ChvI system activates during the Fur/SspB release phase, ChvI sustains its own transcription through the lipid-A-loss transition. The SIMPL-family CCNA_02378 shows progressive amplification: logFC +1.9 in the early contrast increasing to +3.9 in the late, consistent with continued engagement as LpxC is actually deleted. The amelogenin/CpxP-related CCNA_03997 is notable context — CpxP in E. coli is the periplasmic chaperone partner of the CpxA envelope-stress sensor, making CCNA_03997 a Caulobacter echo of cross-sensor envelope-stress signaling.

The late cohort shows a functional theme shift toward envelope and outer-membrane structural effectors — 10.2% envelope/OM and 12.2% TBDT genes among the 49, versus 6.7% regulator/TCS in the unique-to-early 20. The LolA-family OM lipoprotein carrier CCNA_03820 registers at logFC +2.89; the Pal-like CCNA_00784 at +2.08. This theme shift is suggestive of a regulatory-to-structural handoff as lipid-A loss is established, but the Fisher exact test of envelope/transport/regulator enrichment in the late cohort versus the unique-to-early cohort returned p=0.243 — the theme shift is descriptive and not statistically discriminated at these cohort sizes. The partition counts and exemplars are the robust, pre-registered result; thematic interpretation is illustrative and stated as such. The next slide addresses the key open question: what transcriptional driver controls the late 49-gene cohort.

## position 3 — claim_evidence — `SigU coherence fails the pre-registered bar (24.5%, p=0.243); late-cohort driver unassigned`

The largest ChvI cohort — 49 late-consequence genes induced only after lipid-A loss — does not have an identified transcriptional driver in this dataset, and that is the honest result the speaker delivers without softening it in either direction.

The pre-registered SigU coherence test (RESEARCH_PLAN §hypotheses H1, NB03 cell 10) returned 24.5% envelope/transport/regulator enrichment in the late 49 genes with Fisher p=0.243. From REPORT §Finding 3: "The reframed SigU coherence check on the late cohort returned 24.5% envelope/transport/regulator enrichment — below the 50% relaxed criterion." From REPORT §Limitations: "even the relaxed criterion did not pass (Fisher p=0.243)." This is a genuine negative at the pre-registered bar. The 24.5% figure means roughly one in four late-cohort genes fall into the expected functional categories. For a membrane-centric organism where those categories represent a substantial fraction of the genome, this offers no discriminatory signal above what a random draw of 49 genes would produce.

The test does not falsify SigU as a regulator of the Caulobacter envelope-stress response more broadly. Adversarial review N2 corrected an earlier framing in this project: SigU does have published characterization in alphaproteobacteria, so the accurate framing is "late-cohort driver unassigned" rather than "no candidate identified." The coherence test asks whether this specific 49-gene set, under this specific lipid-A-loss perturbation, shows the functional composition expected of a SigU-driven regulon. At 24.5% and p=0.243, the answer is no. The planned next experiment is ectopic vanillate-inducible SigU overexpression RNA-seq in the 4599 background (REPORT Future Directions §2). That experiment would directly test whether a SigU-induced regulon overlaps the late 49-gene cohort and resolve the attribution question. What we have here is an honest absence of support for a specific mechanistic claim, not a disproven hypothesis about SigU's biology.

The ChvR sRNA layer, flagged by adversarial review N3, adds a second source of late-cohort uncertainty. ChvR provides post-transcriptional modulation of ChvI-pathway output in Caulobacter and could sculpt late-cohort composition independently of any sigma-factor logic. We have no ChvR expression data in our strain backgrounds; this gap is explicitly acknowledged and represents a known next experiment, not a failure of the current design. When the audience asks what drives the late cohort, the correct answer is: we do not know, the pre-registered test did not support SigU, and two experiments — SigU-induction RNA-seq and ChvR characterization in this background — are the planned resolution path. With the ChvI two-phase response characterized and its late driver acknowledged as open, S4 takes over to ask what the cell physically does to maintain the envelope without lipid A. Constitutive sphingolipids and reorganized peptidoglycan carry cleaner direct-tier evidence than the signaling layer just covered, and they are the structural-effector complement to the regulatory picture S3 has built.
