# Speaker notes — substory `S4`

**Substory punchline:** Without lipid A, constitutive sphingolipids and reorganized peptidoglycan hold the envelope together.
**Throughline:** Δfur derepression is the demonstrable driver of the lipid A-loss-permissive envelope state, and everything downstream is the supporting (and still-open) mechanism it sets in motion.
**Tier:** STRONG
**Mode:** talk-30

## position 0 — section_divider — `Without lipid A, constitutive sphingolipids and reorganized peptidoglycan hold the envelope together.`

S3 established the signaling layer: the Fur-set envelope state triggers a two-phase ChvI response — 20 unique-to-early genes, 10 both-phase, and 49 late-consequence — and closed with one honest gap: the late-cohort driver was not supported at the pre-registered bar, with SigU coherence at 24.5% and Fisher p=0.243. S4 leaves that open regulatory question behind and asks the structural question that follows it: once lipid A is physically absent from the outer membrane, what holds the envelope together?

The experimental lens is the 4599-vs-4584 contrast — strain 4599 (Δfur ΔsspB ΔlpxC ΔrsaA) against its direct precursor 4584 (Δfur ΔsspB ΔrsaA) — designed to isolate the ΔlpxC-specific structural response with the Fur and SspB permissive state already present and constant across both sides of the comparison. Zik et al. 2022 established the biological preconditions for this comparison: Δfur is genetically required for ΔlpxC viability, and anionic sphingolipid ceramide phosphoglycerate is required as well. S4 asks which molecular changes at the lipid and peptidoglycan layers explain why a cell meeting those two conditions can survive without lipid A.

The evidence yields two answers with direct-tier support and two with important caveats. The well-supported answers are sphingolipid constitutivity — the biosynthesis pathway was already running at baseline, with zero of six core genes induced when lipid A is lost — and peptidoglycan reorganization, where a specific lytic transglycosylase and the Pal-Tol complex engage against a backdrop of broad synthesis suppression. The partial answers are the Lpt transport apparatus, where transcript and protein evidence point in opposite directions and require replicated proteomics to resolve, and the CtpA processing step predicted by Zik 2022, which is rejected at our pre-registered evidential bar. Keeping those grades distinct — not flattening them into a uniform "supported" story — is the core responsibility of S4's delivery. The methods slide establishes both modalities before any results appear.

## position 1 — methods_summary — `Measuring the structural-effector response: transcript panel and single-replicate OM proteome`

Before any numbers appear, the audience needs to understand that two modalities underpin this section with fundamentally different statistical standing, and that the analytical decisions about what counts as evidence were made before any data were examined.

The primary modality is replicated transcriptomics. Differential expression was computed with edgeR across all 3,943 Caulobacter protein-coding genes using the 4599-vs-4584 contrast at an FDR < 0.05 threshold. Three gene sets were pre-curated and locked before DE analysis was run: the sphingolipid biosynthesis panel of six core genes plus three Uchendu 2026 inner-membrane transporter genes; the canonical Lpt apparatus panel of six components; and the PG remodeling gene set of 53 loci assembled from transpeptidase, transglycosylase, amidase, and hydrolase family annotations. Pre-curating before analysis prevents the circular selection that would make any enrichment result uninterpretable. Every FDR and logFC value stated in this section comes from the replicated edgeR analysis.

The secondary modality is a single-replicate outer membrane proteome from three strains — 4580 wild-type, the intermediate, and the 4672 rescued strain — analyzed by mass spectrometry. REPORT §Limitations states this directly: "OM proteome is single-replicate per strain — no per-protein statistics; only direction is interpretable." Every protein-level magnitude stated in this section is a directional pilot observation. Whether it survives to statistical certainty depends on the replicated proteomics scheduled for summer 2026. The speaker must say this at each protein-level result, not imply it from a footnote.

The CtpA analysis added a third layer of methodological discipline: a BORDERLINE decision criterion established before the analysis was run. The criterion required simultaneously a p-value between 0.05 and 0.15 in the pre-registered 4599-vs-4584 contrast AND detection of CtpA protein in the outer membrane proteome. Both conditions had to be met at once; failing either triggers REJECTED, not BORDERLINE. The notebook outputs `CTPA VERDICT: REJECTED`. Understanding why that happens — two independent failure modes, not one — is the essential context for the CtpA slide. This methods slide draws the clean line between what is statistically established and what is directional-only, and the evidence grading throughout S4 follows from that division.

## position 2 — data_figure — `Sphingolipid biosynthesis is constitutive: 0 of 6 pathway genes induced when lipid A is lost`

The heatmap delivers a single, unambiguous result: when lipid A is deleted from the rescued strain, the sphingolipid biosynthesis pathway does not respond by increasing output. Zero of the six core biosynthesis genes is significantly upregulated in the 4599-vs-4584 contrast. Two pathway members move in the opposite direction: spt, the serine palmitoyltransferase catalyzing the first committed step, is down at logFC −0.64, FDR 0.002; sphk, sphingosine kinase, is down at logFC −0.40, FDR 0.02. The remaining four genes are flat across the strain series.

This directly falsifies the founding hypothesis of the project: that Δfur derepresses sphingolipid biosynthesis, driving elevated ceramide phosphoglycerate production that substitutes for lipid A. The transcript data show the exact opposite. The cell does not respond to lipid A loss by making more sphingolipid transcript. Rescue is therefore a constitutive-pool story — the existing CPG supply, present before LpxC is ever deleted, is sufficient. Whether that sufficiency reflects baseline pool size, redistribution of shared substrates when LpxC competition disappears, or post-transcriptional flux changes through the shared LptB ATPase is not resolved here; lipidomics of the rescued strain is listed as Future Direction 4 in REPORT.

The biochemical literature is consistent with constitutive production. Dhakephalkar et al. 2023 characterized CpgB ceramide kinase biochemically; Dhakephalkar et al. 2025 characterized CpgD phosphoglycerate cytidylyltransferase. Both papers describe constitutively expressed enzymes whose expression levels do not need to increase to produce CPG at sufficient flux — consistent with the transcript result here. Olea-Ozuna et al. 2021 established that this pathway is essential for Caulobacter stationary-phase survival and outer membrane integrity, anchoring the biological importance of constitutive production rather than inducible production.

One scope caveat: all measurements come from a single PYE-rich growth condition. Whether the sphingolipid pathway upregulates under iron-limiting or membrane-stress conditions in the rescued background is untested. The constitutivity claim is solid within this experimental context and is not claimed beyond it. The transition question is: if the CPG pool is already present at baseline, how does the Lpt transport apparatus reorganize when it loses its canonical LPS cargo and must rely on sphingolipid as the primary outer-leaflet lipid?

## position 3 — two_column_compare — `Lpt apparatus in delta-lpxc: transcripts stable or up, but detected proteins decline`

The canonical Lpt system transports lipopolysaccharide from the inner membrane through the periplasm and into the outer leaflet of the outer membrane. Uchendu et al. 2026 showed that in Caulobacter, three inner-membrane protein homologs — lptG2 (CCNA_01213), lptF2 (CCNA_01214), and lptC2 (CCNA_01226) — are required for sphingolipid transport and share the canonical LptB ATPase with LPS transport. The shared-component model predicts that when LPS disappears in the rescued strain, the Lpt machinery should be maintained or upregulated to continue shipping CPG cargo.

The transcript data are consistent with that prediction. Zero of the six canonical Lpt components are significantly down at FDR < 0.05. MsbA-like CCNA_00307 is up +0.89 log2 at FDR 0.01, and LptC-related CCNA_03716 is up +0.56 log2 at FDR 0.005. These are statistically robust, replicated observations from the transcript layer that support the Uchendu 2026 shared-component model.

The protein layer disrupts that picture. Of the canonical Lpt proteins actually detected in the single-replicate outer membrane proteome, both decline in the rescued strain. LptD (CCNA_01760) shows log2(4672/4659) of −0.47 and log2(4672/4580) of −0.62. LptE (CCNA_03866) shows log2(4672/4659) of −0.78 and log2(4672/4580) of −0.68. The lptC2 sphingolipid transporter protein shows the opposite direction: log2(4672/4659) of +1.08. Importantly, that step is partially a recovery from a prior decrease — lptC2 protein was already down −0.42 log2 in the intermediate strain (4659/4580), making the net change versus WT baseline +0.66 log2 (approximately 1.58-fold), not the full +1.08.

The ⚠ partial grade on this analysis (A14) is honestly earned. The LptD and LptE protein declines could reflect substrate-limitation — when no LPS remains in the outer leaflet, the canonical apparatus has lost its cargo and protein pools turn over without replacement — or they could reflect genuine downregulation of the LPS-specific apparatus as the cell transitions to sphingolipid-only outer-membrane composition. These two interpretations cannot be discriminated from single-replicate data. The discordance is real and belongs in the speaker's delivery, stated as discordance, not resolved by privileging transcript over protein or vice versa. Replicated proteomics scheduled for summer 2026 are required to settle this.

## position 4 — claim_evidence — `CtpA as LpxF-equivalent processing step: not supported at the pre-registered bar`

Zik et al. 2022 predicted that CtpA — CCNA_03113 — functions as a Caulobacter LpxF-equivalent processing enzyme, catalyzing the headgroup modification to ceramide phosphoglycerate that enables CPG to replace lipid A in the outer membrane. This was a mechanistically specific, testable prediction: CtpA should be induced in the lipid A-deficient rescued state because demand for CPG headgroup processing would rise when LPS is no longer available as an outer-leaflet anchor.

We tested this using the pre-registered 4599-vs-4584 contrast with a BORDERLINE criterion written down before analysis: a p-value between 0.05 and 0.15 in that contrast AND detection of CtpA protein in the outer membrane proteome — both conditions simultaneously required. The notebook outputs `CTPA VERDICT: REJECTED`, and the verdict holds on two independent grounds.

First, the transcript p-value in the 4599-vs-4584 contrast is 0.048, with logFC +0.58 and FDR 0.109. A p-value of 0.048 falls below the BORDERLINE lower bound of 0.05, placing it outside the borderline range — too significant for BORDERLINE, but FDR too high to pass the standard threshold. Second, CtpA was not detected in the single-replicate outer membrane proteome. The BORDERLINE criterion required both conditions; protein absence alone triggers REJECTED regardless of transcript behavior.

The CPM panel across strains shows a monotonic rise: 37 CPM in 4580 wild-type, 42 in the intermediate, 63 in 4599 rescued. The pattern looks suggestive of gradual induction. Audiences will ask why this is not being called a trend. The answer must be delivered directly: the pre-registered contrast was 4599-vs-4584, designed before analysis as the clean ΔlpxC isolation test, and it does not meet the criterion set before the data were examined. There is a cumulative 4599-vs-4580 contrast yielding FDR 0.035, which is significant — but it conflates Δfur, ΔsspB, and ΔlpxC effects together rather than isolating lipid-A-loss-driven CtpA induction. REPORT §Finding 4 records it as a supplemental observation consistent with CtpA being Fur-regulated, not as evidence for the Zik 2022 LpxF-equivalent hypothesis.

The Zik 2022 prediction is not refuted — it remains untested at protein resolution — and is the highest-priority mechanistic question for replicated proteomics and targeted lipidomics listed in REPORT Future Directions 1 and 4. Honoring the pre-registered bar is the discipline that makes every SUPPORTED result in this talk credible; this slide is where that discipline is most explicitly on display.

## position 5 — data_figure — `PG remodeling: SdpA lytic transglycosylase and Pal-Tol engage as broad synthesis shuts down`

The peptidoglycan remodeling heatmap shows two superimposed stories, and both need to be named clearly without collapsing one into the other. The dominant pattern is broad suppression: 20 of the 28 loci meeting the H4 threshold are significantly downregulated. The suppressed genes include FtsI penicillin-binding protein involved in cell division; PbpZ at −1.08 log2 transcript; PbpC at −1.15 log2 protein; the D,D-transpeptidase at −1.27 log2 protein; MurD and MurJ biosynthesis and flippase genes; multiple LdpD, LdpE, and LdpF M23-family endopeptidases; amiC amidase; and membrane-bound transglycosylase A at −2.47 log2 protein. The cell systematically shuts down the PG metabolism program it uses during normal growth and division.

Against that background of suppression, three specific inductions stand out. SdpA — CCNA_01252, a soluble lytic murein transglycosylase — shows +4.8 log2 at the protein level in the rescued strain; this is the largest single protein-level signal in the entire ΔlpxC response dataset and appears specifically in the rescued state. Pal — CCNA_00784, the peptidoglycan-associated outer membrane lipoprotein of the Tol-Pal envelope-integrity complex — is up +2.08 log2 at transcript and +2.84 log2 at protein. Critically, Pal appears in both the NB05 PG remodeling analysis and as a top late-cohort ChvI gene from NB03, providing strong cross-notebook convergence for the specificity of its induction.

The mechanistic interpretation of Pal upregulation follows Tan and Chng 2025 (PMID 40055349), who established in E. coli that the primary function of the Tol-Pal complex is retrograde phospholipid transport for outer membrane lipid homeostasis, not LPS-anchored structural scaffolding. In the ΔlpxC rescued strain, the outer leaflet has lost LPS and must maintain lipid homeostasis with sphingolipid instead; Pal upregulation is parsimoniously interpreted as increased retrograde phospholipid transport demand to restore outer-membrane lipid balance. Yeh et al. 2010 (PMID 20693330) established that Caulobacter Tol-Pal is essential for OM constriction at cell division — unlike E. coli where it is dispensable — making this upregulation load-bearing for viability: the rescued strain must complete cell division without any LPS.

The ⚠ partial caveat (A20) applies at the protein level: SdpA +4.8 and Pal +2.84 at protein are single-replicate observations without per-protein statistics; direction is interpretable, magnitude is not. The Pal transcript signal of +2.08 is replicated and FDR-controlled, providing the anchor for the biological interpretation. For comparison, Kang et al. 2021 showed that A. baumannii uses PBP1A loss plus ld-transpeptidases when it eliminates LPS — a mechanistically distinct PG-remodeling route that neither involves Fur nor sphingolipids, consistent with the pathway-absence story this talk has been building. S5 now asks how far this Caulobacter-specific structural machinery extends across related species, and presents the pre-registered hypothesis scorecard that grades the entire arc from driver to effector.
