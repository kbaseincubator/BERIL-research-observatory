# Caulobacter Fur–Lipid A Loss

## Research Question
Why does inactivation of *fur* (the ferric uptake regulator) permit the loss of lipid A in *Caulobacter crescentus*, when no equivalent connection to Fur or iron homeostasis is reported in the other three Gram-negative species known to tolerate lipid A loss (*N. meningitidis*, *A. baumannii*, *M. catarrhalis*)?

## Status
Exploration — research plan not yet written.

## Overview
Lipid A is an essential component of the outer membrane in nearly all Gram-negative bacteria. *Caulobacter crescentus* can survive complete loss of lipid A only when *fur* is also inactivated. The mechanism is not attributable to any single downstream Fur-regulated gene. This project examines lab-generated phenotypic, transcriptomic, and genetic data (input by the user) alongside BERDL pangenomes, fitness data, and external resources (e.g., Palsson ICA decompositions, PaperBLAST, structural biology references) to identify the regulatory and physiological architecture that makes Caulobacter uniquely tolerant of this perturbation.
