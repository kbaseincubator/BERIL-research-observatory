**[1] Raetz CRH, Whitfield C. (2002). "Lipopolysaccharide endotoxins." Annu Rev Biochem 71:635-700.** [REVIEW ARTICLE]
doi:10.1146/annurev.biochem.71.110601.135414 PMID:12045108

- **Studied:** Gram-negative bacteria broadly; lipid A structure, biosynthesis (Raetz pathway), transport, and TLR4 signaling
- **Finding:** Lipid A biosynthesis follows the conserved nine-step Raetz pathway; lipid A-Kdo2 is the minimal LPS structure required for outer membrane biogenesis in most Gram-negatives. LpxC validated as an antibiotic target.
- **Scope alignment:** ⚠ partial
- **Assessment:** ✓ supports
- **Notes:** Foundational LPS review; establishes the lipid A essentiality paradigm that Caulobacter violates. Serves Introduction background.

**[2] Langklotz S, Schakermann M, Narberhaus F. (2011). "Control of lipopolysaccharide biosynthesis by FtsH-mediated proteolysis of LpxC is conserved in enterobacteria but not in all gram-negative bacteria." J Bacteriol 193(5):1090-1097.**
doi:10.1128/JB.01043-10 PMID:21193611

- **Studied:** E. coli, Salmonella, Yersinia, Vibrio, Agrobacterium tumefaciens, Rhodobacter capsulatus, Pseudomonas aeruginosa; FtsH-mediated LpxC proteolysis
- **Finding:** FtsH-mediated LpxC proteolysis is conserved in Enterobacteriaceae but NOT in all Gram-negatives. A. tumefaciens and R. capsulatus LpxC degraded by Lon protease; P. aeruginosa LpxC is stable. Alphaproteobacteria may use non-FtsH mechanisms.
- **Scope alignment:** ⚠ partial
- **Assessment:** ✓ supports
- **Notes:** Relevant to why LpxC dynamics in Caulobacter differ from E. coli. Supports species-specificity sub-claim.

**[3] Moffatt JH, et al. (2010). "Colistin resistance in Acinetobacter baumannii is mediated by complete loss of lipopolysaccharide production." Antimicrob Agents Chemother 54(12):4971-4977.**
