## Abstract

Lipid A, the hydrophobic anchor of lipopolysaccharide (LPS), is broadly required for outer membrane biogenesis in Gram-negative bacteria, yet the alphaproteobacterium *Caulobacter crescentus* can survive its complete ablation when the iron-uptake master repressor Fur and the ClpXP adaptor SspB are additionally deleted, and when the anionic ceramide phosphoglycerate (CPG) sphingolipid is present. The regulatory and proteomic constituents of this compensatory program have not previously been characterized at a genome-wide level. We performed transcriptomic (edgeR differential expression across two pairwise contrasts in a three-strain RNA-seq series) and outer membrane proteomic (single biological replicate) analysis of the permissive and rescued strains, integrated with *Caulobacter* RB-TnSeq fitness data across 198 conditions and with comparative genomics, to define four mechanistic layers at their actual evidence levels. First, Fur-derepressed TonB-dependent transporter genes show a statistically significant Spearman correlation with the published Δ*fur* transcriptome (ρ = 0.315, p = 2.08 × 10⁻³ over 93 reference genes [C-001]), and the concordant-strong subset (32 genes) is marginally enriched for envelope-stress fitness phenotypes relative to the genome background (53.1% vs. 33.25% background; fold = 1.60×, p = 0.016 [C-004, C-005]). Second, the ChvG–ChvI envelope-stress two-component system engages in two temporally distinct phases: an early cooperator phase of 20 genes and a late consequence phase of 49 genes induced only after lipid A is lost [C-010], with ChvI itself autoregulating (logFC = +1.45 [C-014]). Third, sphingolipid biosynthesis is constitutive rather than induced — zero of six biosynthesis genes are upregulated, and *spt* and *sphk* show mild downregulation [C-020, C-021, C-022] — consistent with rescue by a pre-existing CPG pool. Fourth, peptidoglycan remodeling is broad (28 of 53 pre-registered loci reach threshold [C-037]), dominated by basal machinery shutdown (20 loci down [C-039]) with targeted induction of lytic transglycosylases and the Tol-Pal complex. The ΔsspB-buffered respiratory-chain arm shows no fitness enrichment above genome background (fold = 1.04×, p = 0.515 [C-006]), and the candidate LpxF-substitute CtpA fails both transcript and proteome pre-registered criteria (FDR = 0.109, protein undetected [C-019]) and is formally rejected. Comparative genomics confirms that the sphingolipid pathway and ChvG–ChvI are absent from three other Gram-negative species that tolerate loss or truncation of LPS, explaining the species specificity of the rescue. Together, these findings describe a multi-layer, species-specific envelope-remodeling strategy whose key mechanistic components are each reported at an honest evidence grade.

## Introduction

The lipid A moiety of lipopolysaccharide anchors the outer leaflet of the Gram-negative outer membrane and is broadly indispensable for outer membrane biogenesis [Raetz2002]. The enzyme LpxC catalyzes the first committed step in lipid A biosynthesis; conditional inactivation of *lpxC* is lethal in paradigmatic organisms such as *Escherichia coli*. This essentiality was considered universal until the emergence of colistin-resistant *Acinetobacter baumannii* clinical isolates carrying LPS-null mutations in *lpxA*, *lpxC*, or *lpxD*, demonstrating that complete LPS loss is compatible with viability in at least some Gram-negatives [Moffatt2010, Moffatt2011]. Subsequent genetic dissection established that *A. baumannii* achieves lipid A dispensability via a peptidoglycan (PG)-remodeling route involving PBP1A inactivation and ld-transpeptidase activity [Kang2021]. *Neisseria meningitidis* achieves the same outcome via a fundamentally different mechanism: substitution of LPS structural and charge-buffering functions by its capsular polysaccharide [Steeghs2001, Ley2003]. *Moraxella catarrhalis* achieves viability with structurally truncated rather than absent LPS via late-acyltransferase modification [Gao2008, Gao2013]. The mechanistic divergence of these routes reflects the distinct genomic repertoires of each organism; no common mechanism has emerged that generalizes across Gram-negative phyla [Hummels2024].

*Caulobacter crescentus* represents a fourth, mechanistically distinct case of lipid A dispensability. Zik et al. (2022) demonstrated that *C. crescentus* NA1000 survives deletion of *lpxC* only when the ferric-uptake regulator *fur* and the ClpXP adaptor *sspB* are additionally deleted, and that viability further requires the anionic ceramide phosphoglycerate (CPG) sphingolipid — the dominant sphingolipid of *Caulobacter* [Zik2022]. That study framed the permissive requirement as "alterations in Fur-regulated processes, rather than iron status per se," implicitly pointing to Fur-derepressed gene products as mechanistic contributors to the rescue. *Caulobacter* encodes an elaborate sphingolipid biosynthesis pathway [OleaOzuna2021, OleaOzuna2024, Dhakephalkar2023, Dhakephalkar2025] whose components — serine palmitoyltransferase (*spt*), ceramide reductase (*cerR*), ceramide kinase (CpgB), and the phosphoglycerate head-group transferases — are phylogenetically restricted to alphaproteobacteria and Bacteroidetes and are absent from *A. baumannii*, *N. meningitidis*, and *M. catarrhalis*. Recent biochemical work from the Klein laboratory has identified a set of inner-membrane LPS-transport paralogs — LptG2, LptF2, and LptC2 (CCNA_01213, CCNA_01214, CCNA_01226) — required for sphingolipid transport to the outer membrane, sharing the canonical LptB ATPase with the LPS-transport apparatus [Uchendu2026]. This shared-machinery model raises the possibility that the canonical Lpt apparatus is repurposed for sphingolipid transport when LPS is absent.

The ChvG–ChvI two-component system is the principal envelope-stress signaling circuit in *Caulobacter* and related alphaproteobacteria [Greenwich2023]. ChvG is an inner-membrane sensor kinase activated by acidic pH, PG damage, and osmotic perturbation; its cognate response regulator ChvI controls expression of hundreds of genes involved in outer membrane composition, PG assembly and recycling, and inner membrane maintenance [Stein2021, QuinteroYanes2022]. Because Δ*lpxc* strains have profoundly altered outer membrane composition, and because ChvG–ChvI suppressor mutations can partially substitute for Δ*fur* in enabling *lpxC* deletion [Zik2022], the ChvG–ChvI regulon is a primary candidate for coordinating the compensatory transcriptional response.

The role of Δ*sspB* in the rescue is mechanistically ambiguous. SspB is a ClpXP adaptor that in *E. coli* delivers the anti-sigma-E factor RseA to ClpXP for degradation, directly coupling SspB to extracytoplasmic-stress signaling [Flynn2004]; whether this logic extends to *Caulobacter*, where ClpP substrate specificity differs [Bhat2013], is not established. The buffering of specific transcript programs by ΔsspB, and whether those programs are mechanistically critical for lipid A loss, remains an open question that the present data address for the first time.

We performed systematic multi-omic characterization of the permissive (Δ*fur* Δ*sspB* Δ*rsaA*) and rescued (Δ*lpxc* Δ*fur* Δ*sspB* Δ*rsaA*) strains using a pre-registered hypothesis framework. Four mechanistic layers were tested — Fur-released TBDT fitness enrichment (H2), ChvI two-phase engagement (H1), constitutive sphingolipid substitution and Lpt apparatus maintenance (H3), and PG reorganization (H4) — each reported at its actual evidence level alongside two formal rejections and one pilot observation requiring proteomic replication.

## Materials and Methods

### Bacterial strains and growth conditions

Three *C. crescentus* NA1000-background strains were analyzed for transcriptomics: strain 4580 (Δ*rsaA*, serving as the WT-like parental reference), strain 4584 (Δ*fur* Δ*sspB* Δ*rsaA*, the permissive strain in which *lpxC* deletion is viable), and strain 4599 (Δ*lpxc* Δ*fur* Δ*sspB* Δ*rsaA*, the rescued strain). All strains were cultured in PYE rich medium under standard aerobic conditions at 30°C; RNA was collected from mid-exponential-phase cultures. Three strains were used for outer-membrane proteomics: 4580, 4659 (Δ*fur* Δ*sspB*, the proteomics-series permissive strain), and 4672 (Δ*lpxc* Δ*fur* Δ*sspB*, the proteomics-series rescued strain). Outer membrane fractions were prepared by sarkosyl extraction, trypsin digestion, and tandem mass spectrometry with a single biological replicate per strain. The absence of per-protein statistics from a single-replicate proteome restricts interpretation to the directionality of individual protein log₂-ratio changes.

### Differential expression analysis

Two pairwise RNA-seq contrasts were analyzed: the permissive-vs-parental contrast (4584-vs-4580), capturing the combined Δ*fur* and Δ*sspB* transcriptional programs; and the rescued-vs-permissive contrast (4599-vs-4584), isolating the *lpxC*-deletion-specific response. Read counts were normalized by trimmed mean of M-values (TMM) and differential expression was tested using the edgeR negative-binomial model [Robinson2010], with false discovery rate correction by the Benjamini-Hochberg method. The significance threshold was FDR < 0.05 throughout, unless otherwise noted by the pre-registration. The CtpA sub-claim additionally defined a BORDERLINE criterion requiring 0.05 < p-value < 0.15 and protein detection in the OM proteome.

### Concordance analysis with the published Δ*fur* transcriptome

The Leaden 2018 Δ*fur* RNA-seq dataset [Leaden2018] was obtained from the original publication's supplementary Table 2. The 93 genes differentially expressed in the Leaden Δ*fur* strain were joined with our 4584-vs-4580 log₂ fold changes. Spearman rank correlation was computed using `scipy.stats.spearmanr`; sign concordance was defined as the fraction of the 93 genes showing the same direction of change in both datasets.

### RB-TnSeq fitness enrichment analysis

*Caulobacter* RB-TnSeq fitness data were retrieved from the BERDL `kescience_fitnessbrowser` database [Price2018, Arkin2018], comprising 198 experiments. Iron-limitation experiments were absent from the compendium (n = 0 [C-065]); hypothesis H2 was formally descoped to envelope-stress conditions per the pre-registration preflight rule. A gene was classified as phenotype-bearing if its fitness t-statistic exceeded |t| > 4 in ≥ 2 of 22 envelope-stress experiments. Genome background was computed over all annotated *Caulobacter* genes (n = 3,943 [C-004]). Hypergeometric enrichment was tested using `scipy.stats.hypergeom`.

### ChvI phase partitioning

Published ChvI regulons from Stein et al. [Stein2021] and Quintero-Yanes et al. [QuinteroYanes2022] were merged to form a union reference set of 488 ChvI-induced genes [C-009]. Each gene was assigned to one of three disjoint cohorts: unique-to-early (induced at FDR < 0.05 only in 4584-vs-4580), both-phase (induced in both contrasts), or late-consequence (induced only in 4599-vs-4584). Functional theme enrichment across cohorts was assessed using Fisher's exact test (`scipy.stats.fisher_exact`).

### Comparative genomics

Presence/absence of focal gene families was initially assessed using the PaperBLAST text-mining system [Price2017] across four species: *C. crescentus*, *A. baumannii*, *N. meningitidis*, and *M. catarrhalis*. Because PaperBLAST showed approximately 80% false-negative rates for known *Caulobacter* lipid A biosynthesis genes (LpxA, LpxC, LpxD, and LpxK all returning zero PaperBLAST hits despite NCBI confirming 11–18 annotation hits per gene [C-061]), the headline comparative claims were confirmed by NCBI Entrez protein annotation queried via Biopython (notebook 06b).

### Pre-registration

Hypotheses H1 through H4 were specified with quantitative thresholds before differential expression analysis was performed. The 53-gene PG-remodeling locus set (H4) was locked before any DE analysis was run. The fitness background rate for H2 was computed from the data (33.25% [C-004]) after de-scoping the iron-arm; the recalibrated H2 verdict uses hypergeometric enrichment over this background rather than the original fixed-percentage threshold, which sat below the background rate.

### Layer 1: Δ*fur* derepression is the major transcriptional driver; the ΔsspB-buffered respiratory cohort lacks fitness enrichment support

Comparison of the 4584-vs-4580 permissive-vs-parental contrast against the published Leaden 2018 Δ*fur* transcriptome [Leaden2018] confirmed Fur derepression as a major driver of our signal. Across the 93 Leaden Δ*fur* differentially expressed genes, the Spearman correlation was ρ = 0.315 (p = 2.08 × 10⁻³ [C-001]) with 71% sign concordance [C-002]. Figure 2 displays the scatter of Leaden Δ*fur* log₂ fold changes against our 4584-vs-4580 fold changes; the concordant-strong cohort (Path A, 32 genes) occupies the upper-right quadrant, while the ΔsspB-buffered cohort (53 of 93 Leaden Δ*fur* DEGs showing logFC ≈ 0 in our data despite Leaden values of −5 to −9 [C-003]) clusters near zero on the ordinate axis. HutA (CCNA_02277), the canonical TonB-dependent heme transporter [Balhesteros2017] and a well-characterized Fur target [Neto2009], is the extreme outlier (Leaden logFC +2.2, our logFC +10.5), consistent with its constitutive hyper-derepression in the combined Δ*fur* Δ*sspB* background. The buffered cohort is dominated by the *cbb3*/*cyd*/*fix*-NOPQ micro-aerobic respiratory operon (CCNA_01466–01476, *ccoNOPQ*, *cydCDA*, *fixG/H/I*), whose blunting in our data relative to the Leaden Δ*fur*-alone comparison represents a novel transcriptomic observation attributable to ΔsspB-mediated protection of this locus.

To determine whether Path A or Path B genes are selectively important under envelope-stress conditions, we performed hypergeometric enrichment analysis against a genome-background phenotype-bearing rate of 33.25% (3,943 annotated *Caulobacter* genes; 1,311 phenotype-bearing [C-004]). The results are summarized in Table 1. Path A (the Fur-released, concordant-strong set of 32 genes) shows marginal but real enrichment: 53.1% phenotype-bearing, fold = 1.60× over background, hypergeometric p = 0.016 [C-005, C-063]. Path B (the ΔsspB-buffered respiratory set of 26 genes) is indistinguishable from the genome background: 34.6% phenotype-bearing, fold = 1.04×, p = 0.515 [C-006, C-064]. The pre-registered ≥10% threshold, which both sets exceed, was found to fall below the 33.25% background and is therefore not informative about specific enrichment. Accordingly, the hypergeometric enrichment over background is the authoritative H2 verdict. The top Path A fitness hits include ChvT (CCNA_03108, |t| = 43.7 under envelope stress [C-007]) and four Fur-derepressed TonB-dependent transporters (CCNA_02910, CCNA_00210, CCNA_02048, CCNA_00028, |t| = 9–28 [C-008]), consistent with the broader catalog of Fur-regulated TBDTs in *Caulobacter* [HernandezOrtiz2025, Ferreira2016].

The mechanistic significance of the ΔsspB-buffered respiratory operon for the Δ*lpxC* rescue thus remains a working hypothesis. The transcript-level buffering is real and reproducible by comparison with the Leaden dataset, and the proposition that respiratory ATP production is required to sustain envelope-remodeling work is biologically plausible; however, the RB-TnSeq fitness data do not selectively support these specific genes as more critical than randomly drawn *Caulobacter* loci under envelope-stress conditions.

### Layer 2: ChvG–ChvI engages in two temporally distinct phases with evidence of autoregulation; SigU-as-driver of the late cohort is not supported

The union of published ChvI regulons [Stein2021, QuinteroYanes2022] comprises 488 ChvI-induced genes [C-009], which we partitioned into three disjoint cohorts based on their pattern of differential expression across the two contrasts. The pre-registered phase-structure threshold of ≥10 genes per cohort is met strongly [C-062].

The unique-to-early cohort (20 genes [C-010]) is induced in the permissive strain (4584-vs-4580) but not further after lipid A loss. It includes the lasso peptide cyclase CCNA_02794 (logFC = +8.6 [C-011]), the ApbE iron-sulfur cluster repair protein (logFC = +3.8 [C-012]), and the SOS DNA polymerase ImuB (logFC = +1.5 [C-013]). The ImuB induction corroborates Leaden et al.'s observation of SOS-response activation in the Δ*fur* background [Leaden2018], consistent with reactive oxygen species accumulation during iron-regulatory perturbation.

The both-phase cohort (10 genes [C-010]) is induced in both contrasts, reflecting cooperative continuation of ChvI signaling through the Δ*lpxC* transition. ChvI itself (CCNA_00237) resides in this cohort with logFC = +1.45 in the early contrast [C-014], providing direct evidence of ChvG–ChvI autoregulation — a positive-feedback architecture consistent with the conserved alphaproteobacterial ChvG–ChvI circuit [Greenwich2023]. The SIMPL-family protein CCNA_02378 is also in the both-phase cohort, showing escalating induction from logFC = +1.9 in 4584-vs-4580 to +3.9 in 4599-vs-4584 [C-015], suggesting a role that intensifies when LPS is removed.

The late-consequence cohort (49 genes [C-010]) is induced exclusively after lipid A loss. It includes the LolA-family outer-membrane lipoprotein carrier CCNA_03820 (logFC = +2.89 [C-016]) — the same gene shown by Uchendu et al. to be induced under Δ*spt* (sphingolipid loss) [Uchendu2026] — indicating that LolA upregulation is a generic outer-membrane stress response in *Caulobacter* triggered by perturbation of either major outer-membrane lipid class. The late cohort also includes the Pal-like lipoprotein CCNA_00784 (logFC = +2.08 [C-017]), which is further discussed in the context of PG remodeling below.

We tested the hypothesis that SigU (CCNA_02977) drives the functional coherence of the late cohort. A literature scout via PaperBLAST returned no substantive snippets characterizing SigU in *Caulobacter*; this sigma factor is functionally uncharacterized, precluding validation against an external gold standard. Applying a relaxed coherence criterion requiring ≥50% of late-cohort genes to be annotated to envelope/transport/regulator functions, we observed 24.5% [C-018] — below the pre-registered threshold (Fisher's exact p = 0.243 [C-018]). The phase-structure component of H1 is supported; the SigU-as-driver component is partial and awaits targeted SigU-induction RNA-seq for resolution.

### Layer 3: Sphingolipid biosynthesis is constitutive; the canonical Lpt apparatus shows transcript–proteome discordance; CtpA is formally rejected

H3 comprised three pre-registered sub-claims tested by panel analysis of the sphingolipid biosynthesis cluster (Figure 3), the canonical Lpt apparatus (Figure 4), and the candidate LpxF-substitute CtpA. Table 2 summarizes the verdict for each sub-claim.

The first sub-claim — that sphingolipid biosynthesis would be constitutive rather than induced in the rescued state — is strongly supported. Zero of six biosynthesis genes (including *spt*, *bcerS*, *cerR*, *cpgB*, *cpgD*, and *sphk*) are upregulated at FDR < 0.05 in the rescued-vs-permissive contrast [C-020]. *spt* (CCNA_01220, encoding serine palmitoyltransferase) shows logFC = −0.64 (FDR = 0.002 [C-021]) and *sphk* (CCNA_01218, sphingosine kinase) shows logFC = −0.40 (FDR = 0.02 [C-022]), both mildly downregulated. This result is consistent with the established biochemistry of the CPG pathway [Dhakephalkar2023, Dhakephalkar2025] showing constitutively active enzymes, and with the requirement for pre-existing CPG for *lpxC*-deletion viability [Zik2022]. The rescue is therefore flux-driven from an existing CPG pool, not driven by transcriptional upregulation of biosynthesis; this rules out a model in which Δ*fur* derepresses the sphingolipid biosynthesis locus as the primary enabling mechanism.

![Heatmap of the sphingolipid biosynthesis locus log2-CPM+1 per replicate across the three-strain series showing flat-to-slightly-decreasing expression in the rescued strain](figures/NB04_sphingolipid_locus_heatmap.png)

**Figure 3.** Sphingolipid biosynthesis cluster expression across the strain series (log₂(CPM+1) per replicate). Zero of six biosynthesis genes are upregulated in the rescued-vs-permissive contrast [C-020]. *spt* (logFC = −0.64, FDR = 0.002 [C-021]) and *sphk* (logFC = −0.40, FDR = 0.02 [C-022]) are mildly downregulated, indicating that CPG production is constitutive and is not transcriptionally induced by lipid A loss.

The second sub-claim — that the canonical Lpt apparatus is maintained or upregulated at the transcript level — is supported. No Lpt component is downregulated at transcript level [C-023]. The MsbA-like flippase CCNA_00307 (logFC = +0.89, FDR = 0.01 [C-024]) and the LptC-related CCNA_03716 (logFC = +0.56, FDR = 0.005 [C-025]) are significantly upregulated in the rescued strain, consistent with the shared-component model of Uchendu et al. in which canonical Lpt machinery participates in sphingolipid transport [Uchendu2026]. However, the two canonical Lpt components actually detected in the single-replicate outer membrane proteome — LptD (CCNA_01760) and LptE (CCNA_03866) — show declining protein levels in the rescued strain relative to both the permissive intermediate (LptD: log₂(4672/4659) = −0.47 [C-026]; LptE: −0.78 [C-027]) and the WT-like parental strain (LptD: log₂(4672/4580) = −0.62 [C-028]; LptE: −0.68 [C-029]).

No component is downregulated at the transcript level [C-023]; the MsbA-like CCNA_00307 (logFC = +0.89, FDR = 0.01 [C-024]) and LptC-related CCNA_03716 (logFC = +0.56, FDR = 0.005 [C-025]) are upregulated. Despite transcript-level support for the Uchendu shared-component model [Uchendu2026], the two canonical Lpt proteins detected in the outer membrane proteome — LptD (log₂(4672/4659) = −0.47 [C-026]) and LptE (log₂(4672/4659) = −0.78 [C-027]) — decline in the rescued strain. This transcript–proteome discordance cannot be resolved by a single biological replicate. The E. coli LptB–LptC interaction provides the mechanistic context in which the shared ATPase is rate-limiting for Lpt machine assembly [Martorana2016]; under this framework, one interpretation is that canonical LptD and LptE are reduced because the LPS-specific channel (for which they are the major outer membrane component) has reduced substrate flux when LPS is absent, leading to partial protein turnover. Replicated outer membrane proteomics are required to adjudicate between this and alternative interpretations.

The third sub-claim — CtpA (CCNA_03113) upregulation as the LpxF-equivalent processing step [Zik2022] — is formally rejected at the pre-registered bar. In the 4599-vs-4584 contrast designed to isolate the Δ*lpxC*-specific response, CtpA shows logFC = +0.58, p-value = 0.048, FDR = 0.109 at the transcript level, and is absent from the outer membrane proteome [C-019]. The p-value of 0.048 fails the BORDERLINE criterion's lower bound (0.05 < p-value); the FDR of 0.109 fails the PASS criterion (FDR < 0.05); and protein non-detection means the secondary BORDERLINE condition is also unmet. A cumulative contrast comparing the rescued strain to the WT-like parental (4599-vs-4580) yields FDR = 0.035, but this contrast conflates Δ*fur*, Δ*sspB*, and Δ*lpxC* effects and was not pre-registered as a test of the LpxF-substitute hypothesis. CtpA's proposed role in lipid A head-group phosphatase activity [Zik2022] therefore remains untested by the present work and requires dedicated genetic or biochemical interrogation.

A pilot observation deserving replication: the Uchendu-identified sphingolipid inner-membrane transporter LptC2 (CCNA_01226) shows a transcript logFC of −0.60 (FDR = 0.034 [C-030]) with a protein log₂(4672/4659) = +1.08 in the single-replicate proteome [C-031]. Because LptC2 protein was already at log₂(4659/4580) = −0.42 relative to the WT-like parental [C-032], the net protein change in the rescued strain versus WT is +0.66 log₂ (approximately 1.58× [C-033]) — a more modest effect that partially reflects recovery from a prior decrease in the permissive intermediate. The transcript-protein decoupling is consistent with post-transcriptional stabilization of LptC2 protein when competition for the shared LptB ATPase is reduced by the loss of LPS cargo [Uchendu2026], but the single-replicate nature of the observation precludes statistical confirmation. The sphingolipid-biosynthesis gene CCNA_01217, required for CHIR-090 tolerance in *Caulobacter* [Zik2022], shows protein log₂(4672/4659) = +0.77 [C-034] and log₂(4672/4580) = +0.74 [C-035], also consistent with elevated sphingolipid processing capacity in the rescued state — and also requiring replicated proteomics for confirmation.

### Layer 4: Peptidoglycan remodeling involves targeted lytic-enzyme induction on a backdrop of broad basal shutdown

Of the 53 PG-remodeling gene loci locked before differential expression analysis [C-036], 28 met the H4 significance criterion (FDR < 0.05 at transcript or |log₂| > 1 in the outer membrane proteome), comprising 25 transcript-significant genes and 6 proteome-significant proteins deduplicated to 28 unique loci [C-037, C-038]. The H4 pre-registered threshold of ≥3 responding enzymes is exceeded strongly. The directional composition is predominantly downregulatory: 20 of the 28 loci are downregulated [C-039], reflecting broad shutdown of the basal division and elongation PG machinery, while 5 genes are upregulated at the transcript level and 3 at the protein level.

The Pal-like CCNA_00784 upregulation at both transcript (logFC = +2.08 [C-041]) and outer membrane protein (log₂ = +2.84 [C-041]) levels warrants particular attention. The primary function of the Tol-Pal complex has recently been re-evaluated by Tan and Chng [Tan2025], whose engineered E. coli chimeric-Tol-Pal experiments established that the complex's primary role is retrograde phospholipid transport for outer-membrane lipid homeostasis rather than structural reinforcement via LPS-anchored protein interactions. In *Caulobacter*, the Tol-Pal complex is essential for outer-membrane integrity and outer-membrane constriction at cell division [Yeh2010] — unlike its dispensable *E. coli* counterpart — meaning that Pal upregulation in the Δ*lpxc* rescued state has both a homeostatic and a cell-division-essential interpretation. The most parsimonious mechanistic reading under the Tan–Chng framework is that Pal upregulation increases retrograde phospholipid transport capacity to restore outer-membrane lipid asymmetry when LPS is absent from the outer leaflet, while simultaneously maintaining the Pal-mediated PG contact required for cell division [Yeh2010].

### Comparative genomics: the *Caulobacter* sphingolipid pathway and ChvG–ChvI are absent from the comparator species

The initial PaperBLAST survey [Price2017] suggested species-specific restriction of the *Caulobacter* sphingolipid biosynthesis pathway and ChvG–ChvI circuit, but was complicated by discovery of approximately 80% false-negative rates for known *Caulobacter* lipid A biosynthesis genes — LpxA, LpxC, LpxD, and LpxK each returned zero PaperBLAST hits for *C. crescentus* despite NCBI confirming 11, 15, 15, and 18 annotation hits, respectively [C-048, C-049, C-050, C-061]. This rate was uncovered by the NB06b cross-check and reflects limitations of PaperBLAST text matching for genes annotated in *Caulobacter* under non-canonical or species-specific gene symbols rather than E. coli-derived gene names.

NCBI Entrez annotation-based confirmatory analysis (notebook 06b) validated the headline biological claims. ## Discussion

The data presented here define the transcriptional and proteomic architecture of Δ*fur*-permitted lipid A dispensability in *C. crescentus* at the resolution of individual mechanistic sub-claims, each assigned a specific evidence grade. The picture that emerges is one in which constitutive CPG sphingolipid substitution provides the structural substrate, ChvG–ChvI regulatory signaling is specifically co-opted through two temporal phases to coordinate the cellular response to LPS loss, and PG reorganization accommodates the mechanical consequences of outer-membrane remodeling — all in a species in which both the sphingolipid pathway and the ChvG–ChvI circuit are uniquely present.

The Δ*fur* derepression arm (Path A) is the most robustly supported layer. The Spearman concordance with Leaden 2018 [Leaden2018] and the characteristic derepression of TBDTs cataloged by da Silva Neto et al. [Neto2009] and Hernandez-Ortiz et al. [HernandezOrtiz2025] confirm that the 4584-vs-4580 transcriptome authentically represents the Fur-derepression state. The marginal fitness enrichment of the concordant-strong subset (fold = 1.60×, p = 0.016 [C-005]) is a genuine enrichment, not a threshold-crossing artifact; the recalibrated hypergeometric test against the 33.25% genome background [C-004] provides the appropriate reference.

In contrast, the ΔsspB-buffered respiratory cohort (Path B) does not achieve fitness enrichment above background (fold = 1.04×, p = 0.515 [C-006]). The biologically reasonable proposition that respiratory ATP production specifically sustains envelope-remodeling work is not falsified — it remains a plausible mechanistic model — but the RB-TnSeq data do not provide independent selective evidence for it. Earlier narrative framings treating the dual-release switch (Δ*fur* arm + Δ*sspB* respiratory arm) as symmetrically established must be corrected: the Δ*fur* arm is supported by both concordance analysis and marginal fitness enrichment, while the Δ*sspB* arm rests on transcript-level buffering without fitness corroboration.

The ChvG–ChvI two-phase engagement is the second well-supported layer. The disjoint partition of 20 unique-to-early, 10 both-phase, and 49 late-consequence genes [C-010] demonstrates that ChvI does not respond as a monolithic stress regulon but temporally discriminates the Δ*fur*/Δ*sspB* perturbation from the added structural challenge of LPS removal. ChvI autoregulation in the both-phase cohort (logFC = +1.45 [C-014]) is consistent with the positive-feedback architecture of the alphaproteobacterial circuit [Greenwich2023], in which activated ChvI sustains its own expression via ChvG-phosphorylated ChvI promoting further ChvI transcription. The convergence of LolA-family CCNA_03820 induction in both the late ChvI cohort and the Uchendu et al. Δ*spt* dataset [Uchendu2026] is particularly notable: a single gene induced when either major outer-membrane lipid class (LPS or sphingolipid) is removed implies that LolA upregulation is a generic outer-membrane stress response rather than a LPS-specific compensatory mechanism. The SigU-as-driver hypothesis for the late cohort fails the pre-registered functional coherence threshold (24.5% [C-018]) primarily because *Caulobacter* SigU is uncharacterized in the published literature — the test cannot be validated against an external gold standard and is therefore partial rather than falsified.

The constitutive sphingolipid biosynthesis finding (Layer 3) rules out a model in which Δ*fur* enables lipid A dispensability by derepressing CPG biosynthesis. Zero biosynthesis genes are induced; *spt* and *sphk* are mildly downregulated [C-020, C-021, C-022]. The constitutive activity of the CPG pathway enzymes CpgB [Dhakephalkar2023] and CpgD [Dhakephalkar2025] under non-inducing conditions is consistent with a flux-driven model in which the existing CPG pool is available for outer-membrane substitution without transcriptional upregulation. The mild downregulation may reflect feedback suppression once the outer membrane is populated with sphingolipid under reduced LPS competition for the shared transport channel [Uchendu2026].

The rejection of CtpA as the LpxF-substitute processing step represents an important clarification. CtpA's failure at the pre-registered bar [C-019] does not falsify the enzymatic hypothesis proposed by Zik et al. [Zik2022] — that CtpA performs lipid-A head-group phosphatase activity equivalent to LpxF in organisms such as *Rhizobium* — but it establishes that the Δ*lpxC*-specific induction of CtpA at both transcript and protein levels is not detectable by the present transcriptome-proteome contrast. Earlier draft reports of a BORDERLINE verdict for CtpA were in error: the p-value of 0.048 falls below, not within, the BORDERLINE range of 0.05 < p-value < 0.15, and the cumulative contrast FDR of 0.035 is not the pre-registered test. Future work should test CtpA by constructing a Δ*ctpA* Δ*lpxC* Δ*fur* Δ*sspB* quadruple deletion to determine whether CtpA is required for the rescue, and should pursue in vitro demonstration of head-group phosphatase activity.

The Lpt apparatus transcript–proteome discordance poses an unresolved interpretive challenge. The transcript upregulation of MsbA-like and LptC-related components is consistent with the Uchendu shared-component model [Uchendu2026]; the protein-level decline of canonical LptD and LptE [C-026, C-027] is not straightforwardly consistent with it. The most parsimonious reconciliation is substrate-limitation: canonical LptD and LptE are the outer-membrane docking and extraction components specifically optimized for LPS as a substrate; in its absence, the canonical Lpt channel has reduced throughput and is partially turned over while the sphingolipid-specific LptC2/LptF2/LptG2 paralogs fill the vacancy [Uchendu2026]. The pilot lptC2 protein elevation (+1.08 log₂ vs. the permissive intermediate [C-031]) supports but does not establish this scenario. Replicated outer membrane proteomics with statistical rigor are scheduled for summer 2026.

The Pal-Tol upregulation provides perhaps the most mechanistically interpretable PG finding, particularly in the light of the Tan–Chng 2025 redefinition of Tol-Pal primary function [Tan2025]. The classic attribution of Pal's role to LPS-anchored structural reinforcement of the outer membrane is not supported by the current mechanistic literature; the primary function is retrograde phospholipid transport for outer-membrane lipid homeostasis [Tan2025]. Under the revised framework, Pal upregulation in the Δ*lpxc* state is expected rather than paradoxical: removal of LPS from the outer leaflet disrupts outer-membrane lipid asymmetry, and upregulation of the Tol-Pal complex increases retrograde phospholipid transport capacity to compensate. Because Caulobacter Tol-Pal is essential for outer-membrane constriction at cell division [Yeh2010] — a function that must be maintained even without LPS — Pal upregulation is mechanistically necessary, not merely compensatory. Pal is induced as both a member of the late ChvI consequence cohort (Layer 2) and a PG-remodeling effector (Layer 4) — convergent evidence from two independent analytic frameworks that is consistent with, but does not establish, ChvI regulation of Pal in this state.

The species-specificity data confirm that the *Caulobacter* strategy is not transferable to any of the three comparator Gram-negative species with known or candidate LPS dispensability. The NCBI-confirmed pattern-1000 absences of *spt*, *cerR*, ChvG, and ChvI from *A. baumannii*, *N. meningitidis*, and *M. catarrhalis* [C-053, C-054, C-055, C-056] establish a genomic basis for the species specificity. The clinical-microbiology interest in understanding and potentially reversing LPS-null *A. baumannii* colistin resistance [Moffatt2010, Moffatt2011, Kang2021] therefore cannot draw direct mechanistic lessons from the *Caulobacter* sphingolipid + Fur strategy, except at the high level of principle that envelope-remodeling accompanies the outer-membrane transition. The mechanistic diversity of LPS-dispensability routes [Hummels2024] reflects deep structural differences among Gram-negative OM repertoires that determine which compensatory strategies are genomically available in each lineage.

### Limitations

The outer membrane proteome is a single biological replicate per strain. No per-protein statistics are derivable, and all protein-level claims — including the LptC2 pilot induction, the LptD/LptE protein-level decline, the SdpA and Pal upregulations, and the CCNA_01217 protein elevation — represent directional observations awaiting confirmation from replicated proteomics. The entire transcriptome was collected under a single growth condition (PYE rich medium), making the Fur-derepression signal constitutive rather than iron-limitation-responsive; because the *Caulobacter* fitness compendium contains no iron-limitation experiments [C-065], the iron-axis arm of H2 was formally descoped. The pre-registered H2 fitness threshold of ≥10% phenotype-bearing was found to fall below the 33.25% genome background [C-004], a calibration error that has been corrected by adopting hypergeometric enrichment as the authoritative H2 verdict; this calibration lesson should inform future pre-registrations in this research program. The SigU cohort attribution is partial because *Caulobacter* SigU is uncharacterized in the published literature, precluding external validation of any coherence criterion. PaperBLAST shows approximately 80% false-negative rates for *Caulobacter* lipid A biosynthesis genes [C-061]; for the comparative claims, however, the headline argument concerns absence in the comparator species, which NCBI annotation independently confirms.

## Conclusions

This study characterizes the transcriptional and proteomic architecture of Δ*fur*-permitted lipid A dispensability in *C. crescentus* at the resolution of individual mechanistic sub-claims, each assigned an honest evidence grade. Three layers are well-supported: constitutive CPG sphingolipid substitution provides the structural substrate for outer-membrane rescue; the ChvG–ChvI two-component system coordinates a two-phase regulatory response distinguishing the Δ*fur*/Δ*sspB* perturbation (early cooperator phase, 20 genes) from LPS-loss consequences (late phase, 49 genes); and PG remodeling — dominated by basal machinery shutdown with targeted induction of lytic transglycosylases and the Tol-Pal complex — mechanically accommodates the outer-membrane transition. A fourth layer, Fur-released TBDT fitness enrichment, is marginally supported. Two sub-claims are formally rejected: the ΔsspB-buffered respiratory arm shows no enrichment above the genome background, and CtpA does not meet the pre-registered transcript and proteome criteria for the LpxF-substitute role. The Lpt apparatus shows transcript–proteome discordance that replicated proteomics will resolve; an lptC2 protein-level pilot observation is suggestive but single-replicate. Comparative genomics confirms that the sphingolipid pathway and ChvG–ChvI circuit are absent from *A. baumannii*, *N. meningitidis*, and *M. catarrhalis*, establishing the genomic basis for the species-specificity of the *Caulobacter* rescue. Together, these findings describe a species-specific multi-layer envelope-remodeling strategy and provide a foundation for understanding how an alphaproteobacterium navigates the complete removal of its major surface-exposed lipid component.
