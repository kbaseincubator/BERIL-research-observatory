# OBI Ontology Coverage and Utilization in BERDL

## Research Question

To what extent is the Ontology for Biomedical Investigations (OBI) loaded into the BERDL ontology store, and how consistently are OBI terms used across NMDC and other DOE/LBL datasets in the lakehouse?

## Status

In Progress — initial data exploration complete, research plan created.

## Overview

OBI is a widely-used ontology for describing biomedical and environmental investigations — instruments, assay types, sample collection devices, and experimental protocols. This project assesses OBI's presence and utilization in BERDL by: (1) characterizing the OBI content in `kbase_ontology_source`, (2) surveying how OBI terms are referenced across NMDC biosamples and other collections, and (3) identifying inconsistencies and missed annotation opportunities.

This is an exploratory, cross-cutting investigation. The same question is being pursued in parallel using other systems. The goal is to determine whether BERDL has useful signal for understanding OBI adoption in DOE/LBL data resources.
