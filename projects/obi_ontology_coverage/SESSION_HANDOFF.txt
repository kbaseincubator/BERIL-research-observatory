WHAT MARK ASKED FOR
--------------------
Mark wants to investigate whether the OBI (Ontology for Biomedical
Investigations) is being well-utilized by NMDC and other LBL/DOE
projects. He suspects OBI may not even be fully loaded into the BERDL
data lakehouse. He acknowledged this may not be a natural fit for this
repo or lakehouse — the same investigation is being pursued in parallel
on other systems. The goal here is a lightweight exploration to see
whether BERDL has useful signal.

This is an unusual project for this repo. Most projects here do
pangenome analysis, fitness browser queries, or metabolic ecology.
This one is an ontology adoption audit.

WHAT WE FOUND SO FAR (exploratory queries, not yet in notebook outputs)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. OBI IS fully loaded in kbase_ontology_source.statements:
   - 4,422 OBI terms with labels, from obi-base.owl
   - 6,029 subClassOf triples (full hierarchy)
   - 2.8M entailed edges in the transitive closure table
   - This contradicts an earlier hypothesis that OBI was only
     present as cross-references from other ontologies.

2. OBI is used in NMDC biosamples, but sporadically:
   - sample collection device → OBI:0003004 (3,018 samples)
   - sequencing method → OBI_0002003 (2,901 samples)
   - collection_device → Virus Transport Medium [OBI:0002866] (2,732)
   - samp_source_mat_cat → environmental swab specimen [OBI:0002613] (1,148)
   - Formatting is inconsistent: OBI:, OBI_, OBI: (with space)

3. OBI is completely absent from env_triads_flattened, the curated
   environment annotation table. That table uses ENVO, UBERON,
   NCBITaxon, FOODON, and PO exclusively.

4. The ontology store contains ~7.2M distinct subjects across many
   ontologies. OBI (4,422 terms) is mid-sized — smaller than
   NCBITaxon (2.6M), CHEBI (223K), GO (52K), but comparable to
   ENVO (4,514).

CORRECTED FINDING (2026-04-03 late session)
--------------------------------------------
Initial report said OBI had "zero computational workflow terms." WRONG.
OBI has:
- OBI:0001872 "sequence assembly process" (no children)
- OBI:0001944 "sequence annotation" (no children)
- OBI:0200187 "sequence analysis data transformation" (2 children)
- OBI:0002587 "machine learning" (2 children)
- Plus assembly/annotation algorithm and metadata terms

The real gaps are: read QC, genome binning (MAGs), taxonomic
classification, and expression quantification. Sequence assembly
and annotation exist but are generic — no metagenome-specific subclasses.

See OBI_NMDC_WORKFLOW_MAPPING.md for the full corrected mapping.

NEW WORK FROM NUC SESSION 2026-04-03 (Claude Opus 4.6)
------------------------------------------------------
Major additions pushed to the branch. The BERDL agent should be aware of:

### 1. Isolate Entities Schema (data/entities-for-isolates.yaml)
A LinkML schema with 18 classes derived from Alicia Clum's whiteboard at
the 2026-04-01 isolate hackathon. Validated. Has 120 OBI/OBO ontology
mappings from OLS4 embeddings search. Key classes: Organism, Strain,
Taxon, Cell, Community, Colony, Population, Tissue, Plasmid, Genotype,
Phenotype, Sample, TreatedSample, Analyte, InstrumentPrepProcessing,
ExperimentalTreatment, Site, Command.

This schema separates Organism from Sample — which is the opposite of
how nmdc-schema models it (OrganismSample conflates both). The mapping
analysis (isolate-schema-nmdc-mapping-analysis.md) documents the
disagreement across three competing models.

### 2. OBI+CHEBI+PATO Phenotype Decomposition
The Phenotype class now has structured slots: quality_term (PATO),
assayed_entity_term (CHEBI/GO), assay_term (OBI). This is the pattern
described in KG_MICROBE_METPO_QUESTIONS.md.

BERDL ACTION: Test this decomposition against real BacDive data.
Query kescience_bacdive.metabolite_utilization and try to map 10 rows
to the Phenotype class slots. Does the pattern hold?

### 3. P. putida KT2440 pTE314 Example
data/example-pseudomonas-putida-kt2440-pte314.yaml — 15 instances
across 10 classes showing an AMP2/OPAL workflow: receive engineered
strain from LBNL, culture, induce with IPTG, extract protein for
proteomics. Demonstrates why Organism + Strain + Plasmid need to be
separate entities for combinatorial engineered biology.

### 4. OpenScientist Autonomous Research (openscientist_results/)
10-iteration autonomous investigation completed. Key quantitative
findings: 9 new mappings → 52% coverage, 6 NTRs → 81%. MAG quality
assessment is the deepest cross-ontology gap. 12 figures included.
The final_report.md has the full writeup.

### 5. Bridge Document (ISOLATE_SCHEMA_OBI_BRIDGE.md)
Connects the isolate schema to three OBI coverage workstreams:
process gaps (ExperimentalTreatment has no OBI home), trait modeling
(BacDive free text vs structured entities), and the organism-vs-sample
question for the OBI April 7 presentation.
