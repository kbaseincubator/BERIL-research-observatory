# References

## Primary Data Sources

- Price MN et al. (2018). "Mutant phenotypes for thousands of bacterial genes of unknown function."
  *Nature* 557, 503–509. DOI: 10.1038/s41586-018-0124-0. [FitnessBrowser RB-TnSeq data source]

- Wetmore KM et al. (2015). "Rapid Quantification of Mutant Fitness in Diverse Bacteria by
  Sequencing Randomly Bar-Coded Transposons." *mBio* 6(3): e00306-15. PMID: 26173555.
  [RB-TnSeq methodology]

## Essential Gene Prediction

- Ning LW et al. (2014). "Predicting bacterial essential genes using only sequence composition
  information." *Genet Mol Res* 13(3): 4564–4572. PMID: 25036505.
  [SVM with composition features; AUC 0.74–0.82 within-organism, 0.76 cross-genome]

- Deng J and Lu LJ (2011). "Investigating the predictability of essential genes across distantly
  related organisms using an integrative approach." *Nucleic Acids Res* 39(18): e118.
  PMID: 20870748. [Cross-organism prediction AUC 0.69–0.89; evolutionary distance effect]

- Deng J (2015). "An integrated machine-learning model to predict prokaryotic essential genes."
  *Methods Mol Biol* 1278: 153–167. PMID: 25636617. [Ensemble classifier; AUC 0.86–0.93 CV]

- Das MM and Sarkar K (2022). "Evaluation of machine learning classifiers for predicting
  essential genes in E. coli." *Bioinformation* 18(11): 1126. PMID: 37701504.

- Yu Y et al. (2017). "Gene essentiality prediction based on fractal features and machine
  learning." *Mol Biosyst* 13(3): 577–585. PMID: 28145541.
