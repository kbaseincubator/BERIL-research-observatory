# References: M. extorquens Pangenome Case Study

## Lanthanide-Dependent Methylotrophy

- **Pol A, Barends TRM, Liebers MF, Tschemernjak D, Luesken F, Schmeisser K, Op den Camp HJM, Jetten MSM.** (2014). Rare earth metals are essential for methanotrophic life in volcanic mudpots. *Environ Microbiol*, 16(1), 255-264. — First demonstration of an essential biological role for lanthanides, showing XoxF requires rare earth elements for activity.

- **Keltjens JT, Pol A, Reimann J, Op den Camp HJM.** (2014). PQQ-dependent methanol dehydrogenases: rare-earth elements make a difference. *Appl Microbiol Biotechnol*, 98(14), 6163-6183. — Review showing XoxF (lanthanide-dependent MDH) is more widely distributed across methylotrophs than MxaF (calcium-dependent MDH).

- **Cotruvo JA Jr.** (2019). The chemistry of lanthanides in biology: recent discoveries, emerging principles, and technological applications. *ACS Central Science*, 5(9), 1496-1506. — Comprehensive review of lanthanide biology; notes the first biological role was demonstrated in 2011 with XoxF characterization.

- **Masuda S, Suzuki Y, Fujitani Y, Mitsui R, Nakagawa T, Shintani M, Tani A.** (2016). Lanthanide-dependent regulation of methanol oxidation systems in *Methylobacterium extorquens* AM1 and their contribution to methanol growth. *J Bacteriol*, 198(8), 1250-1260. — Demonstrated the reciprocal regulation of mxaF and xoxF in M. extorquens AM1 depending on lanthanide availability.

- **Good NM, Vu HN, Surber CJ, Subuyuj GA, Skovran E, Martinez-Gomez NC.** (2016). Pyrroloquinoline quinone ethanol dehydrogenase in *Methylobacterium extorquens* AM1 extends lanthanide-dependent metabolism to multicarbon substrates. *J Bacteriol*, 198(22), 3109-3118. — Identified ExaF, extending lanthanide-dependent metabolism beyond methanol in M. extorquens.

- **Nakagawa T, Mitsui R, Tani A, Sasa K, Tashiro S, Iwama T, Hayakawa T, Kawai K.** (2012). A catalytic role of XoxF1 as La3+-dependent methanol dehydrogenase in *Methylobacterium extorquens* strain AM1. *PLoS One*, 7(11), e50480. — Confirmed XoxF1 functions as a La3+-dependent MDH; showed xoxF genes are more widespread than mxaFI.

## Methylobacterium Genomics and Taxonomy

- **Vuilleumier S, Chistoserdova L, Lee M-C, Bringel F, Lajus A, Zhou Y, et al.** (2009). *Methylobacterium* genome sequences: a reference blueprint to investigate microbial metabolism of C1 compounds from natural and industrial sources. *PLoS One*, 4(5), e5584. — Foundational comparative genomics of M. extorquens AM1 and DM4.

- **Green PN, Ardley JK.** (2018). Review of the genus *Methylobacterium* and closely related organisms: a proposal that some *Methylobacterium* species be reclassified into a new genus, *Methylorubrum* gen. nov. *Int J Syst Evol Microbiol*, 68(9), 2727-2748. — Proposed splitting Methylobacterium based on 16S rRNA phylogeny; GTDB does not adopt this split.

- **Hesse C, Schweer J, Trögl J, Kröber E, Antonielli L, Hedrich S, linger S, by With M, Graham DE.** (2022). Comprehensive phylogenomics of *Methylobacterium* reveals four evolutionary distinct groups and underappreciated phyllosphere diversity. *Genome Biol Evol*, 14(8), evac123. — Analyzed 189 genomes; identified four groups and 45 candidate species. Supports monophyly, contradicting the Methylorubrum split.

- **Alessa O, Ogura Y, Fujitani Y, Takami H, Hayashi T, Mitsui R, Nakagawa T, Tani A.** (2021). Comprehensive comparative genomics and phenotyping of *Methylobacterium* species. *Front Microbiol*, 12, 740610. — Comparative genomics and phenotyping across the genus.

- **Marx CJ, et al.** (2012). Complete genome sequences of six strains of the genus *Methylobacterium*. *J Bacteriol*, 194(17), 4746-4748.

## B Vitamin Auxotrophy

- **Ryback B, Bortfeld-Miller M, Vorholt JA.** (2022). Metabolic adaptation to vitamin auxotrophy by leaf-associated bacteria. *ISME J*, 16(12), 2712-2724. — Up to half of phyllosphere bacteria are auxotrophic for biotin, niacin, pantothenate, and/or thiamine; directly relevant since Methylobacterium is a dominant phyllosphere genus.

- **Morris JJ, Lenski RE, Zinser ER.** (2012). The Black Queen Hypothesis: evolution of dependencies through adaptive gene loss. *mBio*, 3(2), e00036-12. — Framework explaining how free-living bacteria can adaptively lose biosynthetic genes when products are available from community members; may explain Methylobacterium's incomplete B1/B2 pathways.

- **Magnusdottir S, Ravcheev D, de Crecy-Lagard V, Thiele I.** (2015). Systematic genome assessment of B-vitamin biosynthesis suggests co-operation among gut microbes. *Front Genet*, 6, 148. — Genome-based B vitamin pathway completeness assessment in 256 bacteria; methodology parallels our pangenome approach.

## Annotation Quality

- **Schnoes AM, Brown SD, Dodevski I, Babbitt PC.** (2009). Annotation error in public databases: misannotation of molecular function in enzyme superfamilies. *PLoS Comput Biol*, 5(12), e1000605. — Found misannotation rates of 5-63% in GenBank, TrEMBL, and KEGG across 37 enzyme families; Swiss-Prot had lowest errors (~0%).

- **Danchin A, Ouzounis C.** (2018). No wisdom in the crowd: genome annotation in the era of big data -- current status and future prospects. *Microb Biotechnol*, 11(4), 588-605. — Reviews error propagation in automated genome annotation.

- **Promponas VJ, Iliopoulos I, Ouzounis CA.** (2015). Annotation inconsistencies beyond sequence similarity-based function prediction -- phylogeny and genome structure. *Stand Genomic Sci*, 10, 108. — Extends misannotation analysis beyond sequence similarity to phylogenetic and genome-structural artifacts.
