OBJECTIVE
---------
Evaluate the downstream impact of replacing Marvin 23.4 (ChemAxon) pKa
predictions with OPAM2 (graph-convolutional neural network) pKa predictions
across the ModelSEED thermodynamic pipeline:

  OPAM2 pKa → ModelSEEDDatabase → dGPredictor Legendre transform → reaction
  deltaG → metabolic model FBA/FVA constraints → growth phenotype predictions

NB06 - Blocked Reactions Analysis:
  - Applied thermodynamic constraints with |deltaG| > 5 kJ/mol threshold
  - Ran FVA (fraction_of_optimum=0.0) under three conditions:
    unconstrained, Marvin-constrained, OPAM2-constrained
  - Results:
    * E. coli: 1,732 blocked (Marvin) vs 1,731 blocked (OPAM2)
      - 2 newly blocked, 3 newly unblocked = 5 total changes
    * B. subtilis: 1,337 blocked (both) - no changes
  - The 5 changed E. coli reactions:
    * rxn01545 (forward→blocked): dG shift 3.8 → -159.8 kJ/mol
    * rxn01649 (reverse→blocked): dG shift 11.6 → -152.4 kJ/mol
    * rxn10052, rxn00097, rxn00104 (blocked→variable): constraints relaxed
  - Output: data/blocked_reactions_diff.tsv, data/fva_summary.tsv

NB07 - Directionality Changes & Loop Detection:
  - Directionality comparison: 5 direction changes in E. coli, 0 in B. subtilis
    (same 5 reactions from NB06)
  - Loop detection via pFBA: 0 loop reactions detected under either Marvin
    or OPAM2 constraints (both models were infeasible under thermo constraints,
    so no standard FBA flux to compare)
  - Output: data/directionality_diff.tsv

NB08 - Phenotype Impact:
  - Simulated growth on 12 carbon sources per organism (minimal media + single
    carbon source, growth > 1e-6 threshold)
  - Three conditions: unconstrained, Marvin-constrained, OPAM2-constrained
  - Results:
    * E. coli unconstrained: 83.3% accuracy (8 CP, 2 CN, 1 FP, 1 FN)
    * E. coli Marvin/OPAM2: 25.0% accuracy (0 CP, 3 CN, 0 FP, 9 FN)
    * B. subtilis unconstrained: 75.0% (7 CP, 2 CN, 1 FP, 2 FN)
    * B. subtilis Marvin/OPAM2: 25.0% (0 CP, 3 CN, 0 FP, 9 FN)
  - Both thermodynamic constraint sets eliminate growth entirely, producing
    identical false-negative patterns
  - No phenotype predictions changed between Marvin and OPAM2
  - Output: data/phenotype_results.tsv, data/phenotype_accuracy.tsv

KEY SCIENTIFIC FINDINGS
-----------------------
1. pKa Differences Are Widespread: OPAM2 diverges from Marvin across thousands
   of compounds, especially for multi-protic molecules and complex electronic
   environments.

2. deltaG Changes Propagate Through pH Correction:
   - 31,924 reactions recomputed
   - 72.9% change by < 1 kJ/mol
   - 17.6% change by 1-5 kJ/mol
   - 7.5% change by > 5 kJ/mol
   - 311 reactions (1.0%) have extreme changes > 100 kJ/mol
   - 840 reactions (2.6%) reverse deltaG sign

3. Model-Level Impact Is Minimal: Only 5 of 1,765 E. coli reaction
   classifications changed in FVA. Zero changes in B. subtilis.

4. Growth Phenotype Predictions Unchanged: Both Marvin and OPAM2 thermodynamic
   constraints produce identical phenotype predictions across all 24 conditions.

5. Explanation: Most changes are small, large changes affect peripheral
   reactions, and model structure (network connectivity) dominates over
   thermodynamic constraints.
