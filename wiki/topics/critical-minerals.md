# Critical Minerals and Metal Biology

## One-Line Takeaway

The observatory has moved from cataloging metal fitness effects to identifying conserved, partially validated genetic architectures that can drive critical-mineral bioprocessing, field validation, and missing-data experiments.

## Why This Topic Exists

Metal tolerance is one of the clearest places where BERIL-scale data has a DOE-relevant arc: fitness screens identify genes, pangenomes show conservation, specificity analyses separate metal biology from general stress, BacDive and ENIGMA provide environmental validation, and missing rare-earth data points to the next experiment.

## What We Have Learned

### Layer 1 - Metal Fitness Architecture

`metal_fitness_atlas` identified broad gene-metal fitness signals. The surprising systems-level lesson is that metal tolerance is strongly core-genome mediated, not just accessory resistance islands.

### Layer 2 - Specificity Versus General Stress

`metal_specificity` showed that more than half of analyzable metal-important records are metal-specific under a strict non-metal sick-rate screen. Specific genes are still core-enriched, but less than general sick genes.

### Layer 3 - Counter-Ions And Experimental Context

`counter_ion_effects` keeps the field honest: some metal assays partly measure osmotic or counter-ion stress. Agents should preserve this caveat whenever proposing new metal-tolerance claims.

### Layer 4 - Environmental Validation

`bacdive_metal_validation`, `bacdive_phenotype_metal_tolerance`, and `enigma_sso_asv_ecology` connect laboratory fitness or tolerance scores to isolation environments, phenotype records, and contaminated-site community patterns.

### Layer 5 - Critical-Mineral Research Directions

The valuable next layer is not another static atlas. It is ranked gene targets, cross-metal inference for unmeasured elements, field-site validation, and engineered community design.

## Open Caveats

- Rare-earth fitness data appears absent, so REE claims must be framed as predictions.
- Locus ID attrition in `metal_specificity` means some model organisms remain under-covered.
- Counter-ion stress can inflate or redirect apparent metal signals.

## How Agents Should Use This Page

Use this topic as the entry point for proposals about bioleaching, biorecovery, metal contamination, metal-AMR co-selection, or metal-tolerant community design. Any new proposal should cite at least one metal fitness source and one validation or caveat source.
