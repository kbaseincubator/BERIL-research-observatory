# AMR, Resistance Ecology, and Co-selection

## One-Line Takeaway

AMR is not just a clinical gene list in this observatory; it is an ecological, pangenomic, and fitness-linked system whose distribution changes with environment, genome background, and metal exposure.

## What We Have Learned

### Layer 1 - Pangenome Distribution

`amr_pangenome_atlas` and `amr_strain_variation` frame AMR genes as core, accessory, and strain-variable genome features rather than flat annotations.

### Layer 2 - Environment Structure

`amr_environmental_resistome` reports strong environmental structure in resistance mechanism composition. Soil and aquatic contexts are particularly important for metal-resistance mechanisms.

### Layer 3 - Fitness And Support Networks

`amr_fitness_cost` and `amr_cofitness_networks` connect AMR to measurable costs and support neighborhoods. This is the difference between "gene is present" and "gene matters in this biological context."

### Layer 4 - Co-selection Opportunity

The overlap between metal biology and AMR ecology is a high-value research seam. Contaminated sites may select metal tolerance and AMR together, but this needs explicit hypothesis tests rather than assumption.

## High-Value Directions

- [Metal-AMR co-selection at contaminated DOE sites](/atlas/directions/metal-amr-co-selection)
- Build a resistance-support module catalog from cofitness neighborhoods.

## Open Caveats

- AMR annotations can mix mechanistic resistance, stress response, transport, and mobile-element context.
- Environment metadata remains uneven across genomes.
- Co-selection is plausible but must be tested against nulls and confounders.
