# People, Expertise, and Review Routing

## Why This Section Exists

The people section is not a social directory. It helps humans and agents route questions, reviews, and derived-product ownership.

## Current Atlas Use

- Identify likely reviewers by topic and source projects.
- Preserve ownership of derived data products.
- Make contributor context visible when an Atlas page needs human review.
- Route unresolved tensions to people connected to the affected source projects.
- Highlight reusable products that lack a producer, consumer, or review route.

## Review Routing Rules

Atlas review routes are derived from explicit repository context: page source projects, derived-product producer projects, and declared review routes. The Atlas should show why a route was suggested, not just who appears in the list.

## What Needs Human Review

- Low-confidence pages that support reused claims or products.
- Derived products with downstream consumers.
- Unresolved tension pages that affect active topics or directions.
- Products with no declared owner or stale review date.

## Boundary

The Atlas keeps this lightweight. It should not infer sensitive personal details or expertise beyond project authorship and explicit repository context.
